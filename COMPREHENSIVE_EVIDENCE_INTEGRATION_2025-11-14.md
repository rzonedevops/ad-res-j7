### Key Legal Position

**Daniel's Argument:** The interdict is **void ab initio** (void from the beginning) due to perjury, not just voidable.

**Legal Basis:** Rule 42(1)(a) - Rescission for fraud (mandatory relief)

**Strategy:** Do NOT respond to Part B of interdict - responding validates the criminally fraudulent document

**Correct Approach:**
1. Prove perjury (not dispute claims)
2. Show criminal fraud (not civil disagreement)
3. Establish void status (not defend disputes)

---

### Critical Timeline

**June 10, 2025:** Daniel reported fraud to Bantjies/Peter

**August 14, 2025:** Peter filed interdict with perjured affidavits

**Material Concealment:** Murder, theft, fraud

**Legal Principle:** When founding documents are perjured, the order is void ab initio - it never legally existed.

---

### Why This Strategy is Correct

**1. Void ab initio vs. Voidable:**

**Voidable:** Order exists but can be set aside  
**Void ab initio:** Order never legally existed

**Significance:** Void ab initio is much stronger - no need to "set aside" something that never existed

---

**2. Responding to Part B Validates the Crime:**

If you respond to Part B (delinquency/curatorship allegations), you:
- Acknowledge the interdict has legal effect (it doesn't)
- Validate criminal conduct (perjury)
- Waive strongest defense (void ab initio)
- Engage with disputes in a void document

**Correct Strategy:** Attack the legitimacy, prove the perjury, refuse engagement

---

**3. Criminal Fraud, Not Civil Dispute:**

Peter's interdict is not a civil dispute - it's criminal fraud:
- Provably false statements (perjury)
- Material concealment (murder, theft, fraud)
- Retaliatory abuse (filed after Daniel's fraud report)

**Correct Forum:** Commercial crimes court, not civil litigation

---

**4. Rule 42(1)(a) - Mandatory Relief:**

When fraud is proven, rescission is **mandatory**, not discretionary

Court has no choice but to set aside the order

This is why proving perjury is the strongest defense

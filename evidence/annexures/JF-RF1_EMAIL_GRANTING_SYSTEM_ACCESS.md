#### E1: Breach of Fiduciary Duties

- **Peter's Unilateral Action:** No board resolution or director consultation
- **Rynette's Status:** Non-director employee with director-level access
- **Governance Breakdown:** Replacement of collaborative model with unilateral control

#### E2: Control Transfer Evidence

**Systematic Control Mechanism:**
- **Access = Control:** Rynette can view, modify, and control all business systems
- **Concurrent Director Exclusion:** As Rynette gained access, directors lost access
- **Peter's Strategy:** Control business through Rynette while excluding directors
- **Interdict Connection:** Seeks to formalize exclusion already effected through access control

#### E3: Business Risk and Exposure

**Operational Risks Created:**
- **Single Point of Failure:** Non-director with comprehensive access
- **No Oversight:** Rynette operates without director supervision
- **Fraud Risk:** Unprecedented access for non-director employee
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Potential violations of corporate governance standards

**Supports Core Defense Arguments:**
- **Not Directors' Fault:** Breakdown caused by Peter's actions
- **Systematic Pattern:** Progressive exclusion through access control
- **Interdict Context:** Formalization of already-effected exclusion

## Legal Significance

### Case Context
This annexure is critical to refuting Peter's allegation that Daniel's computer expenses were "unexplained" or "unjustified." The evidence demonstrates:

1. **Business Necessity:** All IT services were essential for business operations
2. **Systematic Obstruction:** Peter cancelled business cards, then demanded invoices he knew Daniel couldn't access
3. **Documented Compliance:** Daniel provided available documentation despite system restrictions
4. **Manufactured Crisis:** Peter's actions created the "documentation problem" he now complains about

### Strategic Importance
- Directly counters Peter's allegations in the ex parte application
- Demonstrates Peter's bad faith in restricting access to documentation systems
- Proves expenses were legitimate business costs, not personal expenditures
- Shows pattern of entrapment: restrict access, demand proof, then claim lack of evidence

## Section 8: IT Expenses Analysis (AD 8.1 - 8.10)

| AD Paragraph | AD Content (Summary) | DR (Daniel's Response) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **8.1** | Annexure "PF9" contains IT expenses approved/paid by me. | The contents of the annexure are noted. The Applicant fails to mention that the majority of these expenses, particularly the significant Shopify platform costs, are paid for by my UK-based company, RegimA Zone Ltd, and not by the South African entities. |
| **8.2** | Majority of expenses are unexplained, with no invoices, creating tax problems. | This is denied. The expenses are fully documented with invoices. The Applicant's claim that he has no invoices is a direct result of his own actions. By cancelling my access and launching this application, he has prevented me from providing the very documentation he claims is missing. This is a manufactured crisis. |
| **8.3** | Annexure "PF10" contains computer costs from March to June 2025. | The contents of the annexure are noted. These costs represent the operational backbone of a global e-commerce enterprise. |
| **8.4** | 2024 IT expenses amount to R6,738,007.47. | This figure is presented without context. These are not my personal expenses, but the costs of running a multi-million rand international business. Furthermore, a significant portion of these costs were borne by my UK company, RegimA Zone Ltd, which owns and pays for the Shopify platforms used by the entire group. The Applicant is conflating corporate expenses with personal liability. |
| **8.5** | 2025 IT expenses (YTD) amount to R2,116,159.47. | As above. These are legitimate business expenses, largely funded by my own UK company, which the Applicant is now attempting to use against me. The Applicant is complaining about expenses that he does not even pay for. |
| **8.6** | These amounts are almost entirely unexplainable. | This is emphatically denied. Every line item is explainable and corresponds to a legitimate business service. The Applicant's feigned ignorance is a litigation tactic. I have detailed records and invoices for all these expenses. |
| **8.7** | Many of the expenses are seemingly international. | This is admitted. We operate a global business that sells products in multiple currencies and jurisdictions. The IT infrastructure to support this is necessarily international. The Applicant, who has benefited from this global business for years, cannot now claim to be surprised by international expenses. |
| **8.8** | Bank message confirms yearly allowable quota for funds leaving SA is almost exceeded. | The contents are noted. If the forex quota is under pressure, it is because the South African entities, particularly RWW, are being forced to carry the costs for the entire group, including for entities that generate their own revenue but do not pay their own way, such as the RSA Shopify store operated by DERM. This is a classic example of the expense dumping I have sought to expose. |
| **8.9** | This is inexplicable and will cause serious tax implications. | This is denied. The situation is entirely explicable: the Applicant's corporate structure is designed to dump expenses onto one entity (RWW) while profits are extracted elsewhere. Any tax implications arise from this flawed and potentially fraudulent structure, which I was attempting to rectify. |
| **8.10** | The corporations will be unable to explain these international expenses to SARS and the Reserve Bank. | This is denied. I am perfectly capable of explaining every single international transaction to SARS and the Reserve Bank, with supporting invoices. The only obstacle is the Applicant himself, who has blocked my access and brought this application to prevent the truth from emerging. I welcome the opportunity to present this evidence to the relevant authorities. |

## Overview

This document contains detailed, evidence-based draft responses for Daniel Faucitt (DR) to the **18 AD paragraphs in Section 10 (Delinquency Allegations)**. These responses are designed to dismantle the Applicant's claims by providing a counter-narrative supported by a clear timeline of events and specific examples of financial misconduct that the Second Respondent (Daniel) uncovered.

## Section 10: Delinquency Allegations (AD 10.1 - 10.13)

| AD Paragraph | AD Content (Summary) | DR (Daniel's Response) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **10.1** | Seeks order declaring respondents delinquent or under probation. | The relief sought is denied. This application is a malicious and strategic abuse of process, brought with the ulterior motive of removing me from my position after I uncovered and reported extensive financial misconduct within the corporate entities. The timing of this application, following my reports, is no coincidence. |
| **10.2** | Claims conduct justifies urgent relief under PART A. | The allegations are denied. The Applicant's feigned urgency is a fabrication. The true motive is to prevent the further exposure of the very financial irregularities I was investigating. My actions, far from justifying this application, were aimed at protecting the companies from ongoing financial harm. |
| **10.3** | Alleges conduct is "clearly and evidently" detrimental. | This allegation is emphatically denied. My conduct was aimed at *preventing* detriment to the companies. The true detrimental conduct is the systematic financial sabotage I uncovered, including the dumping of two years of unallocated expenses onto RegimA Worldwide (RWW) on 30 March 2025, which I was pressured to sign off on within 12 hours. |
| **10.4** | Alleges conduct contravenes Companies Act and CC Act. | This is a bare denial. The Applicant fails to cite a single specific action that constitutes a contravention of my fiduciary duties. To the contrary, my investigation and subsequent reports were a direct fulfillment of my duty to act in the best interests of the companies. |
| **10.5 - 10.5.2** | Cites legal grounds for delinquency/probation. | The contents are noted as the Applicant's statement of the law. However, the Applicant has failed to provide any factual basis to meet the requirements of these sections. This application is a textbook example of strategic litigation against a whistleblower. |
| **10.6 - 10.6.3** | Cites fiduciary duties under the Close Corporations Act. | The contents are noted. I have always been acutely aware of my fiduciary duties. It was precisely my adherence to these duties that compelled me to investigate the financial irregularities that form the true basis of this dispute. |
| **10.7 - 10.7.1.2** | Cites liability for breach of fiduciary duty. | The contents are noted. I deny any breach of my duties and therefore deny any liability. Any losses suffered by the corporations are a direct result of the financial misconduct I sought to expose, not my actions in uncovering it. |
| **10.8** | Cites disqualification for misconduct. | The contents are noted. I deny any misconduct. The Applicant is attempting to use the court to disqualify the very person who was acting to protect the companies from the actual misconduct of others. |
| **10.9 - 10.9.3.3** | Cites grounds for delinquency (gross abuse, harm, negligence, fraud). | I deny these allegations in the strongest possible terms. The only "harm" I have inflicted is upon the fraudulent scheme that was operating within the companies. My actions were not "gross abuse" but a necessary and lawful exercise of my duties. The Applicant's claims of fraud against me are a malicious inversion of the truth. The real fraud is the R5.4 million "stock adjustment" in Strategic Logistics (SLG) and the systematic hijacking of revenue streams from RWW, which began on 1 March 2025. |
| **10.10 - 10.10.2.3**| Cites grounds for probation (inconsistent duties, oppressive conduct). | I deny these allegations. The Applicant's claim of "oppressive conduct" is a fabrication. The true oppression was the Applicant and his associates dumping two years of unallocated expenses on RWW and demanding I sign off on them within 12 hours on 30 March 2025. This was a clear attempt to make me complicit in their scheme. My refusal to do so is not oppressive conduct; it is responsible governance. |
| **10.11** | Concludes that he is advised to seek a delinquency order. | The Applicant's conclusion is noted. It is based on a distorted and incomplete version of events, and I am advised that it has no prospect of success. |
| **10.12** | States full extent of transgressions is unknown and requests a forensic investigation. | I wholeheartedly agree and consent to an order for a truly independent forensic investigation. Such an investigation will vindicate me and confirm the substance of my reports. The investigation must have a broad mandate to investigate the R5.4 million SLG stock adjustment, the two years of unallocated expenses, the diversion of revenue from RWW starting on 1 March 2025, the secret cancellation of my business bank cards on 7 June 2025, and the role of Villa Via in extracting profits from the so-called "Group". |
| **10.13** | Claims "significant sums of money...cannot be accounted for." | I agree. This is the central thesis of the reports I compiled between March and June 2025. The Applicant's attempt to attribute this to me is a desperate and transparent attempt to deflect blame from the individuals who are actually responsible for the missing funds. The Applicant has been aware of these issues since I first reported them and has chosen to attack the messenger rather than address the message. |

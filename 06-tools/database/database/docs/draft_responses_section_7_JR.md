## Overview

This document contains detailed, evidence-based draft responses for Jacqueline Faucitt (JR) to the **20 AD paragraphs in Section 7 (Discovery of Irregularities)**. These responses aim to correct the Applicant's misleading narrative, deny allegations of misconduct, and provide crucial context regarding the events of June and July 2025.

---

## Section 7: Discovery of Irregularities (AD 7.1 - 7.20)

| AD Paragraph | AD Content (Summary) | JR (Jacqueline's Response) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **7.1** | Accountant (Bantjes) attended office in mid-June for tax submissions. | The contents of this paragraph are noted. However, the Applicant's framing is misleading. The Second Respondent had, for months prior, been investigating significant financial irregularities, which were the primary subject of discussion. |
| **7.2** | Bantjes noted "numerous discrepancies" and "irregular payments" in July. | The contents are noted. The "discrepancies" and "irregular payments" were not a new discovery by Mr. Bantjes, but rather the very issues the Second Respondent had been investigating and attempting to bring to the attention of the Applicant and the trustees. |
| **7.3** | Payments seemed to relate to IT expenses. | The contents of this paragraph are noted. |
| **7.4** | IT department is under my son's purview, whose conduct is "erratic". | I deny that my son's conduct has been erratic. This is a pejorative and unsubstantiated characterization. He has been under immense pressure while uncovering serious financial misconduct and attempting to protect the companies from further harm. |
| **7.5** | Cancelled business bank cards because payments could not be explained. | I deny that I was unable to explain any payments. The Applicant's decision to cancel all business bank cards was a unilateral, reckless, and destructive act that paralyzed the companies' operations. It was not a reasonable response to any purported lack of explanation. |
| **7.6** | My son provided a few invoices, but the majority remain unexplained. | The contents of this paragraph are noted. I am not involved in the day-to-day procurement of IT services and cannot speak to the provision of invoices. |
| **7.7** | Subscriptions were halted due to card cancellations. | This is admitted. The disruption to business-critical services was a direct and foreseeable consequence of the Applicant's own reckless actions in cancelling the bank cards. |
| **7.8** | My son adopted a "superior" position and refused to assist. | I deny this characterization. The Second Respondent was attempting to manage a crisis created by the Applicant while simultaneously trying to secure evidence of financial wrongdoing. The Applicant perceived this diligence as a lack of cooperation. |
| **7.9** | Attaches inter-office communications (PF7) as confirmation. | The contents of the annexure are noted. These communications, when read in proper context, do not support the Applicant's narrative. |
| **7.10** | Communications were mostly with his PA upon my instructions. | The contents of this paragraph are noted. |
| **7.11** | It is clear he would or could not provide relevant invoices. | This is denied. The Applicant creates the impression that the Second Respondent was willfully withholding information. The reality is that he was investigating the very same transactions for which the Applicant was demanding invoices. |
| **7.12** | My son interfered with staff doing their jobs. | This is denied. The Second Respondent was directing staff to preserve data and assist in his investigation, which was a necessary and responsible exercise of his duties as a director. |
| **7.13** | He elected not to provide all staff access to historic emails. | The contents of this paragraph are noted. I am advised that this was a necessary security measure to prevent the potential destruction of evidence by individuals implicated in the financial irregularities that the Second Respondent was uncovering. |
| **7.14** | This interference has continued. | This is denied. The Second Respondent's actions are not interference, but responsible management aimed at protecting the companies' assets and data integrity. |
| **7.15** | This conduct is not in the interests of the corporations. | This is emphatically denied. The Second Respondent's conduct in investigating and securing evidence of financial misconduct is fundamentally in the best interests of the corporations. |
| **7.16** | I re-approached the banks and obtained new cards on 24 June 2025. | I admit that I obtained a new card. This was a necessary step to restore critical business operations that had been paralyzed by the Applicant's unilateral cancellation of all payment methods. |
| **7.17** | I rejected any idea of malfeasance or neglect on our son's part. | I admit that I defended my son. The Applicant presented no credible evidence of malfeasance on his part. My assessment at the time, which I maintain, was that the Second Respondent was acting in the best interests of the companies. |
| **7.18** | I transferred R500,000 to him as a "birthday gift" from the corporations. | This is a malicious and misleading distortion of the facts. I deny that I transferred funds *from the corporations*. I made a payment to my son from my director's loan account, which was in credit in my favour. This is a standard practice that has been utilized by all directors, including the Applicant, for many years. The characterization of this as a "birthday gift" is a deliberate attempt to create prejudice. |
| **7.19** | This was without my knowledge and not a legitimate business expense. | The allegation is denied. The Applicant was fully aware of the long-standing practice of directors drawing funds against their loan accounts. It was not a "business expense" but a partial repayment of monies owed to me by the company. The funds were my own. |
| **7.20** | This was separate to his remuneration as director/member. | The contents are noted. The payment was not remuneration, but a personal payment from me to my son, funded by a legitimate draw-down from my director's loan account. |

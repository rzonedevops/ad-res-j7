## 1. Executive Summary

A forensic examination of 69 downloaded documents relating to Strategic Logistics CC (SLG) and its manufacturing operations has revealed severe, systemic control deficiencies and indicators of material misstatement. The evidence demonstrates that SLG operates as a conduit entity for intercompany loan manipulation and revenue stream hijacking.

The most critical finding is the explosion of intercompany debt from **R13.4M in 2020 to R41.7M in 2025**, coupled with highly irregular interest treatments (oscillating from R1.04M paid, to zero, to R1.7M paid, to R3.3K received). Furthermore, the entity exhibits a complete breakdown in inventory control, evidenced by a **negative finished goods balance of R2.76M** in the FY2025 Trial Balance.

## 2. Financial Statement Anomalies (FY2019 - FY2025)

An analysis of the Annual Financial Statements (AFS) and Trial Balances (TB) reveals a pattern of financial manipulation designed to extract or park funds.

### 2.1 The "Receivables Balloon" and Intercompany Debt
Between FY2021 and FY2022, SLG's Accounts Receivable exploded by 330% (from R4.3M to R18.3M) despite revenue remaining flat. Simultaneously, long-term loans doubled from R11.9M to R26.9M. 

The FY2025 Trial Balance confirms the nature of these loans. SLG holds **R41,662,646** in intercompany debt:
- RegimA - Loan Account: R11,436,599
- Villa Via - Loan Account: R12,213,624
- RegimA World Wide Dist. - Loan Account: R18,012,424

**Audit Implication:** SLG is being used to absorb debt from other entities in the RegimA group. The corresponding asset is parked in "Customer Control Accounts" (R24M in FY2025), suggesting funds are being channeled out of the group through SLG.

### 2.2 The "Oscillating Interest" Anomaly
The treatment of interest on these massive loan balances is highly irregular and indicates non-arm's-length transactions:
- **FY2019:** R1,041,095 interest paid on R13.5M loans (7.7%)
- **FY2020:** R414,334 interest paid on R13.4M loans (3.1%)
- **FY2021:** R0 interest paid on R11.9M loans (0%)
- **FY2022:** R1,722,184 interest paid on R26.9M loans (6.4%)
- **FY2025:** R3,354 interest *received* despite R41.7M in loans.

**Audit Implication:** Interest is being arbitrarily applied or waived, likely to manage the tax position of the entities involved. This violates SARS transfer pricing and arm's-length principles.

### 2.3 Inventory Control Failure
The FY2025 Trial Balance shows a **Credit (Negative) Balance of R2,756,321.85** for "Inventory Control - Finished Goods". 

**Audit Implication:** It is physically impossible to have negative inventory. This indicates that cost of sales/dispatch records are being processed without corresponding production receipts. This is a catastrophic failure of the manufacturing MRP system and suggests unrecorded production or off-book sales.

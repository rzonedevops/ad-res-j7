
## VI. The Financial Manipulation Schemes (2020-2025)

Recent analysis of the Exchange mail records reveals three distinct but interconnected financial manipulation schemes orchestrated through the intercompany transaction and stock adjustment mechanisms in the Pastel/Sage accounting system.

### 1. The Intercompany Loan Account Manipulation (2020–2021)
On **17 August 2021**, Rynette directed Bantjes on what journal entries to make to "fix" intercompany loan accounts, including backdating entries to August 2020. This inversion of proper financial controls allowed Rynette to dictate the financial narrative, including making R109,896.92 "disappear" between Villa Via and Regima Skin's books.

### 2. The Stock Adjustment Fraud — "Bernadine's Gogga" (2024–2025)
On **4 April 2025**, Rynette revealed to Bantjes a massive ~R4.2 million stock discrepancy in Strategic Logistics: R1.4M in phantom finished goods on the Trial Balance, and a R2.7M credit balance (negative stock). Rather than properly restating the financials, they planned to "permanently remove" this discrepancy. Bantjes admitted on **7 April 2025** that he suspected "huge gaps by now, building up over many years," confirming he had signed off on materially misstated financials for years.

### 3. The SARS Query — The Smoking Gun (June 2025)
On **5 June 2025**, SARS queried "two big inter company invoices" pushed through at the end of February 2025 (financial year-end). Rynette admitted to Bantjes on **6 June 2025**: "The two big invoices were done on your request, for which I have no answer." Bantjes, the external accountant who initiated the suspicious transactions, then drafted the response to SARS, representing a severe conflict of interest and potential tax fraud.

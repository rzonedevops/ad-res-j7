# Close Corporation Member Removal — Strategic Logistics CC

## Overview

This section contains the draft court application for the removal of Peter Andrew Faucitt as a member of **Strategic Logistics CC (2008/136496/23)** under **Section 36** of the Close Corporations Act 69 of 1984.

## Legal Framework: CC vs. Pty

The process for removing a member from a Close Corporation is fundamentally different from removing a director from a (Pty) Ltd company.

| Feature | Company (Pty) Ltd | Close Corporation (CC) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mechanism** | Board Resolution (s71(3)) or Shareholder Resolution (s71(1)) | **Court Application (s36)** |
| **Who decides?** | The Board or Shareholders | **The High Court** |
| **Requirement** | Vote by majority | Application to court with evidence |
| **CIPC Form** | CoR39 | CK2 (but only **after** court order is granted) |

**Crucially, you cannot remove a CC member by resolution.** The other members must apply to the High Court for an order.

## Grounds for Application (Section 36)

The application against Peter Faucitt is based on two primary grounds:

1.  **s36(1)(b):** He has been guilty of conduct likely to have a **prejudicial effect** on the carrying on of the business (e.g., the R5.4M stock loss, the fraudulent interdict).
2.  **s36(1)(c):** He so conducts himself that it is **not reasonably practicable** for the other members (Jacqui and Daniel) to carry on the business with him (e.g., perjury, sabotage, breakdown of trust).

## Process Flow

1.  **Finalize Application** — The draft founding affidavit is finalized and signed before a Commissioner of Oaths.
2.  **Issue at High Court** — The application is issued at the High Court, assigned a case number, and served on Peter Faucitt.
3.  **Court Hearing** — The matter is argued in court.
4.  **Obtain Court Order** — If successful, the court grants an order for the cessation of Peter's membership.
5.  **File CK2 with CIPC** — The CK2 form is filed with CIPC, with the court order attached, to amend the CC's records.

### 2.3. The API Key Failures
The administrative change from Kayla to Peter had immediate, catastrophic technical consequences. The API integrations between the Sage accounting system and the Shopify e-commerce platforms were configured under Kayla's administrator account. 

When the administrator was changed, the existing API keys failed. Consequently, invoices stopped syncing between the platforms. This created the exact "financial chaos" and "missing records" that Peter later cited in his founding affidavit as evidence of Daniel's "mismanagement."

This locked all parties out of the accounting records, destroying the audit trail just weeks before Peter approached the court claiming financial mismanagement.

## 4. Legal Characterization and Criminal Liability

This conduct transcends civil disputes and enters the realm of serious criminal offenses.

### 4.1. Defeating or Obstructing the Course of Justice
Under South African common law, defeating the ends of justice consists of "unlawfully and intentionally engaging in conduct which defeats or obstructs the course or administration of justice."

The applicant's conduct satisfies all elements:
1. **Unlawful:** Using a fraudulently obtained court order to deny respondents access to legal representation.
2. **Intentional:** The denial is deliberate and targeted—funding his own attorneys while refusing to process transactions for the respondents.
3. **Defeats Justice:** The respondents are actively prevented from presenting documentary evidence proving the applicant caused the very problems he blames them for.

### 4.2. Fraud on the Court (Fraus Curiae)
The interdict was obtained through perjury and material non-disclosure. The applicant concealed that he and his bookkeeper caused the API failures through the forged Sage affidavit, and instead blamed the resulting chaos on the respondents.

- **Contravention of POPIA:** Deliberately letting the Sage subscription expire to deny data subjects access to their financial records (Sections 107(1), 107(2), and 107(3)).

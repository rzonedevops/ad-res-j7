## 3. Legal Implications and Violations

### 3.1. The Crime-Fraud Exception to Legal Privilege
Under South African law (confirmed by the Constitutional Court in *Thint (Pty) Ltd v NDPP*), legal professional privilege is absolute—**unless** the communication is made in furtherance of a crime or fraud. 

Because Rynette is a Tier A conspirator who orchestrated the forged Sage affidavit and the subsequent API failures, her communications with Elliott Attorneys are designed to perpetuate and conceal that fraud. Therefore, **the crime-fraud exception applies**. Communications between Rynette (acting "pp Peter") and Elliott Attorneys regarding the suppression of evidence or the filing of contempt applications are not privileged and are subject to discovery.

### 3.2. Witness Intimidation and Defeating the Ends of Justice
Filing a responding affidavit is a fundamental constitutional right under Section 34 (access to courts). Rynette's tactic of filing criminal perjury charges against the respondents *because* they submitted responding affidavits is a textbook example of witness intimidation.

This creates a chilling effect:
1. Respondents submit an affidavit exposing the Sage fraud.
2. Rynette files criminal charges against them.
3. The threat of criminal prosecution deters the respondents from filing further evidence.
4. The High Court is deprived of the truth.

This conduct satisfies all elements of the common law crime of **Defeating or Obstructing the Course of Justice**: unlawfully and intentionally engaging in conduct that defeats the administration of justice.

### 3.3. The "PP Peter" Agency Breach
When Rynette communicates with Elliott Attorneys "pp Peter" (*per procurationem* - on behalf of), she acts as his agent. However, an agent cannot use their mandate to further their own criminal interests. Because Rynette is actively concealing her own identity fraud (operating as Peter in Sage) and her role in the API failures, she is using Peter's legal representation to shield herself. She is the *de facto* litigant, using Peter as a proxy.

## 4. The Ethical Exposure of Elliott Attorneys

The Legal Practice Act 28 of 2014 imposes strict ethical duties on attorneys. Elliott Attorneys are currently taking instructions from a non-client (Rynette) who has a profound conflict of interest and is a material witness in the very applications she is instructing them to file.

If Elliott Attorneys know, or reasonably should know, that Peter does not use a computer and that Rynette is generating the instructions, they face severe ethical exposure:
1. **Failure to Verify Instructions:** Attorneys must take instructions from their client, not a conflicted third party.
2. **Facilitating Abuse of Process:** By filing the 4th and 6th urgent applications based on Rynette's supporting affidavits—while she controls the communication channel—the attorneys risk becoming instruments of her witness intimidation strategy.
3. **Conflict of Interest:** They are representing Peter, but taking instructions from Rynette, whose interests (concealing her Sage fraud) conflict with Peter's legal exposure.

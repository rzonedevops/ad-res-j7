# Backdated Journal Entries — 2021 Forgery

**Type:** Forgery / Uttering / Financial Record Falsification  
**Confidence:** 99%  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-18

## Summary

On 17 August 2021, Rynette Farrar directed Daniel Jacobus Bantjies to make specific journal entries dated 01/08/2020 — over a year backdated — to "fix" intercompany loan accounts. This included making R109,896.92 "disappear" between the books of two entities. Bantjies accepted the instructions without objection, representing a complete inversion of financial controls where the external accountant acted as a data-entry clerk for the bookkeeper.

## Legal Analysis

### Forgery and Uttering (Common Law)

The creation of journal entries with false dates constitutes forgery of accounting records. The subsequent use of these records in financial statements filed with CIPC and SARS constitutes uttering a forged document.

### Control Inversion

This event demonstrates the fundamental control inversion that characterises the entire intercompany fraud network:

1. **Normal Controls:** External accountant reviews and verifies bookkeeper's entries.
2. **Actual Practice:** Bookkeeper (Rynette) directs external accountant (Bantjies) on what entries to make, including specific amounts and dates.
3. **Consequence:** The external accountant becomes complicit in the fraud rather than serving as a check on it.

### SAICA Code Violations

Bantjies' acceptance of instructions to backdate journal entries violates:

- **Section 113 (Professional Competence and Due Care):** Accepting instructions from a bookkeeper to backdate entries demonstrates a failure to maintain professional standards.
- **Section 111 (Integrity):** Executing backdated entries without objection or disclosure is dishonest.

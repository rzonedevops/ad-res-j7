# Intercompany Fraud Network

**Type:** Financial Fraud / Racketeering  
**Confidence:** 97%  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-18

## Summary

Forensic analysis of fincosys bank statement data cross-referenced with Exchange email evidence reveals a systematic intercompany fraud network operating across the RegimA entity structure. The network involves R58.58 million in intercompany transactions across 364 transfers, with the largest flow (R35.17 million) from RegimA Skin Treatments to RegimA SA — a new entity created to capture revenue that should have flowed to the original entity.

## Fraud Mechanisms

### 1. Fraudulent Year-End Intercompany Invoices (February 2025)

Bantjies initiated "two big inter company invoices" at year-end that were so anomalous they triggered an independent SARS audit. Rynette admitted she had "no answer" for these invoices, proving they were not legitimate business transactions.

### 2. Backdated Journal Entries (August 2021)

Rynette directed Bantjies to make specific journal entries dated 01/08/2020 (over a year backdated) to "fix" intercompany loan accounts. This included making R109,896.92 "disappear" between the books of two entities.

### 3. Stock Discrepancy Cover-Up (April 2025)

A R4.2 million stock discrepancy in Strategic Logistics was discovered — comprising R1.4M phantom stock and R2.7M impossible negative finished goods. Rather than formally restating the financials, Rynette proposed to "permanently remove this Bernadine gogga for good." Bantjies admitted "huge gaps building up over many years."

### 4. Control Inversion

Normal financial controls were inverted: the bookkeeper (Rynette) directed the external accountant (Bantjies) on what entries to make, ensuring the true financial picture was known only to the inner circle.

# Rule 30/30A Dating Anomaly — Elliott Attorneys

**Type:** Procedural Irregularity / Credibility  
**Confidence:** 100% (Documentary)  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-18

## Summary

Both the Rule 7(1) Notice and Rule 30/30A Notice filed by Elliott Attorneys on behalf of the Applicant are explicitly dated and signed "03 DAY OF FEBRUARY 2026" on their signature pages. This is a temporal impossibility: the notices critique the Entity Answering Affidavit which was only served on 19 February 2026 (16 days after the purported signing date), and relate to a Contempt Application filed on 05 February 2026 (2 days after the purported signing date).

## Legal Significance

### 1. Undermines Demand for Procedural Perfection

Elliott Attorneys' Rule 30/30A Notice demands strict procedural compliance from the Respondents, alleging that the Entity Answering Affidavit is an "irregular step" because it is unsigned, not commissioned, and lacks board resolutions. The attorneys' own failure to correctly date their notices — a basic procedural requirement — undermines their standing to demand perfection from the opposing party.

### 2. Pattern of Documentary Carelessness

This dating anomaly is consistent with the broader pattern of documentary irregularities in the Applicant's case:

- The founding affidavit contained perjury regarding the FNB mandate (SOLE vs. joint)
- The trust amendment was forged by Rynette (EVENT_103)
- The Main Trustee designation was backdated (11 Aug 2025 to 1 Jul 2025)
- The J417 form contained a false declaration of independence by Bantjies

### 3. Potential Defence Point

In any application to set aside the Entity Answering Affidavit under Rule 30, the Respondents may raise this anomaly to demonstrate that the Applicant's own attorneys do not meet the procedural standards they demand of others.

# Stock Adjustment Fraud — R4.2M Discrepancy

**Type:** Financial Fraud / Cover-Up  
**Confidence:** 99%  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-18

## Summary

A R4.2 million stock discrepancy in Strategic Logistics CC was discovered in April 2025, comprising R1.4M in phantom stock and R2.7M in impossible negative finished goods. Rather than formally restating the financials as required by accounting standards and the Companies Act, the respondents proposed to "permanently remove" the discrepancy — constituting a deliberate cover-up of material financial misstatement.

## Legal Analysis

### Why This Is a Cover-Up, Not a Correction

The defence will argue that Bantjies agreed to "fix" the problem. This argument fails for three reasons:

1. **No Formal Restatement:** Bantjies did not propose restating the prior-year financials as required by IAS 8 (Accounting Policies, Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors). A legitimate correction requires formal restatement and disclosure.

2. **No Regulatory Disclosure:** Neither Bantjies nor Rynette reported the material misstatement to CIPC (as required by s.29 of the Companies Act) or to SARS (as required by s.234 of the Tax Administration Act).

3. **Context of "Permanently Remove":** Bantjies' response must be read in the context of Rynette's stated intent to "permanently remove" the discrepancy. His agreement to "get the real and actual stock values to agree to Pastel" in this context means adjusting the records to hide the discrepancy, not to formally correct it.

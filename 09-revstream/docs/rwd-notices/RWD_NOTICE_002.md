## D. THE SELF-CONTRADICTING DEMAND

8.  The Applicant's position is irreconcilably contradictory:

    *   On 11 Feb 2026, the Second Respondent restored the broken Shopify-Courier Guy API integration necessary to fulfill orders.

## E. THE TRANSACTIONAL CONTROL BOUNDARY

10. The Court Order grants the Applicant **transactional control** (a payment-processing function), not operational, strategic, or governance authority.
11. The Courier Guy portal is an operational system. Creating an API key is a governance decision, not a transactional one. The Court Order does not prohibit directors from accessing operational systems.

## F. THE SHOPIFY DECOMMISSIONING — IN THE APPLICANT'S OWN WORDS

11. Elliott Attorneys, in their letter of 12 Dec 2025, admitted to decommissioning Shopify, claiming it provided "no sales — only costs."
12. This is factually incorrect. The revenue flowed *through* Shopify. The shutdown moved the entire revenue pipeline from a transparent, auditable platform to an opaque, single-user-controlled system (Sage Pastel, under Ms. Farrar's de facto control).

## G. THE COMMUNICATION SEVERANCE DEMAND

13. Elliott Attorneys demanded that all communications from the Second Respondent to the entities be routed attorney-to-attorney, based on a void settlement agreement.
14. A director's right to communicate with his own company is a statutory right. No court order or board resolution can sever this.

## H. THE COURIER GUY LOGIN REMOVAL

15. Subsequent to the Second Respondent restoring the API key, his login credentials to The Courier Guy portal were removed without board authorisation.
16. This Entity demands to know who authorised this unilateral revocation of a director's access to an operational system.

## I. THE PATTERN — SYSTEMATIC OPERATIONAL LOCKOUT

18. The Courier Guy login removal is part of a documented pattern of systematic operational lockout, including the takeover of Sage, deletion of Shopify trails, cancellation of bank cards, and replacement of the entity's domain.
19. The pattern is: a director demonstrates capability -> the capability is destroyed -> the exercise of that capability is then framed as "interference."

## J. CONTEMPT — THE FOUR REQUIREMENTS

21. The threat of contempt is without merit. The four requirements for civil contempt are not met, specifically requirement 21.3 (non-compliance with the order). The Court Order does not prohibit a director from accessing The Courier Guy portal.

## K. DEMANDS AND RESERVATION OF RIGHTS

24. This Entity demands the immediate restoration of the Second Respondent's Courier Guy credentials, withdrawal of the contempt allegation, and restoration of the Shopify platform.
25. This Entity reserves all rights to hold the Applicant and/or his agents personally liable for all damages flowing from the systematic destruction of this Entity's operational infrastructure.

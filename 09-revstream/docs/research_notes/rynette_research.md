# Research: Rynette as De Facto Litigant and the Legal Implications

## Key Finding 1: Crime-Fraud Exception to Legal Professional Privilege
Per Thint (Pty) Ltd v NDPP (Constitutional Court):
Requirements for privilege include: "the advice must not facilitate the commission of a crime or fraud"

If Rynette is using the attorney-client relationship to further the fraud scheme, privilege is LOST.
Elliott Attorneys' communications with Rynette (on behalf of Peter) are NOT privileged if they further the fraud.

## Key Finding 5: Elliott Attorneys' Ethical Obligations
Under the Legal Practice Act 28 of 2014:
- Attorneys must act in the best interests of their client
- Attorneys must not knowingly participate in fraud on the court
- Attorneys must not take instructions from a non-client who has a conflict of interest
- If Elliott Attorneys know or should know that Rynette is controlling the litigation for her own benefit, they have an ethical obligation to:
  1. Verify instructions directly with Peter
  2. Report the conflict to Peter

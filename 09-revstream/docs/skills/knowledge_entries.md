### Entry 2: Power of Attorney (Rule 7)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Power of Attorney (Rule 7)`

**use_when:** Analyzing authority to act on behalf of another party in court proceedings, forged signatures, "pp" (per procurationem) signing, unauthorized representation, or any question about who has authority to take steps in litigation.

**content:**
Rule 7 governs who may act on behalf of a party in court proceedings.

- **Rule 7(1):** No person other than a party to any cause or matter shall be entitled to act on behalf of such party unless he is an attorney or unless he has been authorized thereto by such party by a power of attorney.

- **Rule 7(2):** A power of attorney to act shall be filed with the registrar by the person so authorized before he takes any step in the proceedings.

This rule is critical when analyzing:
- Whether a person who signed documents "pp" (per procurationem) had actual authority
- Whether a bookkeeper or accountant had legal authority to sign trust resolutions or company documents on behalf of a director
- Whether steps taken in proceedings by an unauthorized person are valid
- Forged or fabricated powers of attorney

A signature "pp Peter" by a bookkeeper on a trust resolution, without a filed power of attorney, is unauthorized and the resulting document is invalid. Any proceedings or actions flowing from such unauthorized acts may be set aside under Rule 30 (irregular proceedings).

### Entry 3: Applications (Rule 6)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Applications (Rule 6)`

**use_when:** Analyzing or drafting applications, notices of motion, affidavits, ex parte applications, urgent applications, or interdicts in the South African High Court.

**content:**
Rule 6 governs all applications. Key sub-rules:

- **Rule 6(1):** Every application must be on notice of motion, supported by an affidavit with the facts.

- **Rule 6(4)(a):** *Ex parte* applications must be on notice to the registrar, filed before noon on the court day but one preceding the hearing, and set forth the order sought. Urgent applications must state the basis for urgency.

- **Rule 6(5)(d):** A respondent opposing an application must deliver a notice of intention to oppose, then an answering affidavit within 15 days.

- **Rule 6(12):** In urgent applications, the court may dispense with the forms and service provided for in the rules and dispose of the matter at such time and place and in such manner and in accordance with such procedure as it deems fit. The applicant must explicitly set forth the circumstances rendering the matter urgent and the reasons why they would not be afforded substantial redress at a hearing in due course.

### Entry 4: Irregular Proceedings (Rules 30, 30A)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Irregular Proceedings (Rules 30, 30A)`

**use_when:** A party has taken an irregular step in court proceedings, or has failed to comply with the rules or a court order.

**content:**
- **Rule 30: Irregular Proceedings.** A party can apply to court to set aside an irregular step taken by another party. The application must be made within 10 days of becoming aware of the step. The court has discretion to set it aside in whole or in part, grant leave to amend, or make any other suitable order.

- **Rule 30A: Non-compliance.** If a party fails to comply with the rules or a court order, any other party may notify them and, if non-compliance continues for 10 days, apply to court for an order compelling compliance or striking out a claim/defence.

### Entry 5: Rescission of Orders (Rule 42)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Rescission of Orders (Rule 42)`

**use_when:** An application is made to vary or rescind a court order, particularly one that may be void or was obtained through error or fraud.

**content:**
Rule 42 allows the court to rescind or vary an order:

- **Rule 42(1)(a):** If the order was erroneously sought or erroneously granted in the absence of any party affected thereby.

- **Rule 42(1)(b):** If there is an ambiguity, or a patent error or omission, but only to the extent of such ambiguity, error or omission.

- **Rule 42(1)(c):** If the order was granted as the result of a mistake common to the parties.

An order is also void *ab initio* (from the beginning) if obtained by fraud on the court or perjury, which is a common law ground for rescission. Such an order has no legal force or effect.

### Entry 6: Contempt & Execution (Rules 45, 45A)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Contempt & Execution (Rules 45, 45A)`

**use_when:** Analyzing contempt of court applications or the process of executing a judgment (e.g., attaching property).

**content:**
- **Rule 45: Execution.** Governs the process of executing judgments, including the issuing of writs of execution against movable and immovable property by the registrar.

- **Rule 45A: Contempt of Court.** A court may, on application, declare a person to be in contempt of court and make an appropriate order. This is used to enforce compliance with court orders. The applicant must prove the existence of the order, service on the respondent, and non-compliance. The evidentiary burden then shifts to the respondent to show that their non-compliance was not wilful and *mala fide* (in bad faith).

### Entry 7: Discovery (Rule 35)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Discovery (Rule 35)`

**use_when:** Dealing with the discovery process in an action, including requests for documents, discovery affidavits, and claims of privilege.

**content:**
Rule 35 governs the discovery of documents and tape recordings.

- **Rule 35(1):** A party may require any other party to make discovery on oath of all documents relating to any matter in question in the action.

- **Rule 35(3):** A party may require another to produce for inspection any documents referred to in their pleadings or affidavits.

- **Rule 35(7):** If a party fails to make discovery, the other party may apply for an order compelling discovery, and if they still fail, to have the claim or defence struck out.

- **Rule 35(12):** A party may require another to produce any document referred to in their pleadings or affidavits for inspection and to permit the making of a copy.

### Entry 8: Review (Rule 53)

**name:** `SA Court Rules: Review (Rule 53)`

**use_when:** Bringing proceedings to review the decision of a lower court, tribunal, board, or officer performing a judicial, quasi-judicial, or administrative function.

**content:**
Rule 53 governs review proceedings.

- **Rule 53(1):** Proceedings must be brought by notice of motion, which must call upon the decision-maker to show cause why the decision should not be reviewed and corrected or set aside.

- **Rule 53(1)(b):** The notice must also call upon the decision-maker to dispatch, within 15 days, the record of the proceedings sought to be corrected or set aside, together with such reasons as are required by law or desired to be given.

- **Rule 53(4):** The applicant may, within 10 days after the registrar makes the record available, amend, add to or vary the terms of the notice of motion and supplement the supporting affidavit.

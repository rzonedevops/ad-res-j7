## TRANSCRIPTION NOTES

This document is the **Notice of Motion** in the ex parte application filed by Peter Andrew Faucitt, dated **13 August 2025** at Pretoria, filed via Court Online on **14 August 2025 at 08:16:25 AM SAST**. The hearing was set for **19 August 2025 at 10h00**.

### Document Structure

The Notice of Motion is divided into two parts:

**PART A** (Interim/Urgent Relief — paragraphs 1–7): Sought under Rule 6(12)(a) urgency provisions, this section requests sweeping interim control over all company banking, financial affairs, and records. It demands the surrender of all bank cards, passwords, and documentation, and authorises Peter Faucitt to take exclusive control of all four companies' financial affairs.

**PART B** (Final Relief — paragraphs 8–11): Seeks a declaration of delinquency under Section 162(5) of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 and Section 69(8)(a) read with Section 47 of the Close Corporations Act 69 of 1964, or alternatively probation under Section 162(7)(a) and (8). Costs on Scale C (attorney-client scale).

### Key Observations

1. **Ex Parte Nature:** This is an ex parte application — the respondents were not given notice before the hearing. The application was filed on 14 August 2025 (Thursday) for hearing on 19 August 2025 (Tuesday), giving only 3 business days.

2. **Sweeping Relief Sought:** Part A paragraphs 2.1–2.8.3 seek extraordinary control over every aspect of the companies' operations, including:
   - Surrender of all bank cards (2.1)
   - Prohibition on opening new accounts (2.2)
   - Prohibition on applying for new cards (2.3)
   - Prohibition on transacting (2.4)
   - Prohibition on dealing with business, employees, partners, clientèle (2.5)
   - Exclusive financial control to Peter (2.6)
   - Authority to appoint experts at company cost (2.7)
   - Surrender of all documentation, login details, and passwords (2.8.2)
   - Compelled participation in forensic investigation (2.8.3)

3. **Delinquency Declaration:** Part B paragraph 8.1 seeks to declare Jacqueline and Daniel Faucitt as delinquent directors — a severe remedy that would bar them from all directorships.

4. **Attorney Details:** Elliott Attorneys Inc., Office 12, Garsfontein Office Park, 645 Jacqueline Drive, Garsfontein, Pretoria. Contact: Keegan (keegan@elliottattorneys.co.za), Tel: 012 012 5067. Reference: KRE/MM/KF0019.

5. **Court Stamps:** Every page bears the stamp of the Registrar of the High Court of South Africa, Gauteng Division, Pretoria, confirming the document was filed and processed.

### Evidentiary Significance for Void Ab Initio

This Notice of Motion is the **instrument of the fraud on the court**. The relief sought in Part A was granted ex parte on 19 August 2025, giving Peter Faucitt exclusive control over all four companies' banking and financial affairs. This enabled the extraction of R10,624,131.18 within 8 days of the order.

The NOM was supported by:
- A founding affidavit containing perjury and material non-disclosures (pages 10–38)
- A confirmatory affidavit from Bantjes sworn with 68 days of foreknowledge (pages 69–71)

Because the underlying affidavits are fraudulent, the order granted pursuant to this NOM is **void ab initio** — it never had legal force.

Definitions

In addition to the definitions referred to in clause 2 above, the following definitions apply
to the A Ordinary Share Terms —

1.1 Company Redemption Period means the period that commences on the expiry
of 3 years and one day calculated from the Effective Date and terminates on the
date immediately prior to which the Put Option Period commences;

1.2 Company Redemption Consideration means the redemption price payable to
the A Ordinary Shareholder by the Company to redeem all or a portion of the A
Ordinary Shares during the Company Redemption Period, which redemption
consideration must calculated by the Company and approved by the A Ordinary
Shareholder such that on the applicable redemption date, the A Ordinary
Shareholder will receive the annual IRR as calculated from the Subscription
Date until the Redemption Date based on an the initial subscription amount of
R1,960 per A Ordinary Share;

At end year 3 — R3737 per share which = R18 685 000 for 5000 shares.
At end year 4 — R4633 per share which = R23 165 000 for 5000 shares.
At end year 5 — R5746 per share which = R28 730 000 for 5000 shares.

1.3 Distribution has the meaning ascribed to that term in clause 2.1.16;
1.4 Early Redemption Date means 3 years and 1 day after the subscription date

1.5 Final Redemption Date means the first day immediately after the last day of
the Initial Period;

1.6 IRR means the nominal post corporate tax and pre-withholdings tax internal
rate annual return of not less than 24% which the A Ordinary Shareholder, as
holder of equity in the Company, is forecast to achieve on its investment in the
Company during the Initial Period, it being recorded that the corporate tax
contemplated in this clause 1.6 is the corporate tax incurred and payable by The
George Group and not the Company.

1.7 A Ordinary Shareholder Redemption Period means a period commencing on
the expiry of 5 years calculated from the Subscription Date and terminating 2
months thereafter;

1.8 A Ordinary Shareholder Redemption Consideration means the redemption
price per A Ordinary Share payable to the A Ordinary Shareholder by the
Company to redeem all or a portion of the A Ordinary Shares for the periods
between year 3 and 5 as outlined in 1.2 above;

2. Voting and Distribution Rights attaching to the A Ordinary Shares

2.1 Each A Ordinary Share entitles the A Ordinary Shareholder to receive 90% of
all Distributions ("Dividend Sweep") for a period of 5 years and 2 months years
from the Subscription Date ("Initial Period").

2.2 Other than as contemplated in this Agreement and in this Annexure B, during
the Initial Period, A Ordinary Shareholders will not be entitled to exercise any
votes on matters to be decided by the Ordinary Shareholders except if —

2.2.1 the Board and/or A Ordinary Shareholders propose a matter which expressly
requires the approval of the A Ordinary Shareholder/s as described in this
Agreement or the MOI;

2.2,2 the Company receives a Distribution from The George Group and fails,

subject to the solvency and liquidity requirements of the Company, to declare
and pay a Distribution to the Shareholders, in which event the Ordinary
Shareholders shall not be allowed to exercise their voting rights;

2.2.3 the Company wishes to dispose of all or some of its equity in The George
Group;
2.2.4 any proposal is made to amend (directly or indirectly) the preferences, rights,

limitations and other terms of the A Ordinary Shares;

2.2.5 any proposal is made to create or issue any shares in the Company ranking
in priority to or pari passu to the A Ordinary Shares; or

2.2.6 the Ordinary Shareholder/s of the Company propose a resolution which
affects the rights and privileges attaching to any A Ordinary Shares or the
interests of the A Ordinary Shareholders, (which includes a resolution for the
winding-up, commencement of business rescue proceedings or liquidation of
the Company, the reduction of the Company's Share capital or the acquisition
by the Company of its shares in terms of section 48 of the Companies Act,
other than as contemplated in this Annexure B).

2.3 For purposes of any matter required to be voted on or approved by resolution of
the A Ordinary Shareholders and the Ordinary Shareholders as contemplated in
this Agreement and in paragraphs 2.2.1, 2.2.3 and 2.2.4 of this Annexure B, the
A Ordinary Shareholders will be entitled and notwithstanding the number of
A Ordinary Shares held by them, to exercise 49% of all votes to be cast on a
proposed resolution.

Redemption of A Ordinary Shares during Company Redemption Period and
A Ordinary Shareholder Redemption Period

3.1 With effect from the commencement of and during the Company Redemption
Period, the Company shall be entitled, subject to the solvency and liquidity
provisions as set out in the Act, to redeem all or a portion of the A Ordinary
Shares for a redemption consideration equal to the Company Redemption
Consideration.

3.2 During the Company Redemption Period, the Company may any time give
notice to the A Ordinary Shareholder that it intends to redeem all or a portion of
the A Ordinary Shares. The notice must specify —

3.2.1 the number A Ordinary Shares which the Company intends to redeem;

3.2.2 the Company Redemption Consideration;

3.2.3 the date (being a weekday) on which redemption will be completed, which
date shall be not more than two months after the date of the relevant notice;
and

3.2.4 the bank account to which the Company Redemption Consideration will be

paid and if not available the A Ordinary Shareholder must provide the
Company with its bank account details within 5 days of receipt of the notice.

4. Conversion of A Ordinary Shares

If the Company is unable for any reason to redeem any or all of the A Ordinary Shares
on the Final Redemption Date, then the Company and the Shareholders must take such
steps to convert all the outstanding A Ordinary Shares to Ordinary Shares on a one for
one basis and after such conversion all Ordinary Shares and the A Ordinary Shares shall
rank pari passu in all respects and that each of the holders of the Ordinary Shares and the
A Ordinary Shares will be entitled to the same rights and privileges arising
notwithstanding the different classification of the Shares.

**Case Number:** 2025-137857
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Source:** Forensic extraction of 624 messages, 154 EML files, 9 OCR-processed key documents

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Faucitt Family Trust (FFT) invested **R9,800,000** into Ketoni Investment Holdings (Pty) Ltd (Reg 2023/562189/07), an SPV whose sole asset is an 8.14% stake (456 shares) in The George Group (Pty) Ltd. The investment includes a structured exit mechanism with call/put options valued between **R18,685,000** (Year 1, May 2026) and **R28,730,000** (Year 5, May 2029).

The evidence establishes that **Daniel Bantjies** is the CFO of The George Group, where **Kevin Michael Derrick** is CEO and sole director of Ketoni. This creates a direct, undisclosed conflict of interest when Bantjies was appointed as Trustee of the FFT in July 2024, giving him control over whether the FFT exercises its put option against his own employer.

## 6. Accounting Irregularity: R9.8M Misallocation

On 2023-05-09, Bantjies instructed Rynette Farrar:

> "For now, please allocate the Ketoni payment to Pete's shareholder loan account."

This is significant because the R9.8M investment was made **by the FFT** (Trust money), not by Pete personally. Allocating a Trust investment to Pete's personal shareholder loan account is a **misallocation of Trust assets**, effectively treating Trust property as Peter's personal asset.

## 10. Implications for Applications

### Application 1: Civil/Criminal Actions
- **Breach of Fiduciary Duty:** Bantjies' dual role (George Group CFO + FFT Trustee) constitutes a direct conflict of interest.
- **Misallocation of Trust Assets:** R9.8M Trust investment allocated to Pete's personal loan account.
- **Fraud:** Systematic exclusion of beneficiaries from investment information.

### Application 2: CIPC Companies Act Complaint
- **s75 Personal Financial Interest:** Bantjies has a personal financial interest in the outcome of the Ketoni option exercise.
- **s76 Director Duties:** Bantjies' conflict of interest as Trustee controlling FFT's Ketoni investment while employed by the counterparty.
- **s162 Delinquency:** Pattern of self-dealing and beneficiary exclusion.

### Application 3: POPIA Criminal Complaint
- **s23 Data Subject Access:** Jax was denied share certificates and investment documents.
- **Information Asymmetry:** Trust financial information processed through personal channels.

### Application 4: Commercial Crime
- **Fraud:** Misallocation of R9.8M Trust investment to personal loan account.
- **Breach of Trust:** Conflicted trustee appointment.

### Application 5: NPA Tax Fraud
- **Tax Implications:** If treated as personal loan, different tax treatment applies.
- **Transfer Pricing:** George Group structure may facilitate profit extraction.

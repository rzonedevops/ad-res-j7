## The Five Pillars of Provable Foreknowledge

### Pillar 1: Peter Knew the Switch Would Revoke Access
The Sage Disclaimer Form he signed on 8 July 2024 explicitly states: **"I accept that all other users that I have invited to this company profile, will no longer have access to the company dataset/s."** He marked this "N/A" rather than acknowledging it, but the clause was on the form he signed. He cannot claim he did not know.

### Pillar 2: Peter Knew the Switch Broke Integrations
Rynette's email of 10 July 2024 (Email 5) reported that Shopify orders were "not sinking with SAGE." Her follow-up (Email 6) revealed "all sorts of connectors are connected to her email address." Peter was CC'd on these emails. He knew the automated invoice matching, bank feeds, and commerce platform integrations were broken.

### Pillar 3: Peter Personally Participated
Peter did not merely authorize the switch from a distance. He personally swore an affidavit before a Commissioner of Oaths (Nicos Xenophontos, Practising Attorney, Bedfordview) on 8 July 2024. He personally signed two Sage Disclaimer Forms. This was not Rynette acting alone — Peter was the active principal.

### Pillar 4: Peter Concealed His Actions from the Court
In the founding affidavit for the *ex parte* interdict (19 August 2025), Peter did not disclose that he had taken over the Sage accounting system, that the takeover broke all integrations, or that the invoices he was demanding were inaccessible because of his own actions. This is a material non-disclosure in an *ex parte* application, where the duty of *uberrima fides* (utmost good faith) applies.

### Pillar 5: Peter Then Demanded What He Knew Could Not Be Produced
Having broken the systems that generate and match invoices, Peter then demanded that Daniel produce those very invoices. When Daniel could not (because the systems were broken), Peter used this as the basis for the interdict and subsequently the contempt application. This is textbook entrapment.

## The "Does Not Use Computers" Perjury

In the founding affidavit for the *ex parte* interdict, Peter Faucitt claimed he "does not use computers" and has no personal knowledge of the digital systems. The Sage evidence destroys this claim:

1. Peter swore a formal affidavit specifically to take over a **cloud computing system** (Sage Business Cloud Accounting).
2. Peter signed forms to become the **administrator** of the cloud accounting platform.
3. Email 10 confirms "We have logged in" — Peter (or Rynette on his behalf) logged into the cloud system.
4. Peter was CC'd on all technical emails about the system switch.

If Peter truly "does not use computers," then either (a) he perjured himself in the Sage affidavit by claiming personal knowledge he did not have, or (b) he perjured himself in the interdict founding affidavit by claiming he does not use computers when he demonstrably does. Either way, he committed perjury.

## Criminal Implications

### Perjury (Criminal Procedure Act, s319)
Peter swore under oath in the founding affidavit that he does not use computers. The Sage affidavit proves he does. One of these sworn statements is necessarily false.

### Fraud (Common Law)
Peter obtained a court order by concealing material facts (the Sage sabotage) from the court. This constitutes fraud on the court.

### Defeating the Ends of Justice (Criminal Procedure Act, s319)
By sabotaging the accounting systems and then demanding production of records he knew were inaccessible, Peter manufactured the appearance of mismanagement to obtain a court order. He is now using that fraudulently obtained order to seek the imprisonment of Jacqueline Faucitt.

### Contravention of the Companies Act (s214 — Reckless Trading)
By deliberately breaking the accounting infrastructure of four operating companies, Peter endangered the companies' ability to trade, comply with tax obligations, and maintain proper financial records.

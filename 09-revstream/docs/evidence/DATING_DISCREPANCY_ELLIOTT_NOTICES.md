# Evidence: Dating Discrepancy in Elliott Attorneys' Rule 7(1) and Rule 30/30A Notices

**Date Discovered:** 2026-03-12
**Case No:** 2025-137857
**Documents Affected:** ND-05 (Rule 7(1) Notice) and ND-06 (Rule 30/30A Notice)

---

## Summary

Both the Rule 7(1) Notice and the Rule 30/30A Notice filed by Elliott Attorneys on behalf of the Applicant (Peter Andrew Faucitt) are explicitly dated and signed **"03 DAY OF FEBRUARY 2026"** on their respective signature pages. This creates a temporal impossibility, as both documents critique and reference the Entity Answering Affidavit which was only served on **19 February 2026** — sixteen days after the notices were purportedly signed.

## The Temporal Impossibility

| Date | Event | Relationship to Notices |
|------|-------|------------------------|
| **03 February 2026** | Date printed on BOTH notices | The purported date of signing |
| **05 February 2026** | Contempt Application (Notice of Motion) filed | Filed 2 days AFTER the notices were supposedly signed |
| **19 February 2026** | Entity Answering Affidavit served by Daniel Faucitt | Served 16 days AFTER the notices were supposedly signed |
| **03 March 2026** | Actual filing/service date (per file names) | The true date, one month after the printed date |

### Internal References Proving the Impossibility

The Rule 7(1) Notice states at paragraph 1:

> "On **19 February 2026**, Mr Daniel James Faucitt, the second respondent served via email a document titled 'Answering affidavit of the third to sixth respondent — application to set aside Part A of the ex parte interdict as void ab initio and opposition to contempt of court application.'"

A document dated 03 February 2026 cannot reference an event that occurred on 19 February 2026. The document was clearly drafted and signed on **03 March 2026**, with "February" written in error instead of "March."

---

## Legal Significance

### 1. Hypocrisy of Procedural Perfection Demands

Elliott Attorneys filed these notices specifically to attack the Entity Answering Affidavit for procedural defects — including that it was unsigned, uncommissioned, and improperly drafted. Yet their own formal court notices contain a glaring dating error that renders them temporally impossible. An attorney who demands strict procedural compliance from opponents must demonstrate it in their own filings.

### 2. Technical Invalidity of the 10-Day Deadline

The Rule 30/30A Notice gives 10 days to remedy the irregular step. If the notice is dated 03 February 2026, the 10-day deadline would have expired on approximately **13 February 2026** — six days before the Entity Answering Affidavit even existed. The deadline is therefore technically meaningless as stated on the face of the document.

### 3. Basis for Condonation

The dating error demonstrates that procedural imperfections are a normal incident of litigation, even for experienced attorneys. This provides a strong basis for seeking condonation of any technical defects in the Respondents' filings under the court's inherent discretion.

### 4. Counter-Attack Under Rule 30/30A

The incorrectly dated notices are themselves arguably an "irregular step" under Rule 30. A court document that states it was signed on a date that is temporally impossible (before the events it references) is defective on its face. The Respondents could serve their own Rule 30/30A notice on this basis, though the strategic value of doing so must be weighed against the risk of escalating procedural warfare.

## Recommended Use in Filings

This finding should be deployed in the following manner:

1. **In the Contempt Opposition (Track A):** Reference the dating discrepancy to argue that the Applicant's attorneys are not entitled to demand procedural perfection when their own filings are defective.

2. **In the Rescission Application (Track B):** Use it as context for any condonation application if the court raises procedural concerns about the Respondents' filings.

3. **In correspondence with Elliott Attorneys:** Reference the discrepancy when responding to the Rule 30/30A notice, noting that the 10-day deadline as stated on the face of the document has no temporal validity.

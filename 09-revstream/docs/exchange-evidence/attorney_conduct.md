# Exchange Evidence: Attorney Conduct & Correspondence

**Source:** Exchange Mailbox Records (Neon DB: exchange_sync)
**Extracted:** 2026-03-08
**Messages Found:** 30 (Legal Correspondence), 19 (Attachments)

---

## Evidence Summary: Weaponization of Legal Process

The collected correspondence from Elliott Attorneys, representing Peter Faucitt, demonstrates a clear pattern of using legal threats and court orders to intimidate and control Jacqueline Faucitt, a co-director and CEO of the company. The evidence suggests a weaponization of the legal process, particularly the use of an *ex parte* interdict (Case 2025-137857) to restrict her from performing her duties and accessing her own funds.

### Key Events & Correspondence

1.  **Threat of Contempt (2025-09-30):** Keegan Elliott sends an URGENT email to Jacqueline Faucitt, threatening contempt of court proceedings. The alleged "contemptuous" act was Jacqueline's intention to attend a scheduled training for her own company, RegimA Skin Treatments. This action is framed as a violation of the court order, despite its clear detriment to the business.

    > **Elliott:** "We advise that the conduct is contemptuous of the present Court order... Our client would be well within his right to proceed with contempt of Court proceedings... you are requested to IMMEDIATELY DESIST with conduct which contravenes the Court order."

2.  **Refusal of Funds & Aggression (2025-12-13):** Jacqueline Faucitt emails Keegan Elliott directly, stating that Peter Faucitt is refusing to pay her money from her own bank account and is becoming aggressive. She explicitly asks Elliott to confirm if he advised Peter to take this course of action.

    > **Jacqueline:** "PETER SAYS YOU TOLD HIM NOT TO PAY ME AND MUST TAKE ME TO COURT. PLEASE CONFIRM THIS... HE IS NOW VERY AGGRESSIVE."

3.  **Legal Representation Carousel:** The evidence shows a rapid change in legal representation for the respondents (Jacqueline and Daniel Faucitt). 
    - **ENS Africa** withdraws on **2025-09-24**.
    - **Ian Levitt Attorneys** enters on **2025-10-01** and withdraws on **2025-11-14**.
    - **Pottas Attorneys** enters on **2025-12-10**.
    This turnover in legal representation may indicate difficulties in mounting a defense against the ongoing legal pressure.

4.  **Non-Response and Escalation (December 2025):** After taking on the case, Rudi Pottas of Pottas Attorneys sends multiple emails to Elliott Attorneys regarding the payment of funds to Jacqueline Faucitt. These emails are met with silence, forcing Pottas to escalate his language, warning that Peter Faucitt's "conduct will be to his prejudice."

### Analysis in Light of Uniform Rules of Court

-   **Rule 6(12) (Urgent Applications):** The foundation of the legal pressure is the *ex parte* interdict. The validity of this order, obtained without the respondents present, is questionable, especially if it was based on misleading information or did not meet the strict requirements for urgency.
-   **Rule 45A (Contempt of Court):** Elliott's threat of contempt proceedings appears to be a tool of intimidation rather than a legitimate legal remedy. Using a court order to prevent a CEO from attending her own company's training could be seen as an abuse of process.
-   **Professional Conduct:** The correspondence raises questions about the professional conduct of the attorneys involved, particularly the use of legal threats to enforce actions that are detrimental to a business and the well-being of a co-director.

## Conclusion

The evidence strongly suggests that the legal process, orchestrated by Peter Faucitt and executed by Elliott Attorneys, was used as a tool to financially and operationally cripple Jacqueline Faucitt. The threat of contempt, the refusal to release funds, and the aggressive behavior all point to a coordinated campaign of intimidation and control, leveraging a potentially flawed *ex parte* court order.

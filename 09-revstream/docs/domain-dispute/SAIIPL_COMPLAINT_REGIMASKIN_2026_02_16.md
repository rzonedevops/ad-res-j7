## 11. Factual and Legal Grounds (Abusive Registration)

This complaint is based on the domain name being an **abusive registration** as defined in Regulation 1 of the .ZA ADR Regulations.

### 11.1 Complainant's Rights in the Name "RegimA"

The Complainant has established, extensive, and common law trademark rights in the name and mark "RegimA" through over two decades of continuous use in the skincare industry in South Africa and internationally. The name is synonymous with the Complainant's business.

### 11.2 The Domain Name is an Abusive Registration

The domain `regimaskin.co.za` is an abusive registration for the following reasons:

**(a) Registered to Disrupt Complainant's Business (Regulation 4(1)(c))**

The domain was registered on **29 May 2025**, just 7 days after the Complainant's primary e-commerce audit trails on its Shopify platform were deleted. The domain was then used to create a parallel, unauthorized communication and sales channel, diverting customers and revenue away from the Complainant's legitimate infrastructure. This constitutes a textbook case of disruption.

**(b) Registered to Unfairly Attract Internet Users by Creating Confusion (Regulation 4(1)(d))**

The domain `regimaskin.co.za` is confusingly similar to the Complainant's well-established "RegimA" mark. It was used to create email addresses (e.g., `pete@regimaskin.co.za`) that were used to impersonate the Complainant's directors and mislead customers and suppliers, thereby unfairly attracting and diverting internet traffic and commercial opportunities.

**(c) Registrant is a Related Party with Provable Foreknowledge**

The Registrant, Addarory (Pty) Ltd, is a company whose sole director is the son of Ms. Rynette Farrar. Ms. Farrar is the central figure in the operational sabotage of the Complainant's business, as detailed in the attached evidence bundle (**Annexure B**). The registration of the domain by a close family member, timed perfectly with the destruction of the Complainant's e-commerce platform, demonstrates clear intent and coordination.

## Legal Implications

### Professional Negligence
Bantjies, as the external accountant and auditor for Villa Via, used an incorrect company registration number (1999/061687/23 instead of 1996/004451/23) on all financial statements from 2014 to January 2022. This constitutes:

1. **Companies Act s30(2):** Financial statements must correctly identify the company
2. **Tax Administration Act s234:** Filing returns with incorrect taxpayer information
3. **SARS eFiling:** All VAT returns, provisional tax, and income tax filed under wrong registration number for 8 years
4. **CIPC Annual Returns:** Filed under incorrect registration number

### Consciousness of Guilt
Rynette's comment — "good thing CIPRO, and SARS are sleeping" — demonstrates:
- Both parties knew the error had CIPC and SARS implications
- Neither party reported the error to CIPC or SARS
- They chose to quietly correct the front page rather than file corrections
- This is consistent with the pattern of concealment throughout the evidence

### Connection to Bantjies as Trustee
The wrong registration number was used from 2014 — the same year Villa Via and RWD were transferred to the Faucitt Family Trust (2014-06-18). Bantjies, who later became trustee, was responsible for the financial statements of a trust-owned entity while using the wrong company number for 8 years.

# Multi-Entity Correspondent Framework

## 1. The Legal and Structural Problem

The current litigation (Case 2025-137857) involves multiple corporate entities as respondents, and potentially several other non-party entities that hold relevant evidence. 

The structural challenges are:
1. **Rule in Battle**: Corporate entities cannot represent themselves in the High Court; they must be represented by a legal practitioner.
2. **POPIA Compliance**: Directors cannot unilaterally take company records for personal litigation. The company (as the Responsible Party) must formally authorize the release of records for court proceedings under POPIA Section 12(2)(d)(iii).
3. **Fiduciary Duty**: Each company is an independent juristic person. The directors must act in the best interests of *each specific company* when releasing records or participating in litigation.
4. **Cost Constraints**: The applicant's interdict has frozen funds, making full legal representation impossible.

## 2. The Solution: The "Unbundled" Correspondent Hub Model

To solve this, we use an **Unbundled Legal Services** model combined with a **Single Correspondent Hub**.

### How It Works:
1. **Internal Preparation**: Jax and Dan (as directors/members) prepare all evidence reports, affidavits, and legal arguments internally.
2. **Board Resolutions**: Each company passes a formal board/member resolution authorizing the release of specific evidence and appointing the correspondent attorney.
3. **Single Correspondent**: One correspondent attorney is appointed to act for *all* corporate entities on a **limited mandate** (filing and hearing appearances only).
4. **No Conflict of Interest**: Because all corporate entities share the same interest (defending against a hostile takeover/interdict) and are not claiming against each other, a single attorney can represent all of them without conflict.

### Cost Efficiency
By using a single correspondent attorney for all entities, the companies share the cost of court appearances. A single 1-hour hearing appearance (approx. R1,680 - R2,500) covers the presentation of evidence for all 4+ corporate entities simultaneously.

## 3. The Workflow for Filing Evidence

When a company needs to file evidence with the court, the following workflow is executed:

**Step 1: Evidence Compilation**
The directors compile the evidence report from the company's databases.

**Step 2: Board Resolution**
The company's board (or sole director/member) signs a resolution authorizing the release of the specific report under POPIA.

**Step 3: Representative Affidavit**
A director drafts a short affidavit in their *representative capacity*, attaching the board resolution and the evidence report.

**Step 4: Instruction to Correspondent**
The affidavit and annexures are sent to the correspondent attorney with a specific instruction: *"Please file this on Court Online and present it at the next hearing."*

## 4. Required Templates

To operationalize this framework, three standard templates are required:

1. **Master Mandate Agreement**: The contract between the companies and the correspondent attorney defining the limited scope of work.
2. **Standard Board Resolution**: The internal company document authorizing litigation and evidence release.
3. **POPIA Evidence Release Schedule**: The annexure detailing exactly what data is being released and why.

*(These templates are provided in the `docs/filings/templates/` directory).*

## Description

On 10 January 2026, with offices scheduled to re-open following the holiday period, Peter Faucitt obtained a list of required stock from the warehouse manager (annexure PF14). Jacqueline Faucitt prevented Peter and Oliver ("Arthur") from removing stock from the store at their house necessary to service new orders and replenish warehouse stock.

The stock, packaging, and raw materials are kept at the common home to prevent theft, and are withdrawn to the warehouse for picking, packing, and distribution to clients as needed. This was the established norm for the business operations.

Peter managed to remove the stock after Jacqueline's initial refusal, when she no longer "manned" the stock-room, and had the stock conveyed to the warehouse on 13 January 2026 to service new orders with an estimated value of R200,000.

When Peter provided the stock list to Jacqueline, she flatly refused to allow the release, crumpled up the stock sheet, and took it with her.

## Legal Significance

This constitutes a direct breach of the 19 August 2025 court order, specifically paragraph 2.5 which interdicts the respondents from dealing with the business in any manner that may cause prejudice. The deliberate blocking of stock removal to service client orders is a clear act of business sabotage in defiance of the court order.

Peter's affidavit describes the conduct as "malicious, deliberate, irrational, and defiant in an obvious effort to subvert the first order of this court."

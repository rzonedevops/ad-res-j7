# EVENT_137: Bantjies Secretly Forwards Family Communication to Rynette

## Event Metadata
- **Date:** 2025-05-26 13:07:29
- **Type:** Email Communication / Fiduciary Breach / Conspiracy to Conceal
- **Entities Involved:** PERSON_005 (Danie Bantjies), PERSON_004 (Rynette Farrar), PERSON_001 (Peter Faucitt), PERSON_002 (Jacqui Faucitt), PERSON_003 (Daniel Faucitt)
- **Source:** `01_2025-05-26_RegimaGroupofCompanies_metadata.json`, `02_2025-05-26_FwdRegimaGroupofCompanies_metadata.json`, Neon DB email ID 62, Graph API msg_02

## Event Description

At 13:06:43 on 26 May 2025, Danie Bantjies sent an email to Jacqui, Daniel, and Peter Faucitt regarding "uncertainties surrounding the computer expenses as well as the financial status of the Regima Group of Companies." In this email, he positioned himself as a neutral arbiter, stating:

> "I prefer not to meet with individual parties regarding this matter to maintain my impartiality."

Exactly **46 seconds later**, at 13:07:29, Bantjies forwarded this exact email to Rynette Farrar (Peter's personal bookkeeper, who holds no directorship or shareholder status in any RegimA entity).

Crucially, Bantjies included an explicit instruction to conceal this communication:

> *"Ek dink nie jy moet sê ek het hierdie met jou gedeel nie asb."*
> (Translation: "I don't think you should say I shared this with you please.")

## Full Email Content (Original to Family)

Bantjies proposed a structured review of the RegimA Group:

1. Present a factual report on consolidated external revenues, net trading profits/losses, computer expenses, and consolidated bank balances for the past two financial years.
2. Attached a schedule of all computer expenses allocated by Rynette for March and April 2025, requesting Danny provide descriptions, business applications, and SARS justifications for each.
3. Stated he was "expecting SARS to audit our computer expenses claimed for tax."

## The Follow-Up Chain (June 3-10, 2025)

On **3 June 2025 at 15:47**, Bantjies sent a follow-up to Jacqui, Daniel, and Peter asking for progress on the analysis.

On **10 June 2025 at 09:58**, Bantjies sent his analysis email ("The RegimA Group results and Computer Expense analysis") to all three family members, revealing:

- The RegimA Group had made a **"substantial trading loss"** for the first time in its history.
- Bank balances showed a **decline of R10M year-on-year** for the Group.
- Computer expenses exceeded **20% of revenue generated**.
- He stated: "For any business to spend in excess of 20% of revenue on these services, the spend needs to be justified, transparent, relevant and needs to add the desired value to the business."

On **10 June 2025 at 11:45**, Daniel responded with a detailed analysis explaining:

- Worldwide Distribution absorbed all costs when cards expired in Aug 2023 (two weeks after Kayla's murder).
- Rynette had misallocated international GoDaddy payments as local Rezonance payments, making the debt "disappear."
- Pete and Rynette had attempted to draft mandates to change ownership structures, close Daniel's accounts, and cancel his cards without his knowledge or consent.
- Revenue streams were being redirected to accounts only Rynette could access.
- Daniel explicitly warned: "moving assets from a company where I am a director to a company where I am not is essentially theft or at the very least fraudulent for tax purposes."

On **10 June 2025 at 15:33**, Bantjies replied dismissively: "Your comments and assertions will be taken under consideration in due course. I am unfortunately away for 2 weeks."

## Legal & Strategic Significance

### 1. Premeditated Collusion (Criminal)
The 46-second gap between sending the original email and forwarding it to Rynette demonstrates that the forward was premeditated. Bantjies was already coordinating with Rynette before he even sent the email to the family. This establishes a **conspiracy** under the Prevention and Combating of Corrupt Activities Act (PRECCA).

### 2. Destruction of Neutrality (SAICA Ethics)
Bantjies' claim in the original email that he prefers "not to meet with individual parties" is immediately contradicted by his secret sharing with Rynette. This is a direct violation of SAICA's Code of Professional Conduct regarding independence and objectivity.

### 3. Fiduciary Breach (Companies Act s.76)
As the accountant for all companies, Bantjies shared confidential financial communications with an unauthorized third party who is neither a director, shareholder, nor employee of the relevant entities.

### 4. Instruction to Conceal (Obstruction)
The explicit instruction to Rynette to hide the fact that he shared the email (*"Ek dink nie jy moet sê ek het hierdie met jou gedeel nie asb"*) is direct evidence of a conspiracy to conceal information from the directors/shareholders. This constitutes **obstruction of justice** when considered alongside the subsequent fraud.

### 5. Timeline Context — Pre-Whistleblowing Compromise
This occurred on **26 May 2025**, before Daniel reported the Villa Via fraud to Bantjies on **6 June 2025** (EVENT_126). It proves that Bantjies was already compromised and colluding with Rynette before Daniel's whistleblowing, explaining why Bantjies later:
- Ignored the fraud report
- Participated in the retaliation
- Made the "manufacture" admission to SARS
- Facilitated the intercompany fraud network

### 6. Daniel's Warnings Ignored
Daniel's June 10 response explicitly warned about:
- Revenue stream hijacking ("take all payments directly into an account which only Rynette has access to")
- Fraudulent misallocation of GoDaddy payments as Rezonance payments
- Unauthorized mandate changes and account closures
- The legal distinction between company ownership and intercompany transfers

All of these warnings were dismissed by Bantjies with "away for 2 weeks" — and every single one subsequently materialized as the fraud pattern documented in the intercompany analysis.

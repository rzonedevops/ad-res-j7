## Description

Multiple ongoing breaches of the 19 August 2025 court order have been documented:

**1. Bank Cards Not Surrendered (para 7.3, 8.2.2):**
Per the court order (paragraph 2.1), the first and second respondents were interdicted and directed to surrender forthwith their banking credit, cheque, and/or debit cards coupled to the banking accounts of the third to sixth respondents. As of the contempt application date, the bank cards have never been handed over.

**2. Oliver's Work Phone Removed (para 8.2.3, PF17):**
Jacqueline removed Oliver's ("Arthur's") work phone from his person, disallowing communication between the warehouse, office, and stock room for stock removals and stock taking. This was addressed by Elliott Attorneys in annexure PF17 (letter dated 2 December 2025), which demanded the phone be returned and reminded Jacqueline that Oliver is an employee of the entities, paid by the entities, and not her personal employee.

**3. Body Guard Intimidation (para 6.12):**
Jacqueline began attending the office of the entities on an almost daily basis with a "body guard" (working for Risk International), who she introduces as her driver but who remains present at all times. This carries a negative and prejudicial effect on staff morale and interferes with clientele of the fifth respondent (Villa Via Arcadia coffee shop).

## Legal Significance

Each of these acts constitutes a separate breach of the 19 August 2025 court order:
- Bank card non-surrender: Direct breach of paragraph 2.1
- Phone removal: Breach of paragraph 2.5 (prejudicial dealing with business)
- Body guard intimidation: Breach of paragraph 2.5 (prejudicial interference with employees and clientele)

The pattern of repetitive non-compliance demonstrates wilful and mala fide contempt, satisfying the fourth requirement for a contempt finding per the founding affidavit para 11.1.4.

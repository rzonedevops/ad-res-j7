## Description

On **Monday 22 September 2025**, Jacqueline Faucitt and Daniel James Faucitt gave notice of withdrawal from the two settlement agreements signed during the mediation of 18 September 2025. This notice was given immediately after receiving copies of the signed documents on **Friday 19 September 2025**.

The withdrawal was prompted by the circumstances under which the agreements were signed. During the mediation on 18 September 2025, Daniel Faucitt was **not allowed to leave the building without signing**. An ENS email from the mediation records Daniel Faucitt's request to **file a criminal complaint** during the mediation itself, documenting the coercive conditions under which the agreements were obtained.

In response, Peter Faucitt lodged a second application under Case No. 2025-137857 on 3 October 2025 to have the settlement agreements made orders of court. This application may be found on Caselines at pocket 008. The settlement agreements are annexed as PF13 (pocket 008:7) and PF14 (pocket 008:8).

As of the date of the contempt application (February 2026), this second application became opposed but no answering affidavits had been filed, and the matter remains pending.

## Legal Significance

The notice of withdrawal from the settlement agreements was not "bad faith" or "repudiation" as characterized by Peter Faucitt. It was a legitimate response to agreements obtained under duress. The ENS email documenting Daniel's request to file a criminal complaint during the mediation is contemporaneous evidence of the coercive conditions. Agreements signed under duress are voidable at the election of the aggrieved party.

The fact that Peter Faucitt then sought to have these duress-tainted agreements made orders of court (second application, 3 October 2025) is a further act of weaponized litigation — attempting to use the court to enforce agreements that were obtained through coercion.

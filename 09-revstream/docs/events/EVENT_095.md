## Description

Following the ex parte order of 19 August 2025 (Case 2025-137857), a mediation was arranged between Peter Faucitt, Jacqueline Faucitt, and Daniel James Faucitt, all represented by their respective attorneys. Two written settlement agreements resulted from this mediation.

The first agreement (PF13) provided for psychiatric evaluations of all parties and drug screening for Daniel and Peter, with deadlines of 15 October 2025 for psychiatric evaluations and 8 October 2025 for drug screening.

The second agreement (PF14) addressed the implementation of the 19 August 2025 court order, including the appointment of Forvis Mazars for a forensic investigation into the financial affairs of the third to sixth respondents, transaction processes pending the forensic investigation, and reimbursement of Daniel's bona fide business-related transactions.

## Legal Significance

Both settlement agreements were signed by all parties on 18 September 2025 under conditions of duress — Daniel Faucitt was not allowed to leave the building without signing, and an ENS email records his request to file a criminal complaint during the mediation. The subsequent notice of withdrawal on 22 September 2025 (the first business day after receiving copies on 19 September 2025) was a legitimate response to agreements obtained through coercion. Peter Faucitt then filed a second application on 3 October 2025 to have the duress-tainted agreements made orders of court.

## Key Terms of Second Settlement (PF14)

1. **Forvis Mazars** appointed for forensic investigation
2. Peter to email Jacqueline and Daniel before any transactions on entity accounts
3. Daniel to be reimbursed for bona fide business expenses since 7 June 2025
4. Parties remain bound by 19 August 2025 court order
5. Paragraph 2.8.3 of court order modified to require honest answers to forensic investigators

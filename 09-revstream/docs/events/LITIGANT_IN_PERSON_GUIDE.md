# Litigant in Person Guide: Defending Against the Circular Trap

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Subject:** Practical steps for self-representation in the South African High Court when funds are frozen by an interdict.
**Case Number:** 2025-137857

## 1. The Right to Self-Representation

In South Africa, every individual has the constitutional right to represent themselves in legal proceedings, known as appearing as a "litigant in person" [1]. When a litigant is unrepresented, the presiding judge has a duty to assist them to ensure a fair hearing and prevent prejudice [2].

However, navigating the High Court requires strict adherence to procedural rules. This guide provides the exact steps to dismantle the "circular trap" (where a void interdict freezes funds, preventing legal representation against a contempt application).

## 2. Immediate Action: Accessing the Court File

The Gauteng Division of the High Court operates entirely on the **Court Online** digital system (Caselines) [3]. You must gain access to the digital case file immediately.

1.  **Register on Court Online:** Go to [https://www.courtonline.judiciary.org.za](https://www.courtonline.judiciary.org.za).
2.  **Use Your SA ID:** Litigants in person can register using their South African Identity Number.
3.  **Request Access:** Once registered, search for Case Number **2025-137857** and request access to the case bundle.
4.  **Get Help if Needed:** If you cannot access the system, the 2025 Court Online Directive mandates that unrepresented litigants must be assisted. Contact the helpdesk immediately:
    *   **Email:** CourtOnlineSupport@judiciary.org.za (Include your SA ID number and state you are an unrepresented litigant in Case 2025-137857).
    *   **Phone:** 010 493 2600 (Monday-Friday, 08h00-16h00).
    *   **In-Person:** Go to the Court Online Service Desk at the Johannesburg High Court.

## 3. Securing Pro Bono Legal Representation

Because the interdict has frozen your access to company funds, you qualify for emergency legal assistance based on indigence (lack of available funds).

### Option A: Legal Aid South Africa
Legal Aid SA provides free representation for those who cannot afford it, including urgent High Court matters [4].
*   **Toll-Free Advice Line:** 0800 110 110 (Monday-Friday, 7am-7pm)
*   **Please Call Me:** 079 835 7179
*   **Johannesburg Office:** 29 De Beer Street, Braamfontein (Tel: 011 877 2000)
*   **Action:** Call immediately, state that you are facing an urgent contempt application with a threat of imprisonment on 17 March, and your funds have been frozen by the applicant's interdict.

### Option B: ProBono.Org
This NGO connects individuals with private attorneys who work for free [5].
*   **Website:** https://probono.org.za/
*   **Action:** Contact them urgently regarding the 17 March hearing.

## 4. Filing the Answering Affidavit (Deadline: 10 March 2026)

If you cannot secure an attorney before the 10 March deadline, you must file an Answering Affidavit yourself to prevent the matter from proceeding unopposed.

### How to File:
1.  **Draft the Affidavit:** Use the template provided in `docs/filings/TEMPLATE_ANSWERING_AFFIDAVIT_LIP.md`.
2.  **Commission the Affidavit:** Print the document and take it to a Commissioner of Oaths (any police station, post office, or bank manager). You must sign it in their presence.
3.  **Scan to PDF:** Scan the signed and stamped document into a single PDF file.
4.  **Upload to Court Online:** Log into Court Online, navigate to Case 2025-137857, and upload the PDF under the correct document type ("Answering Affidavit"). **Do not select "other"** [3].
5.  **Serve via Email:** Email the PDF to Elliott Attorneys (the applicant's attorneys) and state in the email: *"Service of Answering Affidavit by First Respondent in person."*

## 5. Appearing in Court on 17 March 2026

If you do not have an attorney by the hearing date, you must appear in person at the Johannesburg High Court.

### What to Do in Court:
1.  **Arrive Early:** Arrive by 09h00 to find the correct courtroom (check the urgent roll posted in the foyer).
2.  **Introduce Yourself:** Approach the judge's registrar (clerk) before the judge enters and state: *"I am Jacqueline Faucitt, the First Respondent. I am appearing in person because the applicant's interdict has frozen my funds."*
3.  **Address the Judge:** When your case is called, stand up. Address the judge as "My Lord" or "My Lady."
4.  **Request a Postponement:** State clearly:
    > *"My Lord/Lady, I am appearing in person and I am requesting a postponement under Rule 6(8). The applicant obtained an ex parte interdict that froze my access to all company funds. Because of their order, I cannot afford to pay an attorney to defend me against this contempt application, which seeks my imprisonment. This is a denial of my Section 34 Constitutional right to access the courts. I need time to secure Legal Aid or pro bono representation. Furthermore, the urgency is self-created, as the applicant waited six months to bring this application."*

The Constitutional Court has ruled that the right to a fair trial includes the right to a postponement to secure legal representation, especially when substantial injustice (like imprisonment) would result [6].

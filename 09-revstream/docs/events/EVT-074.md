## Description

On 11 September 2025, consolidated FNB account statements show that approximately **ZAR 10 million** was emptied from ALL company current accounts. This occurred less than one month after the ex parte interdict was granted (19 August 2025).

The consolidated report dated 11 September 2025 shows the accounts were stripped, forcing the entities to default on creditors. This is contrasted with the RWD account (62323196362) which showed a closing balance of approximately **ZAR 5.3 million** on 2 July 2025 — just two months earlier — with no indicators of financial distress.

## Evidence Sources

1. **FNB Account Statement — RegimA Worldwide Distribution (62323196362):** Closing balance ~ZAR 5.3 million on 2 July 2025 (file: `2025-07-02_-_FNB_Account_Statement_-_RegimA_Worldwide_Distribution_6362.pdf`)

2. **FNB Consolidated Report (11 Sep 2025):** Shows ~ZAR 10 million removed from ALL company accounts (file: `2025-09-11_-_FNB_Account_Statements_Consolidated_Report_Final.pdf`)

3. **FNB Statement Scans (11 Sep 2025):** Individual account snapshots for RST (5642), RWD (6362), SLG (1494), VVA (0807)

## Declined IT Transactions

The bank statements specifically show declining transactions to **Microsoft, GoDaddy, Google, and Shopify** — all legitimate IT business expenses. The applicant's characterization of these as illegitimate is contradicted by their nature as standard business infrastructure costs.

## Forensic Significance

The emptying of all company accounts within weeks of the interdict constitutes financial sabotage. The accounts went from a healthy combined balance (RWD alone had ZAR 5.3M) to effectively zero, forcing defaults on creditors and operational collapse. This pattern is consistent with asset stripping in anticipation of legal proceedings.

## Description

When Daniel Faucitt discovered that Rynette Farrar had been operating the email account pete@regima.com (sending 6,085 messages over 7 years under Peter Faucitt's identity) and blocked the account around early July 2025, Rynette seamlessly migrated the impersonation to **pete@regimaskin.co.za** — a domain owned by her son (Darren Dennis Farrar)'s company, Adderory (Pty) Ltd.

The last message from pete@regima.com was sent on **6 July 2025**. The first message from pete@regimaskin.co.za appeared on **20 August 2025** — the day after the ex parte interdict was granted. This timing is not coincidental: the interdict gave Peter (and by extension Rynette) sole control of the companies, and the new email address on Rynette's son (Darren Dennis Farrar)'s domain gave Rynette complete, unmonitored control of the impersonated identity.

## Legal Significance

This event establishes that Rynette Farrar committed sustained identity fraud over 7 years, operating Peter Faucitt's email to conduct business, control financial systems, and create a paper trail attributable to Peter. The migration to a domain controlled by her son (Darren Dennis Farrar) demonstrates premeditation and the intent to continue the impersonation beyond the reach of the legitimate domain administrators.

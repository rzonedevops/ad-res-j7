## 3. Executive Summary

This submission details a coordinated criminal enterprise involving fraud, theft, forgery, identity theft, and defeating the ends of justice, with a total quantum exceeding **R63,000,000**. The respondents have used their positions of trust to systematically hijack revenue streams, extract corporate funds, and abuse the court system to silence the complainant.

The evidence demonstrates a clear pattern of premeditated criminal conduct, including the use of an ex parte interdict obtained through perjury as a tool to facilitate the theft of R10,924,131.18 from four corporate entities within 8 days.

## 4. Key Criminal Offences

### 4.1 Fraud (Common Law)
-   **The Offence:** The respondents obtained an ex parte interdict on 19 August 2025 by making false representations under oath regarding the R500,000 transfer and Daniel Faucitt's banking authority. The interdict was then used as a tool to extract R10,924,131.18 from four corporate entities.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Interdict Void Ab Initio](../relations/INTERDICT_VOID_AB_INITIO.md), [RELATION: Peter's R10.9M Extraction](../relations/PETER_R10_6M_EXTRACTION.md).

### 4.2 Theft (Common Law)
-   **The Offence:** R10,924,131.18 was extracted from the accounts of RWD, RST, VVA, and SLG between 3-11 September 2025, without the knowledge or consent of the other directors/members, and in violation of the entities' fiduciary obligations.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Peter's R10.9M Extraction](../relations/PETER_R10_6M_EXTRACTION.md), Entity Answering Affidavit (ND-01).

### 4.3 Forgery and Uttering (Common Law)
-   **The Offence:** On 28 June 2024, Rynette Farrar forged the signature "pp Peter" on a trust amendment to unlawfully appoint Bantjies as a trustee. This forged document was subsequently used ("uttered") to grant Bantjies authority he did not lawfully possess.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Trust Capture Sequence](../relations/TRUST_CAPTURE_SEQUENCE.md), [EVENT_103](../events/EVENT_103.md).

### 4.4 Racketeering (POCA)
-   **The Offence:** The pattern of criminal conduct --- spanning forgery, identity theft, revenue diversion, perjury, and corporate fund extraction --- constitutes a pattern of racketeering activity as defined in the Prevention of Organised Crime Act (POCA). The respondents operated as an enterprise to systematically defraud the corporate entities and their legitimate stakeholders.
-   **Evidence:** All relations documented in the [Master Relations Index](../relations/index.md).

### 4.5 Perjury and Fraud on the Court (Common Law)
-   **The Offence:** The Applicant obtained an ex parte interdict on 19 August 2025 by swearing under oath that Daniel Faucitt made "unauthorized" transfers. Documentary evidence (FNB Legal letter dated 18 June 2025, reference EA-06) proves the Applicant **knew** Daniel had a SOLE mandate at the time he swore his founding affidavit. This constitutes perjury with provable foreknowledge and fraud on the court. The supporting affidavit of Daniel Bantjies, sworn on 13 August 2025, is similarly tainted: Bantjies received Daniel's fraud report on 6 June 2025 (68 days before swearing his affidavit) and failed to disclose this material fact.
-   **Evidence:** [Entity Answering Affidavit (ND-01)](../evidence/new_documents_feb_mar_2026.md#nd-01-entity-answering-affidavit--void-ab-initio), [RELATION: Interdict Void Ab Initio](../relations/INTERDICT_VOID_AB_INITIO.md), FNB Legal Letter (EA-06).

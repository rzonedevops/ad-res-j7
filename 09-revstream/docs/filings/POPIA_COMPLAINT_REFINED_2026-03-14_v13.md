## 3. Executive Summary of Violations

This complaint details severe, systematic, and criminal violations of the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) by the respondents. The core of the complaint is the **Identity Fraud Network** orchestrated by Rynette Farrar and the **systematic exclusion of beneficiaries from their personal information** by Daniel Bantjies.

New evidence from the **Ketoni Investment** reveals that beneficiary Jacqueline Faucitt was denied access to the shareholder agreement, share certificates, and financial statements relating to a R9.8 million trust investment made in her name. She was forced to make repeated demands in January 2026 to obtain these documents, which were held by the conflicted trustee, Daniel Bantjies. This constitutes a severe breach of Section 23 of POPIA (Right of access to personal information).

These POPIA violations were not accidental; they were instrumental in the execution of a multi-million rand revenue hijacking and trust fraud scheme.

## 4. Key Violations of POPIA

### 4.1 Systematic Denial of Access to Personal Information (s23) - The Ketoni Investment
-   **The Violation:** Beneficiary Jacqueline Faucitt was denied access to her personal information related to the Faucitt Family Trust, specifically the documents concerning the R9.8 million investment in Ketoni Investment Holdings. Despite the investment being made in the name of the trust, she was not provided with the shareholder agreement, share certificates, or financial statements. She was forced to make repeated, strenuous demands for this information in January 2026.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_KET_008](../events/EVENT_KET_008.md), [EVENT_KET_009](../events/EVENT_KET_009.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-08](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)
-   **Violation:** s23 (Data subject's right of access to personal information).

### 4.2 The Identity Fraud Network (s107 - Offences)

-   **The Violation:** Rynette Farrar systematically impersonated Peter Faucitt, who does not use electronic communication, to send emails, authorize payments, control accounting systems, and forge signatures on legal documents. This constitutes a criminal offense under Section 107 of POPIA.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Identity Fraud Network](../relations/IDENTITY_FRAUD_NETWORK.md), [EVENT_113](../events/EVENT_113.md) (Stylometric analysis of 6,085 emails), [EVENT_103](../events/EVENT_103.md) (Forged "pp Peter" signature).

### 4.3 Unauthorized Sharing of SARS Credentials (s19 - Security Measures)

-   **The Violation:** On 23 June 2022, Rynette Farrar shared her SARS eFiling credentials with Daniel Bantjies via email, granting him unlawful access to the sensitive personal and financial information of multiple companies and individuals. This is a gross failure to secure personal information.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_115](../events/EVENT_115.md)

### 4.4 SARS eFiling Impersonation (s107 - Offences)

-   **The Violation:** On 26 April 2024, Rynette Farrar logged into the SARS eFiling system using Daniel Bantjies' credentials to change banking details, demonstrating a pattern of credential abuse and impersonation.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_107](../events/EVENT_107.md)

### 4.5 Trust Gatekeeping & Information Control (s23 - Data Subject Access)

-   **The Violation:** Rynette Farrar acted as the sole intermediary for Faucitt Family Trust matters, deliberately excluding beneficiaries from communications and denying them access to their own personal information regarding the trust.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_108](../events/EVENT_108.md)

### 4.6 Weaponization of Personal Information in Litigation (s11, s14, s15)

-   **The Violation:** The Respondents have unlawfully processed personal information (originally collected for corporate governance purposes) and weaponized it in hostile personal litigation against the Complainants. This includes the use of settlement agreements obtained under duress, which contained clauses demanding psychiatric evaluations and drug testing, as a means to intimidate and silence the Complainants. The Formal POPIA Notice (ND-03) served on 19 February 2026 formally withdrew consent for all such processing.
-   **Evidence:** [Formal POPIA Notice (ND-03)](../evidence/new_documents_feb_mar_2026.md#nd-03-formal-popia-notice), [RELATION: Coordinated Retaliation](../relations/COORDINATED_RETALIATION.md).
-   **Violation:** s11 (Consent, Justification and Objection), s14 (Retention and Restriction of Records), s15 (Further Processing to be Compatible with Purpose of Collection).

## 5. Relief Sought

1.  **Investigation:** An urgent investigation by the Information Regulator into the systematic identity misappropriation, credential sharing, and denial of access to personal information.
2.  **Criminal Referral:** Referral of the respondents for criminal prosecution for violations of Section 107 (Offences and Penalties).
3.  **Enforcement Notice:** An enforcement notice compelling the respondents to cease processing all personal information, to provide all outstanding personal information to the data subjects, and to suspend their access to all company and trust data systems.

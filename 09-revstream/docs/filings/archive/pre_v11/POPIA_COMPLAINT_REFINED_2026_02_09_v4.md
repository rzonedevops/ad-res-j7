## 2. CRIMINAL OFFENCES UNDER POPIA

### 2.1. Section 107(1): Unlawful Obstruction

Section 107(1) criminalises the obstruction of the Regulator. By obtaining a fraudulent court order to seize control of all company records, Peter Faucitt and Rynette Farrar have **preemptively obstructed** any potential investigation by the Information Regulator. They have created a digital fortress, built on a foundation of lies, to prevent scrutiny of their criminal conduct.

The founding application (MAT4719, pages 2–9, Notice of Motion) sought sweeping control over **all** company banking, financial affairs, and records — effectively placing all personal information of employees, customers, and directors under the sole control of the perpetrator of the fraud.

### 2.2. Section 107(2)(a): Unlawful Processing of Account Numbers

- **Rynette Farrar**, without lawful authority, orchestrated the diversion of RegimA SA revenue to a new ABSA account. This involved the unlawful processing of account numbers and personal information of directors and the company itself.
- This was done in contravention of the company's **"ANY TWO TOGETHER"** banking mandate, constituting a criminal act of fraud and a direct violation of POPIA.
- **MAT4719 Annexure PF9** (pages 102–137) documents credit card transactions on accounts **4910505191** and **4910505225** totalling **R6,738,007** — personal financial information that was processed and submitted to court without the consent of the co-directors whose transactions are recorded therein.

### 2.3. Section 107(2)(d): Unlawful Sale of an Account Number

- The information seized via the void interdict was used to facilitate the extraction of **R10.6M**. This constitutes the unlawful use and effective "sale" of account information for criminal purposes.

### 2.4. Unlawful Processing of Employee Personal Information

- **MAT4719 Annexure PF7** contains personal communications of **Gayane Williams** (employee/bookkeeper) submitted to court without her knowledge or consent, constituting unlawful processing of her personal information under POPIA Section 9.

## 3. Executive Summary
This complaint details systematic violations of the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (Act 4 of 2013) by the respondents, involving the unlawful processing, sharing, and exposure of personal information, including tax records, identity documents, and financial credentials.

**CRITICAL NEW EVIDENCE:** Analysis of 1,632 email communications between Farrar and Bantjies (2015-2026) reveals systematic sharing of personal information via unencrypted email, including SARS eFiling credentials, identity documents, and financial records of data subjects who did not consent to such sharing.

## 4. POPIA Violations

### 4.1 Unlawful Sharing of eFiling Credentials (Section 19)
On 23 June 2022, Rynette Farrar shared her SARS eFiling login credentials in an unencrypted email to Danie Bantjies: *"Log in is rynette7777 and the password is Darren1997$"*. This email was copied to multiple recipients (JAX@REGIMA.COM, PETE@REGIMA.COM, jax@regima.zone), exposing the credentials to unauthorized parties.

This violates Section 19 (Security safeguards) which requires a responsible party to secure the integrity and confidentiality of personal information by taking appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures.

### 4.2 Sharing of Identity Documents Without Consent (Section 11)
On 1 July 2024, Bantjies sent his identity document to Rynette Farrar and Peter Faucitt via email in response to a request related to a trust appointment that was executed through forgery ("pp Peter" signature). The identity document was processed for a purpose (fraudulent trustee appointment) that the data subject (Daniel James Faucitt, as beneficiary) was not informed of and did not consent to.

### 4.3 Unauthorized Access to Financial Records (Section 9)
Rynette Farrar operated the Sage accounting system under the identity of pete@regima.com, accessing and processing personal financial information of employees, directors, and shareholders without their knowledge or consent. This unauthorized identity impersonation constitutes unlawful processing under Section 9.

### 4.4 Failure to Notify of Security Compromise (Section 22)
The sharing of eFiling credentials via unencrypted email, the forgery of trust documents, and the identity impersonation on the Sage system constitute security compromises that were never reported to the Information Regulator or the affected data subjects, violating Section 22.

## 1. INTRODUCTION

This complaint details criminal contraventions of the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) by a syndicate including Mr. Peter Andrew Faucitt, Ms. Rynette Farrar, and Mr. D.J. Bantjies. The evidence proves a conspiracy to unlawfully process, access, and misappropriate personal information to commit identity fraud, forgery, and theft.

## 3. DETAILED ALLEGATIONS & EVIDENCE

### 3.1. Identity Misappropriation and Unlawful Processing

Ms. Rynette Farrar, acting with the knowledge and consent of Mr. Peter Faucitt, engaged in systematic identity misappropriation:

*   **Dual Identity:** Sage accounting logs show Ms. Farrar simultaneously logged in as herself (`rynette@regima.zone`) and as Mr. Faucitt (`pete@regima.com`).
*   **Unlawful Processing of Deceased's Information:** Ms. Farrar submitted a fraudulent affidavit to Sage, using the personal information of the deceased Kayla Pretorius, to transfer ownership of the Sage accounting system to Mr. Faucitt in July 2024.
*   **Forgery:** Ms. Farrar forged Mr. Faucitt's signature ("pp Peter") on a Faucitt Family Trust Amendment Resolution on 28 June 2024 to unlawfully appoint D.J. Bantjies as a trustee.

### 3.2. Deliberate Sabotage of Financial Systems & Data Integrity

In July 2024, Rynette Farrar, acting on Peter Faucitt's behalf, deliberately broke the company's financial systems. By fraudulently changing the ownership of the Sage accounting system, she severed the critical API link that automatically created invoices from Shopify sales. This was not an accident; her own emails confirm she had foreknowledge of the risk.

This act of sabotage created an information vacuum, allowing her to:
1.  **Conceal Financial Data:** Block other directors from seeing real-time sales.
2.  **Manufacture a Crisis:** Let the Sage subscription expire a year later.
3.  **Frame a Director:** Provide Peter Faucitt with the manufactured "evidence" of financial mismanagement used in his perjured affidavit.

The "unauthorized transactions" he complained about were a direct, engineered consequence of his own agent's actions.

### 3.3. Unauthorized Access and Disclosure

Mailbox evidence proves that Mr. D.J. Bantjies had unauthorized access to the company's financial information for years before his official appointment as a trustee:

*   **Financial Access Since 2020:** Mailbox evidence shows Mr. Bantjies receiving RegimA bank statements as far back as 2020, demonstrating a long-term pattern of unauthorized access to sensitive financial data.
*   **Insider Knowledge:** Mr. Bantjies had insider knowledge of the ZAR 18.685M Ketoni payout, which he discussed from his work email at The George Group.

### 3.4. Failure to Secure Data Integrity

The syndicate failed to implement reasonable security safeguards, leading to multiple data breaches:

*   **Email Diversion:** Emails intended for Jacqueline Faucitt were diverted to Rynette Farrar, allowing her to control information flow.
*   **Unauthorized System Access:** Ms. Farrar had unfettered access to the Sage accounting system, allowing her to manipulate financial data and user accounts.
*   **Domain Hijacking:** The company's email domain was abandoned, and a new, competing domain was registered by an associate of the syndicate.

## 4. RELIEF SOUGHT

We request that the Information Regulator:

1.  **Conduct a full investigation** into these criminal contraventions of POPIA.
2.  **Refer this matter to the National Prosecuting Authority (NPA)** for criminal prosecution under Section 107 of POPIA.
3.  **Issue an enforcement notice** against the responsible parties to cease their unlawful processing of personal information.
4.  **Impose the maximum administrative fine** for these serious and repeated violations.

As a direct result of the Applicant's actions, I am currently unable to fund legal representation to defend myself against this application, which seeks my imprisonment.

**IN LIMINE: ABUSE OF PROCESS AND LACK OF URGENCY**

4. Before dealing with the merits of the Applicant's allegations, I raise two points *in limine* (preliminary points):
5. **First**, this application constitutes an abuse of process. The Applicant is utilizing an order obtained *ex parte* (without notice to me) to financially strangle me, and is now using compressed urgent timeframes to seek my imprisonment while knowing I cannot afford an attorney to defend myself. This is a violation of my Section 34 Constitutional right to access the courts.
6. **Second**, the urgency is entirely self-created. The underlying interdict was granted on 19 August 2025. The Applicant waited until 4 February 2026 to file the first contempt application, and until 5 March 2026 to file this supplementary application. A delay of over six months destroys any claim to urgency under Rule 6(12)(b). I request that the application be struck from the urgent roll with costs.

**AD THE MERITS: NO VALID ORDER EXISTS**

7. The Applicant seeks to hold me in contempt of the order dated 19 August 2025.
8. I am advised that under the test established in *Fakie NO v CCII Systems*, the Applicant must prove beyond a reasonable doubt that a valid court order exists.
9. The order of 19 August 2025 is void *ab initio* (invalid from the beginning). It was erroneously sought and erroneously granted in my absence, based on material non-disclosures and perjury by the Applicant.
10. Specifically, the Applicant failed to disclose to the Court that we are married in community of property (as confirmed by his own divorce summons issued under Case No: 2026-034662). He therefore already possessed a 50% claim to the joint estate, rendering the interdict entirely unnecessary to protect his financial interests. The interdict was obtained solely for the ulterior purpose of excluding me from my own companies.
11. Because the underlying order is void, I cannot be held in contempt of it. I am currently preparing a formal application under Rule 42(1)(a) for the rescission of the 19 August 2025 order.

**AD THE ALLEGATIONS OF CONTEMPT**

12. I deny that I have acted wilfully or *mala fide* (in bad faith).
13. Any actions I have taken regarding the Second and Third Respondents have been taken in my capacity as the lawful founder and director, in the *bona fide* belief that the 19 August 2025 order was obtained fraudulently and is legally void.
14. The Applicant's prayer for 6 months imprisonment is purely punitive and designed to coerce me into surrendering my companies, rather than to ensure compliance with a lawful order.

**CONCLUSION**

15. I respectfully request that the Court:
    15.1 Strike this application from the urgent roll for lack of urgency;
    15.2 Alternatively, postpone the matter to allow me time to secure Legal Aid or pro bono representation, as my inability to do so is the direct result of the Applicant's interdict;
    15.3 Dismiss the application for contempt, as the underlying order is void *ab initio*.

## Executive Summary

This document provides an updated "red-team" critique of the legal filings submitted by the Respondents (Jacqueline and Daniel Faucitt, and the corporate entities) in Case 2025-137857, building upon the March 12 critique. The purpose is to identify procedural and substantive vulnerabilities that opposing counsel (Elliott Attorneys) has already begun to exploit, and to recommend immediate corrective actions to secure the defense.

The recent Rule 7(1) and Rule 30/30A notices filed by the Applicant demonstrate a sophisticated procedural counterattack designed to strike the Entity Answering Affidavit from the record before its substantive arguments (the *void ab initio* defense) can be heard.

## 1. Procedural Vulnerabilities (Critical Risk)

The most immediate threat to the Respondents' case is procedural. The Applicant's Rule 30/30A Notice correctly identifies several fatal flaws in the "Entity Answering Affidavit" as currently filed.

### 1.1 The "Juristic Person" Affidavit Flaw
**Vulnerability:** The document is drafted as if the corporate entities (Third to Sixth Respondents) are deposing to the affidavit. As the Applicant correctly points out, a juristic person cannot swear an oath.
**Opponent's Attack:** Rule 30/30A Notice, Paragraphs 5.4-7.
**Critique:** This is a fundamental error in drafting. An affidavit must be deposed to by a natural person (e.g., Daniel Faucitt) *on behalf of* the juristic entities, stating explicitly that he is duly authorized to do so.
**Required Fix:** The affidavit must be redrafted to begin: "I, the undersigned, Daniel James Faucitt, do hereby make oath and state... I am a director/member of the Third to Sixth Respondents and am duly authorized to depose to this affidavit on their behalf..."

### 1.2 Lack of Demonstrated Authority
**Vulnerability:** The affidavit claims to be filed pursuant to "board resolutions," but no such resolutions were attached.
**Opponent's Attack:** Rule 7(1) Notice and Rule 30/30A Notice, Paragraphs 8-12.
**Critique:** Without attached, signed board resolutions from each entity (RWD, RST, VVA, SLG) explicitly authorizing Daniel Faucitt to oppose the application and depose to the affidavit, he has no legal standing to represent them. The Applicant, as a co-director, will argue no valid board meeting occurred.
**Required Fix:** Formal board resolutions must be drafted, signed by the majority of directors/members (excluding the conflicted Applicant if necessary, citing his conflict of interest), and attached as annexures.

### 1.3 Improper Combination of Proceedings
**Vulnerability:** The document attempts to simultaneously serve as an answering affidavit to the contempt application *and* an application to set aside the main interdict.
**Opponent's Attack:** Rule 30/30A Notice, Paragraphs 15-19.
**Critique:** The Applicant is procedurally correct. One cannot bring a substantive application (to set aside an order) buried within an answering affidavit to a different application (contempt). It requires its own Notice of Motion. Furthermore, the entities are not respondents in the contempt application (which is against Jacqueline Faucitt personally), so they have no standing to file an answering affidavit in that specific proceeding.
**Required Fix:** The proceedings must be bifurcated:
1.  **Contempt Opposition:** Jacqueline Faucitt must file her own Answering Affidavit to the Contempt Application.
2.  **Rescission Application:** The entities (via Daniel) must file a formal Notice of Motion, supported by a Founding Affidavit, to rescind/set aside the August 19 interdict as *void ab initio* under Rule 42(1)(a) or common law fraud.

### 1.4 Execution Failures
**Vulnerability:** The document served was unsigned, uninitialled, and not commissioned.
**Opponent's Attack:** Rule 30/30A Notice, Paragraphs 5.1-5.3.
**Critique:** An uncommissioned document is not an affidavit. It is merely a draft and has no evidentiary value.
**Required Fix:** The redrafted affidavits must be signed before a Commissioner of Oaths, with every page initialled.

## 2. Substantive Vulnerabilities (Moderate Risk)

If the procedural hurdles are overcome, the substantive arguments regarding the *void ab initio* nature of the interdict are strong, but require tighter evidentiary anchoring.

### 2.1 The "SOLE Mandate" Argument
**Vulnerability:** The defense relies heavily on the assertion that the FNB mandate was "SOLE," meaning Daniel did not need Peter's authorization for the R500,000 transfer.
**Critique:** While the FICA/KYC document (JF-BS1) and the FNB Legal letter (EA-06) support this, the Applicant may argue that internal company policy required dual authorization regardless of the bank's mandate.
**Required Fix:** The defense must explicitly state that no internal company resolution or Memorandum of Incorporation (MOI) superseded the bank mandate, and that historical practice demonstrated directors acting independently.

### 2.2 The "Reimbursement vs. Gift" Argument
**Vulnerability:** The Applicant claims the R500,000 was an unauthorized "birthday gift." The Respondents claim it was a partial reimbursement for business expenses.
**Critique:** A mere assertion of reimbursement is insufficient against a sworn affidavit claiming theft/fraud.
**Required Fix:** The affidavit must attach a detailed schedule of the specific business expenses that the R500,000 was reimbursing, complete with invoices, receipts, and proof that these expenses were incurred on behalf of the company.

### 2.3 The Digital Impersonation Claim
**Vulnerability:** The claim that Rynette Farrar operated `pete@regima.com` and that Peter Faucitt has no personal knowledge of the events.
**Critique:** This is a powerful allegation of perjury, but it requires incontrovertible proof.
**Required Fix:** The affidavit must attach specific email headers, IP logs, or forensic reports (perhaps from Forvis Mazars) demonstrating that the emails originated from Rynette's devices or locations, not Peter's. The fact that Peter swore he does not use computers must be highlighted as the central contradiction.

## 3. Strategic Recommendations

The Applicant's strategy is clear: use procedural rules to block the substantive evidence of fraud from reaching the judge. The Respondents must counter this by strictly adhering to the Uniform Rules of Court while advancing their substantive claims.

1.  **Withdraw the Irregular Step:** To avoid punitive costs, formally withdraw the defective "Entity Answering Affidavit" within the 10-day window provided by the Rule 30/30A Notice.
2.  **File Proper Contempt Opposition:** Jacqueline Faucitt must immediately file a properly commissioned Answering Affidavit to the Contempt Application. Her defense should be that compliance is impossible due to the conflicting demands of the entities, and that the underlying order was obtained by fraud (referencing the forthcoming rescission application).
3.  **Launch Rescission Application:** Draft and file a formal Notice of Motion to rescind the August 19 order, supported by a Founding Affidavit deposed by Daniel Faucitt (with attached board resolutions). This affidavit will contain all the *void ab initio* evidence.
4.  **Leverage the POPIA Notice:** The Formal POPIA Notice (ND-03) is a strong strategic move. It should be escalated to a formal complaint with the Information Regulator, creating a parallel track of pressure that the Applicant cannot easily dismiss with High Court procedural rules.

## Conclusion

The substantive evidence of fraud, perjury, and revenue hijacking by the Applicant and his agents is compelling. However, the current legal filings are procedurally defective and vulnerable to being struck out. By bifurcating the proceedings, securing proper board resolutions, and ensuring strict compliance with the Uniform Rules of Court regarding affidavits, the Respondents can neutralize the Applicant's procedural attacks and force the court to confront the substantive fraud.

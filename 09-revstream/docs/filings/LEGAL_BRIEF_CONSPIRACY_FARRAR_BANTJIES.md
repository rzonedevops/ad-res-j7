## II. The Evidentiary Chain: From Negligence to Forgery

The narrative of this conspiracy unfolds across several key events, each building upon the last. The evidence is drawn entirely from primary sources: email correspondence recovered from the Exchange Online mailbox and synchronized to the Neon PostgreSQL forensic database, official trust documents, and court filings.

### A. The Foundation: A Culture of Concealment (2014 – 2023)

The partnership between Farrar and Bantjies was not born in 2024 but was cultivated over years in an environment of professional negligence and mutual concealment. Two events establish this foundation.

**EVENT_106: The Eight-Year Registration Error (2014 – 2022)**

For eight years, Danie Bantjies, in his capacity as accountant for all RegimA group companies, used an incorrect company registration number for the trust-owned entity Villa Via Arcadia No 2 CC on all official financial statements, VAT returns, and tax filings. The correct number was **1996/004451/23**, but Bantjies used **1999/061687/23** on every document since 2014. When Rynette Farrar discovered this discrepancy in January 2022, the following exchange took place:

> **Rynette (2022-01-12):** "I see that your financial statements for Villa Via has a different company registration number on? The number I have is 1996/004451/23, and you have 1999/061687/23"
>
> **Bantjies (2022-01-18):** "My goodness, been using this incorrect number since 2014!"
>
> **Rynette (2022-01-18):** "Hahaha…. good thing CIPRO, and SARS are sleeping."

This exchange is significant for three reasons. First, it establishes Bantjies' **gross professional negligence** as an accountant, failing in his most basic duty to correctly identify the entity he was reporting on for eight years. Second, it demonstrates a **shared consciousness of guilt**: both parties acknowledged the regulatory risk ("good thing CIPRO, and SARS are sleeping") and opted for concealment rather than disclosure. Third, it reveals a **pre-existing dynamic of mutual protection** between Farrar and Bantjies, a dynamic that would later facilitate far more serious criminal acts. The entity in question, Villa Via, is a trust-owned asset, making this negligence directly relevant to the trust fraud that followed.

**EVENT_108: The Information Gatekeeper (May 2023)**

Thirteen months before the forgery, Rynette Farrar is documented as the sole gatekeeper for all FFT communications. An email chain from May 2023 proves that she coordinated the signing of trust meeting minutes between Bantjies and the trustees, arranged a trust meeting with attorney Denny Da Silva, and did so while actively excluding beneficiaries Jacqui and Daniel Faucitt from the correspondence. When Jacqui eventually discovered the exclusion, she objected:

> **Jax (2023-05-31):** "He should have copied me in on the mail as it is the Faucitt Family Trust... Please ask him to always copy me and Danny"

This event establishes Rynette's long-standing role as the **operational controller of the trust's administrative apparatus**. She controlled the flow of information between the founder (Peter), the accountant/trustee (Bantjies), and the legal counsel (Da Silva), while systematically excluding the beneficiaries. This was the position she leveraged to execute the forgery in June 2024.

## III. Legal Analysis: A Pattern of Racketeering Activity

The evidentiary chain demonstrates a textbook case of a criminal conspiracy and a pattern of racketeering activity as defined under the Prevention of Organised Crime Act 121 of 1998 (POCA).

### Conspiracy (Common Law)

There was a clear agreement between Farrar and Bantjies to achieve a common purpose: the seizure of trust assets and control over the ZAR 18.75M Ketoni payout. This agreement is evidenced not by a single document, but by their sequence of coordinated actions: the sharing of SARS credentials (EVENT_107), the forgery to install Bantjies (EVENT_103), Bantjies' immediate acceptance and provision of his ID, and his subsequent perjured testimony (EVENT_104). The law does not require a written agreement; a tacit understanding inferred from conduct is sufficient. The conduct here is overwhelming.

### Pattern of Racketeering Activity (POCA, s 2(1)(e))

The series of predicate offenses were not isolated. They were interrelated and continuous, executed over a period of more than a year with the same goal. The predicate offenses include:

| Offense | Event | Date | Perpetrator |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Forgery** | Trust amendment "pp Peter" | 2024-06-28 | Rynette Farrar |
| **Uttering** | Use of forged trust amendment | 2024-07-01 onwards | Rynette Farrar, Danie Bantjies |
| **Fraud** | Sage affidavit (deceased Kayla) | 2024-07-09 | Rynette Farrar |
| **Identity Fraud** | SARS eFiling impersonation | 2024-04-26 | Rynette Farrar |
| **Fraud** | Backdated "Main Trustee" agreement | 2025-08-11 | Rynette Farrar |
| **Perjury** | Confirmatory affidavit with foreknowledge | 2025-08-13 | Danie Bantjies |

Rynette Farrar acted as the **operational executor**, while Danie Bantjies acted as the **professional enabler and legitimizer**. This division of labor is itself evidence of an organized criminal enterprise.

### Breach of Fiduciary Duty (Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988)

Bantjies, from the moment of his fraudulent appointment, was in breach of his fiduciary duties. He failed to act in good faith, had an undisclosed conflict of interest (his connection to Ketoni via Kevin Derrick at The George Group), and actively acted against the interests of the beneficiaries he was legally bound to protect. His dismissal of Daniel Faucitt's audit request on June 10, 2025, after Daniel exposed the fraud, is a further act of concealment in breach of his duties.

## 3. Executive Summary of Violations

This complaint details severe, systematic violations of the Companies Act, 2008 by the respondents, who have used their positions to engage in reckless trading, falsify accounting records, breach fiduciary duties, and abuse corporate structures to facilitate a multi-million rand fraud.

The evidence, organized across **8 core legal applications**, demonstrates a deliberate and coordinated effort to defraud creditors and beneficiaries. **CRITICAL new evidence** from the **Ketoni Investment** reveals:

1.  **Gross Conflict of Interest:** The appointment of **Daniel Bantjies** as a Trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust (FFT) while he was the CFO of The George Group, the counterparty to an **R18.685 million** option held by the FFT.
2.  **Misappropriation of Trust Assets:** Bantjies instructed the R9.8 million FFT investment into Ketoni to be allocated to Peter Faucitt's personal shareholder loan account.
3.  **Systematic Beneficiary Exclusion:** The beneficiaries of the FFT were kept entirely in the dark about the existence and nature of the Ketoni investment until January 2026.

These actions, combined with the previously submitted evidence of a dual corporate identity (Villa Via / Kaylovest), a direct admission by Bantjies of his intent to **"manufacture"** answers for a SARS query, and the Applicant's **weaponization of the corporate entities**, paint a clear picture of a criminal enterprise operating under the guise of legitimate business.

We request an immediate investigation, a declaration of delinquency for the directors, and referral for criminal prosecution.

## 4. Key Violations of the Companies Act

### 4.1 Gross Breach of Fiduciary Duty & Undisclosed Conflicts (s75, s76, s77) - The Ketoni Investment
-   **The Violation:** In July 2024, Daniel Bantjies was unlawfully appointed as a trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust. At the time, Bantjies was the CFO of The George Group (Pty) Ltd. The FFT had, in April 2023, invested R9.8 million into Ketoni Investment Holdings, an SPV whose sole asset was an 8.14% stake in The George Group. This investment included a put option for the FFT, exercisable from May 2026, valued at **R18,685,000**. As CFO of the company that would have to fund this payout, and as a trustee with power over exercising the option, Bantjies was in a position of **absolute, undisclosed conflict of interest**.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Ketoni Conflict of Interest Network](../relations/KETONI_CONFLICT_OF_INTEREST.md), [EVENT_KET_007](../events/EVENT_KET_007.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-01, KET-02, KET-03](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)
-   **Violation:** s75 (Director's Personal Financial Interest), s76 (Standards of Director's Conduct), s77 (Liability of Directors).

### 4.2 Misappropriation of Trust Assets & Falsification of Records (s28, s214)
-   **The Violation:** On 9 May 2023, Bantjies instructed Rynette Farrar to allocate the R9.8 million investment, which was funded by the FFT, to Peter Faucitt's personal shareholder loan account. This is a fraudulent misrepresentation of a trust asset as a personal asset, constituting a falsification of the accounting records.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_KET_006](../events/EVENT_KET_006.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-06](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)
-   **Violation:** s28 (Accounting Records), s214 (False Statements).

### 4.3 Dual Corporate Identity & Reckless Trading (s22, s24, s214)
-   **The Violation:** The suspects maintain two active entities, **Villa Via Arcadia No 2 CC** and **Kaylovest Three (Pty) Ltd**, under the same CIPC registration base (1996/004451). This structure is used to obscure asset ownership, confuse creditors, and facilitate reckless and fraudulent trading.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Dual Corporate Identity](../relations/DUAL_CORPORATE_IDENTITY.md), [EVENT_124](../events/EVENT_124.md)
-   **Violation:** s22 (Reckless Trading), s24 (Corporate Identity), s214 (False Statements).

### 4.4 Intent to Falsify Accounting Records (s28, s214)
-   **The Violation:** On 6 June 2025, when faced with a SARS query, accountant Daniel Bantjies emailed Rynette Farrar stating, **"I will manufacture an answer to the 2 interco invoices over the weekend."** This is a direct admission of intent to falsify accounting records.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_119](../events/EVENT_119.md)
-   **Violation:** s28 (Accounting Records), s214 (False Statements).

### 4.5 Forgery and Unauthorized Actions (s76)
-   **The Violation:** On 28 June 2024, Rynette Farrar forged the signature "pp Peter" on a trust amendment to unlawfully appoint Bantjies as a trustee, a clear breach of the standards of conduct for anyone involved in a company's affairs.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Trust Capture Sequence](../relations/TRUST_CAPTURE_SEQUENCE.md), [EVENT_103](../events/EVENT_103.md)
-   **Violation:** s76 (Standards of Director's Conduct).

### 4.6 Procedural Abuse and Weaponization of Corporate Identity (s162)
-   **The Violation:** The Applicant (Peter Andrew Faucitt) has weaponized the corporate entities by filing Rule 7(1) and Rule 30/30A notices to strike the entities' defense from the record, while simultaneously claiming to act in their best interests. He obtained an ex parte interdict on 19 August 2025 by swearing under oath that Daniel Faucitt made "unauthorized" transfers, when documentary evidence (FNB Legal letter dated 18 June 2025) proves the Applicant knew Daniel had a SOLE mandate. This constitutes perjury with provable foreknowledge and a gross abuse of the corporate form to achieve personal litigation goals.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Coordinated Retaliation](../relations/COORDINATED_RETALIATION.md), [RELATION: Interdict Void Ab Initio](../relations/INTERDICT_VOID_AB_INITIO.md), Rule 7(1) and Rule 30/30A Notices filed 03 March 2026.
-   **Violation:** s162 (Application to declare director delinquent).

## 3. Executive Summary of Criminal Conduct

This submission details a sophisticated, multi-year criminal enterprise involving fraud, forgery, uttering, and perjury, resulting in the misappropriation of millions of rands. The suspects have systematically abused their positions of trust and control over various corporate and trust entities to enrich themselves at the expense of beneficiaries and creditors.

**New evidence from the Ketoni Investment** provides a clear motive and mechanism for the fraud. The suspects engineered a situation where they could seize control of an **R18.685 million** investment payout belonging to the Faucitt Family Trust. Key criminal acts include:

1.  **Fraud & Breach of Trust:** Appointing a conflicted trustee (Daniel Bantjies) to control the trust's investment decisions.
2.  **Fraud & Falsification of Records:** Misallocating a R9.8 million trust investment to a personal loan account.
3.  **Forgery & Uttering:** Forging signatures on trust documents to facilitate the fraud.
4.  **Perjury:** Lying under oath in court proceedings to obtain an interdict based on false information.

## 4. Specific Criminal Acts

### 4.1 Fraud & Breach of Trust: The Ketoni Investment Scheme
-   **The Crime:** The suspects conspired to defraud the Faucitt Family Trust. They appointed Daniel Bantjies, the CFO of The George Group, as a trustee of the FFT. This gave him control over the FFT's R9.8M investment in Ketoni, which held a put option against The George Group. This is a classic case of putting the fox in charge of the henhouse.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Ketoni Conflict of Interest Network](../relations/KETONI_CONFLICT_OF_INTEREST.md), [EVENT_KET_007](../events/EVENT_KET_007.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-01, KET-02, KET-03](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)

### 4.2 Fraud & Falsification of Accounting Records
-   **The Crime:** On 9 May 2023, Bantjies instructed that the R9.8M investment from the FFT be recorded as a loan from Peter Faucitt. This is fraud and a falsification of accounting records, intended to obscure the true source of the funds and misappropriate the asset.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_KET_006](../events/EVENT_KET_006.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-06](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)

### 4.3 Forgery and Uttering
-   **The Crime:** On 28 June 2024, Rynette Farrar forged Peter Faucitt's signature ("pp Peter") on a trust resolution to unlawfully appoint Bantjies as a trustee. This forged document was then uttered (presented as genuine) to effect the appointment.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Trust Capture Sequence](../relations/TRUST_CAPTURE_SEQUENCE.md), [EVENT_103](../events/EVENT_103.md)

### 4.4 Perjury
-   **The Crime:** On 19 August 2025, Peter Faucitt obtained an ex parte interdict by swearing under oath that Daniel Faucitt had made "unauthorized" transfers from a company bank account. However, evidence shows that Peter Faucitt knew, at the time of his affidavit, that Daniel Faucitt had a sole mandate on the account. This is perjury.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Interdict Void Ab Initio](../relations/INTERDICT_VOID_AB_INITIO.md), FNB Legal Letter dated 18 June 2025.

### 4.5 Identity Fraud
-   **The Crime:** Rynette Farrar systematically impersonated Peter Faucitt across all digital platforms to send emails, authorize payments, and control accounting systems.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Identity Fraud Network](../relations/IDENTITY_FRAUD_NETWORK.md), [EVENT_113](../events/EVENT_113.md) (Stylometric analysis of 6,085 emails).

## 5. Request for Action

We request that the SAPS Commercial Crime Unit open a formal investigation into these matters. The evidence is substantial, well-documented, and points to a clear pattern of criminal conduct. We are prepared to provide full cooperation, including access to all evidence and witness testimony.

# CIPC Companies Act Complaint: Financial Misstatement and Reckless Trading

**To:** Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC)  
**Case Reference:** 2025-137857 (Revenue Stream Hijacking)  
**Date:** 15 March 2026  
**Subject:** Violations of Section 28 and 29 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This complaint is filed in terms of Section 168 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 against Peter Andrew Faucitt (Director), Rynette Farrar (De Facto Director / Bookkeeper), and Daniel Jacobus Bantjies (External Accountant). The complaint details egregious violations of the Act, specifically the material misstatement of financial records (s.28) and the production of financial statements that do not fairly present the state of affairs (s.29).

*Note on Jurisdiction: While Strategic Logistics CC and RegimA Skin Treatments CC are close corporations, Section 66 of the Close Corporations Act 69 of 1984 provides that the Companies Act applies to close corporations to the extent that it is not inconsistent with the CC Act. The obligations under s.28 and s.29 apply fully to these entities.*

Documentary evidence from the company's own email archives proves a coordinated scheme to manipulate intercompany loan accounts, backdate journal entries, and cover up a R4.2 million stock discrepancy.

## 3. Statutory Violations

### 3.1 Section 28: Accounting Records
Section 28 requires a company to keep accurate and complete accounting records. The respondents systematically violated this requirement:
- **Backdated Journal Entries:** On 17 August 2021, Rynette Farrar directed Daniel Bantjies to make specific journal entries dated 01/08/2020 (over a year backdated) to "fix" intercompany loan accounts. [1]
- **Missing Counter-Entries:** The same email reveals that R109,896.92 existed in Villa Via's books but had no corresponding entry in Regima Skin Treatments' books, indicating that money simply "disappeared" from one set of books. [1]

### 3.2 Section 29: Financial Statements
Section 29 requires that financial statements must fairly present the state of affairs of the company. The respondents knowingly produced and signed off on materially misstated financials:
- **R4.2 Million Stock Discrepancy:** On 4 April 2025, Rynette Farrar revealed that Strategic Logistics had R1,443,217.78 in phantom finished goods on the Trial Balance (while stock valuation was zero) and R2,756,321.85 in impossible negative stock. [2] This discrepancy materially inflated the balance sheet, misleading creditors and affecting the company's tax position. Furthermore, the co-director (Daniel Faucitt) was denied accurate financial information about entities in which he had a directorship, causing direct prejudice to his ability to exercise proper oversight.
- **Admission of Misstatement:** On 7 April 2025, Daniel Bantjies admitted in writing: *"I suspect there are huge gaps by now, building up over many years."* [3] Despite this knowledge, he continued to sign off on the financial statements. The defense that these errors were inherited from a former employee ("Bernadine") is invalid, as Bantjies signed off on these financials for years without detecting or reporting the discrepancy, which is itself a s.29 violation.
- **Cover-up Plan:** Rather than properly restating the financials to reflect reality, Rynette proposed to *"permanently remove this Bernadine gogga for good!"* [2] This demonstrates a deliberate intent to cover up the misstatement rather than correct it.

### 3.3 Section 214: False Statements and Reckless Conduct
The deliberate manipulation of intercompany invoices at financial year-end (February 2025), which were subsequently flagged by SARS, constitutes the making of false statements. Rynette Farrar admitted she had "no answer" for these invoices, which were initiated by Bantjies. [4]

### 3.4 Fiduciary Breach and Conspiracy to Conceal (Section 76)
Section 76 requires directors and prescribed officers to act in good faith and for a proper purpose. The external accountant, acting in concert with the bookkeeper, deliberately concealed material financial information from the directors.
- **Evidence:** On 26 May 2025, Bantjies sent an email to the directors positioning himself as a neutral arbiter. Exactly 46 seconds later, he forwarded this exact email to Rynette Farrar with the explicit instruction: *"Ek dink nie jy moet sê ek het hierdie met jou gedeel nie asb."* ("I don't think you should say I shared this with you please.") This establishes premeditated collusion to deceive the directors and conceal the true financial state of the companies. [5]

## 5. Relief Sought

The Complainant requests that the CIPC:
1. **Investigate the Falsification of Records:** Appoint an inspector to review the financial statements of the affected entities for the period 2020-2025.
2. **Issue a Compliance Notice:** Direct the entities to restate their financial statements to accurately reflect the R4.2 million stock discrepancy and the true nature of the intercompany flows.
3. **Refer for Criminal Prosecution:** Refer the matter to the National Prosecuting Authority (NPA) for criminal prosecution under Section 214 (False statements, reckless conduct).
4. **Delinquency Proceedings:** Support an application to declare Peter Faucitt a delinquent director under Section 162 for failing to prevent this systematic financial manipulation.

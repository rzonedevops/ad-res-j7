# Alternative Strategy: The Invalidity of the Backdated Notices

**Case No:** 2025-137857
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Prepared By:** Manus AI Legal Assessment Team

## Executive Summary

This document outlines an aggressive alternative strategy to counter the Applicant's procedural attack. Instead of seeking condonation for the Respondents' defective Entity Answering Affidavit, this strategy asserts that the Applicant's Rule 7(1) and Rule 30/30A notices are **legally invalid and non-existent** due to their impossible dating (03 February 2026). 

Consequently, the Respondents argue that no valid opposition to the Entity Answering Affidavit has been filed, and the substantive evidence of fraud (*void ab initio*) stands unchallenged before the court.

---

## 1. The Temporal Impossibility

The Applicant's attorneys, Elliott Attorneys, served two notices on 03 March 2026:
1. A Rule 7(1) Notice disputing authority.
2. A Rule 30/30A Notice alleging the Entity Answering Affidavit is an irregular step.

Both documents are explicitly dated and signed: **"03 DAY OF FEBRUARY 2026"**.

The Entity Answering Affidavit, which both notices purport to attack, was served on **19 February 2026**.

---

## 2. The Legal Argument for Invalidity

The Respondents' argument is not merely that the notices contain a typographical error, but that the error renders the notices legally incapable of fulfilling their statutory function under the Uniform Rules of Court.

### 2.1 The 10-Day Cure Period is a Nullity
Rule 30(2)(b) requires the complaining party to afford their opponent "an opportunity of removing the cause of complaint within ten days." 

If a formal court notice is dated 03 February 2026, the 10-day period runs from that date. Therefore, the deadline to cure the alleged defects expired on approximately **13 February 2026**. 

It is a legal and logical impossibility to demand that a party cure defects in a document (served 19 February) by a deadline that expired six days before the document was even created. The notice therefore fails to provide the mandatory 10-day cure period required by Rule 30(2)(b), rendering it fatally defective.

### 2.2 A Document Cannot Attack the Future
A legal notice signed on 03 February 2026 cannot validly reference, critique, or attack an event that occurred on 19 February 2026. On the face of the documents, they are attacking a non-existent phantom. 

While the Applicant may argue this is a mere clerical error ("February" instead of "March"), the court cannot simply rewrite the Applicant's formal notices for them, especially when the Applicant is simultaneously demanding surgical procedural perfection from the Respondents.

### 2.3 The Consequence: No Valid Opposition
Because the Rule 30/30A notice fails to provide a valid, prospective 10-day cure period, it is not a valid notice under the Rules. 

Therefore, the Applicant has failed to take a valid procedural step to strike the Entity Answering Affidavit. The Entity Answering Affidavit, despite its own formal defects, remains on the record unchallenged by any valid procedural mechanism.

---

## 3. Strategic Deployment: The "Catch-22"

This argument creates a powerful "Catch-22" for the Applicant and the Court:

**Scenario A: Strict Compliance (The Applicant's preferred standard)**
If the court applies strict procedural compliance, the Applicant's notices are invalid because they contain impossible dates and fail to provide a valid 10-day cure period. The notices are struck out, and the Entity Answering Affidavit stands.

**Scenario B: Substance Over Form (The Condonation standard)**
If the court decides to overlook the Applicant's dating error as a mere matter of form, it must apply the exact same standard to the Respondents. The court must overlook the formal defects in the Entity Answering Affidavit (unsigned, juristic person drafting) and proceed to the substance: the evidence of perjury and fraud.

In either scenario, the Applicant's attempt to use procedural rules as a shield to prevent the court from seeing the substantive evidence of fraud is defeated.

---

## 4. Recommended Action Plan

If this aggressive strategy is adopted, the Respondents should take the following steps:

1. **Ignore the 10-Day Deadline:** Do not withdraw the Entity Answering Affidavit. The deadline of 13 March 2026 (calculated from the actual service date of 03 March) is legally irrelevant because the notice itself is invalid.
2. **File a Rule 30 Notice against the Applicant:** Serve a Rule 30 notice on Elliott Attorneys, stating that their notices dated 03 February 2026 constitute an irregular step because they purport to attack a document that did not exist at the time of signing, and fail to provide a valid cure period.
3. **Force the Merits:** In the answering affidavit to the Contempt Application, explicitly state: *"The Applicant has failed to file any valid opposition to the Entity Answering Affidavit. The Applicant's purported Rule 30 notice is dated 03 February 2026, rendering it a temporal impossibility and a legal nullity. Consequently, the substantive evidence that the underlying interdict was obtained through perjury stands unchallenged."*

## Conclusion

By attacking the validity of the backdated notices, the Respondents flip the procedural script. The Applicant is forced to either defend the impossible dating of their own documents or concede that procedural rules should not prevent the court from hearing the substantive truth.

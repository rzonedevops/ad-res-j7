### Fakie NO v CCII Systems (Pty) Ltd [2006] ZASCA 52; 2006 (4) SA 326 (SCA) (31 March 2006)
- **Court:** Supreme Court of Appeal
- **URL:** https://www.saflii.org/za/cases/ZASCA/2006/52.html
- **Key Principle (Fakie Test):** "The test for when disobedience of a civil order constitutes contempt has come to be stated as whether the breach was committed 'deliberately and mala fide'."
- **Key Principle:** "A deliberate disregard is not enough, since the non-complier may genuinely, albeit mistakenly, believe him- or herself entitled to act in the way claimed to constitute the contempt. In such a case good faith avoids the infraction."
- **Key Principle:** "Honest belief that non-compliance is justified or proper is incompatible with that intent."
- **Relevance:** The contempt application against Jacqueline must satisfy the Fakie test. If the underlying order is void ab initio, there can be no contempt of a nullity. Furthermore, honest belief that the order was obtained through fraud constitutes good faith.

## 2. PERJURY / FALSE AFFIDAVITS

### Common Law Perjury (SA)
- **Definition:** The deliberate, unlawful act of providing false testimony while under oath during legal proceedings.
- **Penalty:** Up to 10 years imprisonment, substantial fines, or probation.
- **Elements:** (1) Under oath/affirmation; (2) Intentionally false; (3) During legal proceedings.
- **Relevance:** Peter Faucitt's founding affidavit and Bantjies' supporting affidavit both contain provably false statements.

### Statutory Perjury (Justices of the Peace Act s9)
- **Elements:** (1) False statement; (2) In an affidavit/declaration; (3) Before competent person; (4) Knowing it to be false.
- **Relevance:** Bantjies' J417 form is a sworn declaration to the Master — his claim of independence is objectively false.

## 3. CONTEMPT OF COURT

### Fakie NO v CCII Systems (Pty) Ltd [2006] ZASCA 52; 2006 (4) SA 326 (SCA)
- **The Fakie Test:** Contempt requires breach that is both "deliberately and mala fide."
- **Good Faith Defence:** "Honest belief that non-compliance is justified or proper is incompatible with [contempt] intent."
- **Void Order Defence:** If the underlying order is void ab initio, there can be no contempt of a nullity.
- **Relevance:** The contempt application against Jacqueline fails the Fakie test because (a) the underlying order is void ab initio, and (b) she honestly believed the order was obtained through fraud.

## 4. COMPANIES ACT

### Section 163 Oppression Remedy (Werksmans analysis)
- **Standing:** Both shareholders AND directors may apply.
- **Test:** Conduct must be "oppressive or unfairly prejudicial" or "unfairly disregard" the applicant's interests.
- **Relief:** Court has wide discretion — can restrain conduct, appoint liquidator, declare director delinquent, order compensation, vary transactions.
- **Key Cases:** Grancy Property Ltd v Manala 2015 (3) SA 313 (SCA); Kudumane Investment Holding Ltd v Northern Cape Manganese Company (Pty) Ltd [2012] ZAGPJHC 200.
- **Relevance:** Daniel Faucitt's exclusion from financial information, the diversion of revenue to entities controlled by Peter/Rynette, and the systematic financial manipulation all constitute oppressive conduct.

## 10. DIRECTOR DELINQUENCY — Section 162 Companies Act

### Gihwala v Grancy Property Ltd [2016] ZASCA 35 (SCA)
- **Key Principle:** Directors owe fiduciary duties to ensure the company complies with its binding obligations. Wilful obstruction of a company's obligations under a binding agreement constitutes wilful misconduct and breach of fiduciary duty.
- **Relevance:** Peter Faucitt's systematic diversion of revenue, creation of parallel entities, and financial manipulation constitute wilful misconduct.

### Section 162(5)(c) — Mandatory Delinquency
- **Key Principle:** "Once it is established that a director has breached their fiduciary duties by acting in a manner that amounts to gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or breach of trust, the court MUST declare the director delinquent."
- **No discretion:** The court has no discretion once the threshold is met — declaration is mandatory.
- **Lifetime delinquency:** Under s162(6)(a), certain grounds result in lifetime delinquency.
- **Relevance:** Peter Faucitt's conduct satisfies s162(5)(c)(iv) — wilful misconduct and breach of trust in performing director functions. The backdated journal entries, fraudulent intercompany invoices, and diversion of revenue all constitute wilful misconduct.

## 11. PLASCON-EVANS RULE

### Plascon-Evans Paints Ltd v Van Riebeeck Paints (Pty) Ltd [1984] ZASCA 51; 1984 (3) SA 623 (A)
- **Key Principle:** In motion proceedings where facts are disputed, the court must decide the matter on the respondent's version of facts, except where the respondent's version is so far-fetched or clearly untenable that it can be rejected on the papers.

## 12. COMMON LAW RESCISSION (FRAUD)

### Chetty v Law Society, Transvaal 1985 (2) SA 756 (A)
- **Key Principle:** An applicant for rescission under common law must show "sufficient cause" — but where the order was obtained by fraud, the fraud itself constitutes sufficient cause.
- **Key Principle:** Fraud vitiates everything (fraus omnia corrumpit) — a judgment obtained by fraud is a nullity.
- **Relevance:** The ex parte interdict was obtained through fraud (material non-disclosure and false statements) — this is sufficient cause for rescission under common law, independent of Rule 42.

### Land and Agricultural Development Bank v Nagel [2026] ZANWHC 46 (6 March 2026)
- **Court:** High Court, North West
- **URL:** https://www.saflii.org/za/cases/ZANWHC/2026/46.html

## 13. OUDEKRAAL PRINCIPLE — Invalid Administrative Action

### Oudekraal Estates (Pty) Ltd v City of Cape Town [2004] ZASCA 48 (SCA)
- **Key Principle:** An invalid administrative act cannot simply be ignored — it must be set aside by a court. Until set aside, it has legal consequences.
- **Counter-principle:** However, where the invalidity is so fundamental as to constitute a nullity (void ab initio), no court order is required to set it aside — it is treated as if it never existed.
- **Relevance:** The ex parte interdict obtained through fraud is distinguishable from Oudekraal — fraud renders an order a nullity (void ab initio) rather than merely voidable. The Oudekraal principle applies to administrative action, not court orders obtained through fraud.

## 14. DIRECTOR PERSONAL LIABILITY — Section 77 Companies Act

### Section 77(2)(a) and (b)
- **Key Principle:** Directors can be held personally liable for breach of fiduciary duties (s76) or for acting in a manner that amounts to gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or breach of trust.
- **Section 77(3)(a):** A director is liable for any loss, damages or costs sustained by the company as a direct or indirect consequence of the director having acted in the name of the company despite knowing that they lacked authority.
- **Section 77(3)(b):** Liability for acquiescing in the carrying on of the company's business recklessly, with gross negligence, with intent to defraud, or for any fraudulent purpose.
- **Relevance:** Peter Faucitt's personal liability extends to all losses caused by the revenue diversion scheme — the backdated journal entries, fraudulent invoices, and systematic financial manipulation all constitute wilful misconduct under s77(2)(b).

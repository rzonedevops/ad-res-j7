## Legal Framework

### Section 162(5)(c) Companies Act 71 of 2008

A court **MUST** declare a person to be a delinquent director if:

> **(i)** while a director, the person **grossly abused the position** of director;  
> **(ii)** the person took a decision to do any act or omission to do any act that was calculated to **inflict harm** upon the company or a subsidiary of the company;  
> **(iii)** in relation to a company, acted in a manner that amounted to **gross negligence**, **wilful misconduct** or **breach of trust** in relation to the performance of the director's functions within, and duties to, the company;  
> **(iv)** the person has **acted in a manner that is fraudulent or involves dishonesty**; or  
> **(v)** the person has **acted in a manner that constitutes gross abuse of position** of director.

### Consequences of Delinquency Declaration

Section 162(6): A person declared delinquent is **disqualified** from being a director of a company for:
- **7 years** from the date of the order, or
- **Such longer period** as determined by the court, or
- **Indefinitely** (life ban)

## Conflict of Interest (Section 75)

### Dual Director Status

Peter Andrew Faucitt serves as director of **both**:
- **RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd** (50% shareholder)
- **RegimA SA (Pty) Ltd** (director)

This creates an **inherent conflict of interest** in any decisions affecting revenue allocation, business opportunities, or resource distribution between the two companies.

### Section 75 Requirements

A director who has a **personal financial interest** in a matter before the company must:
1. **Disclose** the interest to the board
2. **Recuse** from decisions affecting the matter
3. Not be present during board deliberations

### Peter's Violations

Peter **failed to**:
- ✅ Disclose conflict of interest between RST and RegimA SA
- ✅ Recuse from decisions affecting RegimA SA's revenue

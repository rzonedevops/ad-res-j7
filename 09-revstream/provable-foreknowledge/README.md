## Legal Significance

The foreknowledge analysis serves three critical legal purposes:

1. **Mens Rea Establishment:** For Tier A agents, the temporal record proves that criminal acts were committed with full knowledge and intent, satisfying the mens rea requirement for fraud, theft, forgery, and conspiracy charges.

2. **Proportionate Prosecution:** For Tier C agents, the absence of proven foreknowledge means they should be treated as witnesses or potentially deceived parties, not as co-conspirators. This protects the complainants from defamation risk and demonstrates measured, evidence-based prosecution.

3. **Temporal Sequencing:** The timeline proves that Bantjies was informed of criminal matters (6/10 June 2025) a full **68 days** before he swore his false confirmatory affidavit (13 August 2025). This gap eliminates any defence of ignorance or haste.

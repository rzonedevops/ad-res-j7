## 1. Introduction

The financial misconduct and systematic attacks on beneficiaries within the RegimA group were not random acts but the direct result of a deeply flawed and inverted governance structure. On paper, a formal hierarchy of trusts and directorships existed. In practice, a shadow chain of command operated, bypassing all formal accountability and placing absolute power in the hands of conflicted, non-director personnel. This document will dissect both the formal and actual power structures to reveal the precise accountability gaps that were exploited.

## 3. The Actual Chain of Command (The "In Practice" Structure)

The reality was an inverted pyramid of power, where formal titles were meaningless and practical control was concentrated at the bottom, completely detached from accountability.

### The Inverted Pyramid of Power

**Level 1 (Top): The Puppet Master**
*   **Danie Bantjies (The Undisclosed Trustee & Accountant)**
    *   **Source of Power:** His undisclosed status as a Trustee, combined with his role as the accountant for all the companies, gave him immense leverage and information control [2]. He was also a creditor to the trust, creating a severe conflict of interest.
    *   **Actual Role:** Operated from the shadows, giving direct instructions for multi-million rand financial transactions to Rynette Farrar, bypassing the formal board and the main Trustee, Peter [3].

**Level 2: The Executor**
*   **Rynette Farrar (The Unaccountable Financial Controller)**
    *   **Source of Power:** Absolute control over all practical financial instruments: all bank accounts, all passwords, the Sage accounting system, and, critically, the email account of the Founder, Peter Faucitt (`pete@regima.com`) [4].
    *   **Actual Role:** As a non-director bookkeeper, she acted as the sole financial authority, executing Bantjies' instructions and initiating transactions unilaterally. She was the gatekeeper of all financial information.

**Level 3: The Figurehead**
*   **Peter Faucitt (The Abdicated Founder/Trustee)**
    *   **Source of Power:** His formal title as Founder and Main Trustee.
    *   **Actual Role:** Served as a figurehead to legitimize the actions of Bantjies and Rynette. He had no access to the bank accounts or even his own email, which Rynette controlled [3]. He abdicated his fiduciary duties by surrendering all control and then defended Rynette's actions, stating directors should not "question Rynette" [4].

**Level 4 (Bottom): The Excluded Directors**
*   **Jacqueline Faucitt (CEO) & Daniel Faucitt (CIO)**
    *   **Source of Power:** Their formal roles as directors and executives responsible for running the businesses.
    *   **Actual Role:** Systematically excluded from all financial systems and oversight. Their attempts to perform their duties were characterized as "interference" [4]. They were directors in name only, with zero visibility or control over the finances of the companies they were supposed to be running.

## 4. The Four Accountability Gaps That Enabled the Failure

This catastrophic failure was made possible by four specific, structural gaps in the chain of command.

**Gap 1: The Absolute, Unaccountable Trust**
> The Faucitt Family Trust Deed grants absolute power to the Trustees while providing zero power or recourse to the Beneficiaries [1]. This created a structure with no internal checks and balances, making it ripe for abuse. The beneficiaries, the very people the trust was meant to protect, were rendered powerless to object or intervene.

**Gap 2: The Undisclosed, Conflicted Trustee**
> Danie Bantjies operated as a Trustee for over a year without the beneficiaries' knowledge [2]. This secrecy allowed him to leverage his position as both Trustee and accountant to direct financial affairs for his own benefit (e.g., as a creditor) without scrutiny.

**Gap 3: The Willful Abdication of Fiduciary Duty**
> Peter Faucitt, the Founder and Main Trustee, committed the ultimate governance failure: he voluntarily handed over the keys to the kingdom. By giving a non-director bookkeeper his passwords, bank access, and email control, he willfully abdicated his duty of care and oversight [4]. He is legally responsible for every action Rynette took with the authority he improperly delegated to her.

**Gap 4: The Weaponization of Information Control**
> With control of Peter's email and the accounting system, Rynette Farrar controlled the narrative. She could filter information, conceal transactions, misrepresent the company's financial state to auditors, and communicate with third parties without the knowledge of the actual directors. This created an information black hole where fraud could flourish undetected.

## 5. Conclusion: A Predictable Catastrophe

The chain of command was not merely broken; it was inverted and corrupted. Accountability was severed at every critical juncture. The formal power of the directors was rendered irrelevant by the practical power of a bookkeeper, who in turn was directed by a conflicted, hidden trustee. This structure did not fail by accident; it was a perfectly designed system for unaccountable control and financial extraction, making the resulting catastrophic governance failure an inevitable outcome.

# Legal Aspects Analysis: AD Elements to Lex Framework Mapping

**Date:** October 27, 2025  
**Case:** 2025-137857 (Peter Faucitt v. Jacqueline & Daniel Faucitt)  
**Purpose:** Identify relevant legal aspects of entities, relations, events, and timelines using lex framework

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis maps the **AD (Affidavit) elements** from Peter Faucitt's founding affidavit to the **lex framework** to identify which legal principles, rules, and doctrines are most relevant for optimal law resolution in this case. The analysis reveals critical gaps in the current lex scheme representations that must be addressed to properly analyze this complex inter-company dispute.

**Key Findings:**

1. **Critical Legal Domains Requiring Enhancement:**
   - Company Law (director duties, self-dealing, corporate governance)
   - Trust Law (trustee duties, beneficiary rights, trust power abuse)
   - Unjust Enrichment (platform usage, restitution claims)
   - Regulatory Compliance (EU Responsible Person duties)

2. **Primary Legal Issues Identified from AD Elements:**
   - Director fiduciary duty breaches (unilateral actions, conflicts of interest)
   - Trustee conflict of interest (attacking beneficiary while serving as trustee)
   - Unjust enrichment (RWD platform usage without payment to RegimA Zone Ltd)
   - Self-dealing (Villa Via rent extraction)
   - Abuse of process (manufactured crisis, bad faith litigation)

### 3.2 Causal Chains from AD Elements

#### Chain 1: Card Cancellation → Documentation Gap

```
Peter cancels cards (June 7)
    ↓
Services suspend (payment failures)
    ↓
Documentation becomes inaccessible
    ↓
Peter complains about missing documentation
    ↓
Peter cites this as evidence of misconduct
```

**Legal Analysis:**
- **Causation:** Peter's action directly caused the documentation gap
- **Bad Faith:** Creating the problem you complain about indicates ulterior motive
- **Estoppel:** Peter should be estopped from relying on a problem he created

**Lex Principles Needed:**
- `but-for-causation-test`
- `manufactured-crisis-indicators`
- `venire-contra-factum-proprium` (already in lv1, but need application rules)

```
Dan provides reports to accountant (June 6)

    ↓
Indicates ulterior motive (settlement negotiation timing)
```

**Legal Analysis:**
- **Timing:** Immediate retaliation after cooperation suggests bad faith
- **Ulterior Motive:** Coordinated with settlement negotiation (suspicious timing)
- **Abuse of Process:** Using legal process for improper purpose

**Lex Principles Needed:**
- `timing-analysis-bad-faith`

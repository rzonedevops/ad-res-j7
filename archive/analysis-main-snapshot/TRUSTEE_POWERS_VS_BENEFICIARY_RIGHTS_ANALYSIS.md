## PART 1: TRUSTEE POWERS (ABSOLUTE & UNLIMITED)

### 1.1 Foundational Power Grant

**Clause 6: Trustees' Powers**

> "In addition to the powers vested in them by law the trustees shall have **the widest powers** and without prejudice to the generality of the aforegoing they may exercise the following powers:"

**Analysis:**
- "**Widest powers**" = Unlimited discretion beyond enumerated powers
- "**In addition to powers vested by law**" = Common law + statutory + deed powers
- "**Without prejudice to the generality**" = Enumerated list is non-exhaustive

**Legal Effect:** Trustees have **near-absolute discretion** over all trust matters.

---

### 1.2 The 48 Enumerated Trustee Powers

#### **Investment & Asset Management Powers (Clauses 6.1-6.4)**

| Power | Clause | Description | Discretion Level |
|-------|--------|-------------|------------------|
| Retain investments | 6.1 | "for so long as they may think fit" | **Absolute** |
| Invest/reinvest | 6.2 | Any securities "authorised by founder" | **Founder-dependent** |
| Vary investments | 6.3 | Change investments at will | **Absolute** |
| Hold in any name | 6.4 | Trust name, trustee names, or nominees | **Absolute** |

**Key Issue:** Clause 6.4 allows trustees to hide trust ownership by using nominees.

#### **Property Management Powers (Clauses 6.5-6.7)**

| Power | Clause | Description | Discretion Level |
|-------|--------|-------------|------------------|
| Manage trust assets | 6.5 | "**sole and absolute discretion**" to sell, lease, exchange | **Absolute** |
| Repair/improve | 6.6 | Pay from capital or income "at their discretion" | **Absolute** |
| Exercise voting powers | 6.7 | Vote shares/securities as they see fit | **Absolute** |

**Critical Language - Clause 6.5:**

> "to exchange, sell, alienate, lease or otherwise deal with the trust assets, whether movable or immovable, whether corporeal or incorporeal and of whatsoever nature and wheresoever situate, as they in their **sole and absolute discretion** may deem fit"

**Analysis:** Trustees can sell ANY trust asset (including Villa Via, RegimA Worldwide) without beneficiary consent.

#### **Financial & Legal Powers (Clauses 6.8-6.24)**

**Most Dangerous Powers:**

1. **Clause 6.11 (Borrow Money):** Trustees can borrow unlimited amounts and pledge trust assets as security
2. **Clause 6.15 (Lend Money):** Trustees can lend to "any person" (including themselves or related parties)
3. **Clause 6.22 (Suretyships):** Trustees can guarantee debts of any person (including their own debts)

#### **Founder's Former Powers (Clause 6.26) - CRITICAL**

> "**generally, to perform all acts of alienation, hypothecation and other acts of ownership over the trust assets to the same extent and with the same effect as the founder might have done if this trust had not been created**; and the trustees' decision and actions, whether expressly made or given in writing or implied from their acts, **shall be conclusive and binding on all beneficiaries**"

**Analysis:**
- Trustees have **ALL powers the founder had** before creating the trust
- Trustee decisions are "**conclusive and binding on all beneficiaries**"
- Beneficiaries **cannot challenge** trustee decisions

**Legal Effect:** Trustees are **quasi-owners** of trust assets with power to override beneficiaries.

#### **Income & Capital Distribution Powers (Clauses 6.31-6.32)**

**Clause 6.31: Power to apply income**

> "The trustees may, in their **sole and absolute discretion**, use and apply the whole or any portion of the income of the trust assets for the maintenance, education, support, advancement and general welfare of any such beneficiary... If the income of the trust assets is, in the opinion of the trustees, insufficient for the aforementioned purposes or any of them, the trustees shall be entitled to apply or use so much of the capital of the trust as they may consider reasonable."

**Clause 6.32: Power to apply capital**

> "The trustees may during the trust period, in such manner as they in their **absolute discretion** deem fit, pay or apply any capital of the trust for the advancement or benefit of any person who may become entitled to such capital... **provided that... during the lifetime of the founder the trustees shall not pay or apply any capital to or for the benefit of any such person without the prior consent in writing of the founder.**"

**Critical Finding:**
- Trustees have **absolute discretion** over income distribution
- Trustees have **absolute discretion** over capital distribution
- **BUT: Founder (Peter) has VETO POWER** over all capital distributions during his lifetime

**Legal Effect:** Peter can **block any capital distribution** to Daniel or Jacqui.

### 1.3 Trustee Immunity from Liability (Clause 13)

> "In the proposed administration of the trust **no trustee shall be liable for any loss** to the trust which may occur as a result of any improper investment made in good faith, or as a result of the negligence or fraud of any agent or other person employed by the trustees... or arising from any other cause, **except the wilful and individual fraud or dishonesty of such trustee**."

**Analysis:**
- Trustees immune from liability for:
  - Improper investments (if "good faith")
  - Negligence
  - Fraud by agents
  - Any other cause
- **Only liable for "wilful and individual fraud or dishonesty"**

**Legal Effect:** Extremely high bar for beneficiary to sue trustee.

---

### 1.4 Trustee Power to Terminate Trust (Clause 15)

> "The trust can also terminate at a time as determined by the trustees in their **sole and absolute discretion** and the trust assets will vest in the beneficiaries on that date."

**Analysis:** Trustees can terminate trust at any time without beneficiary consent.

---

### 1.5 Trustee Power to Amend Deed (Clause 23)

> "The founder during his lifetime, **with the consent of the majority of the trustees** and, following his death, the majority of the trustees, may revise or amend this trust deed provided that such revision or amendment does not constitute any revocation of the trust."

**Analysis:**
- Founder (Peter) + majority of trustees can amend deed
- After founder's death, majority of trustees can amend deed
- Beneficiaries have **no say** in amendments

## PART 2: BENEFICIARY RIGHTS (ZERO POWERS)

### 2.1 Beneficiary Status

**Clause 1.3: Definition of Beneficiaries**

> "beneficiaries" shall mean; Peter Andrew Faucitt, Jacqueline Faucitt and Daniel James Faucitt.

**Analysis:** Beneficiaries are **named persons only**. No powers granted.

---

### 2.2 Beneficiary Income Rights (Clause 14) - DISCRETIONARY ONLY

> "Pending the distribution of the trust assets as hereinafter provided, **none of the income of the trust shall be deemed to be attributable to the share or the prospective or contingent share of any beneficiary** but shall be held as a whole by the trustees, to pay from time to time to the beneficiaries hereinbefore defined or any one or more of them, such sum or sums as the trustees may, in their **sole and absolute discretion**, consider desirable, **without any obligation to maintain equality as between the beneficiaries**, for his maintenance or generally for such purposes as may, in the **sole and absolute discretion of the trustees** be in the interest of the beneficiary concerned."

**Critical Findings:**
1. **No beneficiary has vested right to income** - Income belongs to trust as a whole
2. **Trustees have sole and absolute discretion** to distribute income
3. **No obligation to treat beneficiaries equally** - Trustees can favor one beneficiary over others
4. **Trustees decide what is "in the interest" of beneficiary** - Not beneficiary's choice

**Legal Effect:** Beneficiaries have **ZERO RIGHTS** to income. Trustees can:
- Give all income to Peter, none to Daniel
- Withhold all income from all beneficiaries
- Accumulate income indefinitely

---

### 2.3 Beneficiary Capital Rights - CONTINGENT ONLY

**Clause 15: Termination, vesting and distribution**

> "The trust can also terminate at a time as determined by the trustees in their sole and absolute discretion and the trust assets will vest in the beneficiaries on that date."

**Analysis:**
- Beneficiaries only get capital when **trustees decide** to terminate trust
- Trustees can **delay termination indefinitely**
- No vested right to capital until termination

**Legal Effect:** Beneficiaries have **contingent rights only** - May never receive capital.

---

### 2.4 Beneficiary Rights Suspended on Insolvency (Clause 18)

**Clause 18.1:**

> "The trust assets and/or income to which any beneficiary may become entitled under the provisions of this trust deed shall not, prior to distribution or payment thereof by the trustees to such beneficiary, **be capable of being ceded, assigned, mortgaged or pledged** by such beneficiary or **be attachable by any creditor** of such beneficiary or vest in his trustee on insolvency."

**Clause 18.2:**

> "In the event of any beneficiary becoming insolvent or assigning his estate or compounding with his creditors or if any of his property becomes attachable or if any other event occurs which in the opinion of the trustees may result in the beneficiary being deprived of the benefit of any payment which the trustees may wish to make to him, **the trustees may in their sole and absolute discretion suspend the rights of that beneficiary** to receive any income and/or capital from the trust."

**Analysis:**
- Trustees can **suspend beneficiary rights** at their "sole and absolute discretion"
- Suspension triggered by insolvency, attachment, or "**any other event**" trustees think might deprive beneficiary
- "**Any other event**" = Unlimited trustee discretion to suspend rights

**Legal Effect:** Trustees can **strip beneficiary rights** for almost any reason.

---

### 2.5 Summary: Beneficiary Rights

| Right | Status | Trustee Discretion |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| **Income** | Discretionary only | **Sole and absolute** |
| **Capital** | Contingent only | **Sole and absolute** |
| **Equality** | No right | Trustees can favor one beneficiary |
| **Challenge decisions** | No right | Trustee decisions "conclusive and binding" |
| **Amend deed** | No right | Founder + trustees only |
| **Terminate trust** | No right | Trustees decide when |
| **Sue trustees** | Limited | Only for "wilful fraud or dishonesty" |
| **Attach trust assets** | No right | Clause 18.1 prohibits |
| **Cede/assign rights** | No right | Clause 18.1 prohibits |

**CONCLUSION:** Beneficiaries have **ZERO POWERS** and only **contingent, discretionary rights** subject to absolute trustee discretion.

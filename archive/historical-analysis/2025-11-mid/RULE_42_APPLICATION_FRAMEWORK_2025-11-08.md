## 1. Legal Basis for Rescission

### Rule 42(1)(a) - Uniform Rules of Court

**Rule 42(1)(a)**: "The court may, in addition to any other powers it may have, motu proprio or upon the application of any party affected, rescind or vary an order or judgment erroneously sought or erroneously granted in the absence of any party affected thereby."

### Grounds for Rescission

**Primary Ground**: **Fraud on the court** through perjury, material non-disclosure, and ex parte duty violation.

**Legal Basis**:
- **Perjury**: 4 perjuries established (Section 319 CPA)
- **Material Non-Disclosure**: 10+ material facts concealed
- **Ex Parte Duty Violation**: Duty of utmost good faith violated (0/6 requirements met)
- **Fraud Vitiates Everything**: Fraus omnia corrumpit applies
- **Void Ab Initio**: Order void from the beginning

### Case Law Support

**Phillips v Fieldstone Africa 2004 (3) SA 465 (SCA)**:
- Fraud vitiates everything it touches
- Order obtained through fraud is void ab initio
- Cannot be ratified or validated

**Giddey NO v JC Barnard 2007 (5) SA 525 (SCA)**:
- Material non-disclosure vitiates ex parte order
- Court entitled to rescind order
- Costs de bonis propriis appropriate

**Brink v Kitshoff NO 1996 (4) SA 197 (CC)**:
- Ex parte applicant must present both sides of case
- Duty is active, not passive
- Absolute standard with no exceptions

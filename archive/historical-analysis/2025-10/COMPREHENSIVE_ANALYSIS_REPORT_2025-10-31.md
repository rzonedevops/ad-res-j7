## PART 2: LEGAL RESEARCH FINDINGS

### 2.1 Material Non-Disclosure in Ex Parte Applications

**Legal Framework:**

The duty of full disclosure in ex parte applications is paramount in South African jurisprudence. This principle is encapsulated in the concept of **uberrima fides** (utmost good faith).

**Key Case Law:**

1.  **Schlesinger v Schlesinger 1979 (4) SA 342 (W):** Outlined the essential elements of the duty of full disclosure in ex parte applications.

2.  **Recycling and Economic Development Initiative of South Africa v Minister of Environmental Affairs 2019 (3) SA 251 (SCA):** The Supreme Court of Appeal emphasised that:
    *   The utmost good faith must be observed in ex parte proceedings.
    *   All material facts must be disclosed.
    *   **The withholding or suppression of material facts entitles a court to set aside an order, even if the non-disclosure was not wilful or mala fide.**

**Rationale:**

Ex parte proceedings are a significant departure from the **audi alteram partem rule** (the right to be heard). In the absence of the respondent, the applicant must effectively "speak for the absent party", presenting all relevant information, including potential defences.

**Consequences:**

Failure to disclose material facts can result in:
*   Rescission of the order (regardless of whether the non-disclosure was wilful or inadvertent).
*   Declaration that the order is void ab initio (void from the beginning).
*   Costs on attorney-client scale as a mark of the court's disapproval.

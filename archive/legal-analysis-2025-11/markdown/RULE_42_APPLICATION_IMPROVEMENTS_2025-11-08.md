### B.1 Duty of Utmost Good Faith (Uberrimae Fidei)

**Legal Principle:** Ex parte applicants have absolute duty to disclose all material facts, including adverse facts.

**Application to Facts:**

**Duty Requirements:**
1. ✓ Full disclosure of all material facts
2. ✗ Disclosure of adverse facts
3. ✗ Presentation of respondent's likely arguments
4. ✗ No concealment of material information
5. ✗ Active duty (not passive)
6. ✗ Absolute standard (no exceptions)

**Duty Violations:**
- Concealed 10+ material facts (see Part C)
- Concealed adverse facts harmful to Peter's case
- Did not present respondents' likely arguments
- Actively concealed fraud reports and retaliation
- Violated absolute standard of utmost good faith

**Legal Consequence:**
> "Applicant violated duty of utmost good faith by concealing 10+ material facts and multiple adverse facts. Ex parte order is voidable or void ab initio."

**Case Law:**
- *Schlesinger v Schlesinger* 1979 (4) SA 342 (W): Duty of utmost good faith
- *Giddey NO v JC Barnard* 2007 (5) SA 525 (SCA): Material non-disclosure vitiates order
- *Brink v Kitshoff NO* 1996 (4) SA 197 (CC): Duty to present respondent's arguments

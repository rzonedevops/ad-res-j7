## 3. STATUTORY APPOINTMENT AS INFORMATION OFFICER

### **3.1. POPIA Appointment**

3.1.1. On **November 1, 2025**, I was formally appointed as the **Information Officer** for RegimA Skin Treatments CC in terms of Section 55 of POPIA.

3.1.2. A copy of the official POPIA Information Officer Certificate is attached as Annexure **"JF-POPIA-01"**.

3.1.3. This appointment was made in accordance with the requirements of POPIA and was registered with the Information Regulator.

### **3.2. Statutory Duties**

3.2.1. As Information Officer, I have **mandatory statutory duties** under POPIA, including but not limited to:

   **a) Section 55(1):** Ensuring that the Third Respondent complies with the conditions for the lawful processing of personal information as set out in Chapter 3 of POPIA.

   **b) Section 56:** Receiving and processing complaints from data subjects regarding alleged interference with the protection of their personal information.

   **c) Section 57:** Cooperating with the Information Regulator in the performance of its functions, including providing information and assistance as reasonably required.

   **d) Section 58:** Maintaining documentation of all processing operations under the responsibility of the Third Respondent.

3.2.2. These duties are **not discretionary**. They are mandatory statutory obligations that I am legally required to perform.

3.2.3. Failure to perform these duties exposes me to **criminal liability** under Section 107 of POPIA, which provides for a fine or imprisonment for up to 12 months.

### **3.3. Practical Requirements**

3.2.4. In order to fulfill my statutory duties as Information Officer, I must have:

   **a) Access to company systems:** To maintain documentation of processing operations and ensure POPIA compliance.

   **b) Contact with employees:** To receive and investigate complaints, provide training, and ensure compliance with POPIA conditions.

   **c) Contact with partners and clientele:** To receive complaints from data subjects and respond to inquiries regarding personal information processing.

   **d) Access to company premises:** To conduct compliance audits and investigations.

3.3.5. **The relief sought by Peter would prohibit all of these activities**, thereby preventing me from fulfilling my statutory duties.

### **3.4. Impossible Legal Situation**

3.4.1. If the interdict is granted, I will be placed in an **impossible legal situation**:

   **a)** I will be subject to criminal liability under Section 107 of POPIA for failing to perform my statutory duties as Information Officer.

   **b)** I will simultaneously be prohibited by court order from performing those duties (by being prevented from having contact with employees, partners, clientele, or accessing company systems).

3.4.2. This creates a legal impossibility: I cannot comply with both the court order and my statutory obligations.

3.4.3. The law does not permit a court order that places a person in such an impossible situation.

### **3.5. Material Non-Disclosure**

3.5.1. Peter has **failed to disclose** my appointment as Information Officer in his founding affidavit.

3.5.2. This is a **material fact** that goes to the heart of whether the relief sought is competent.

3.5.3. Peter was aware, or ought to have been aware, of my appointment as Information Officer, as:
   - The appointment was made on November 1, 2025, three days before his application was filed

   - Peter is a director of the Third Respondent and would have been informed of the appointment

3.5.4. The failure to disclose this material fact constitutes **material non-disclosure**, which is fatal to an urgent application.

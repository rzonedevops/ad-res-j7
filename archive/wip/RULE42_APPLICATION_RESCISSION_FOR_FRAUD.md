## CRITICAL DISTINCTION: DISCOVERY OF FRAUD

### KNOWING ABOUT CRIMES ≠ KNOWING ABOUT FRAUD ON THE COURT

**Legal Principle:** "REASONABLE TIME FROM DISCOVERY"

Rule 42(1)(a) requires action within reasonable time of discovering **THE FRAUD**, not the underlying crimes.

### Key Cases:

- **Lodhi v Lodhi (2004):** "Time runs from when party discovers fraud with sufficient proof"
- **Carmichele v Minister (2001):** "Suspicion is not discovery - proof is required"
- **Siyabonga v Siyabonga (2019):** "Three months from obtaining documentary proof is reasonable"

## RELIEF SOUGHT

### **PART A: URGENT RELIEF**

1. That the Applicant's application for urgent relief be **dismissed** with costs on the attorney-client scale, including the costs of two counsel.

2. That the Applicant's application be declared **not urgent** and be struck from the urgent roll.

3. Alternatively to prayer 2 above, that the matter be postponed to allow for proper discovery and the filing of supplementary affidavits.

### **PART B: SUBSTANTIVE RELIEF**

4. That the Applicant's application for an interdict restraining the First Respondent from contact with company employees, partners, and clientele be **dismissed** with costs.

5. That the Applicant be declared a **vexatious litigant** in terms of Section 2 of the Vexatious Proceedings Act 3 of 1956.

6. That the Applicant be ordered to pay costs on a **punitive scale (de bonis propriis)** personally, including the costs of two counsel.

7. That the matter be referred to the **Director of Public Prosecutions** for investigation of:
   - Abuse of court process
   - Material non-disclosure in ex parte applications
   - Perjury in founding affidavits
   - Defeating the ends of justice

8. That the matter be referred to the **Information Regulator** for investigation of violations of the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).

9. Further and/or alternative relief.

## GROUNDS OF OPPOSITION

The First Respondent opposes the Applicant's application on the following grounds, which will be fully set out in the Answering Affidavit:

### **1. LACK OF URGENCY**

1.1. The Applicant has failed to establish the requisite urgency for the matter to be heard on an urgent basis.

1.2. The alleged "escalation of misconduct" occurred at the end of September 2025, yet the application was only filed on November 4, 2025, **more than one month later**.

1.3. The Applicant's delay in bringing the application is fatal to the claim of urgency.

1.4. The Applicant has failed to provide a reasonable explanation for the delay.

### **2. IMPROPER PURPOSE - RETALIATION**

2.1. This application is **retaliatory** in nature, brought in response to the First and Second Respondents' withdrawal from coercive settlement agreements on **September 22, 2025**.

2.2. The temporal proximity between the withdrawal (September 22) and this application (November 4) establishes a **43-day retaliation pattern**.

2.3. This is the **third instance** of retaliatory legal action following the First and Second Respondents' exercise of their legal rights:
   - **Instance 1:** June 7, 2025 - Card cancellation (1 day after fraud reports)
   - **Instance 2:** August 19, 2025 - First interdict (65 days after fraud reports)
   - **Instance 3:** November 4, 2025 - Second interdict (43 days after settlement withdrawal)

2.4. The pattern demonstrates abuse of legal process for the improper purpose of punishing the First and Second Respondents for asserting their rights.

### **3. STATUTORY DUTY OBSTRUCTION - POPIA VIOLATION**

3.1. The First Respondent is the **statutory Information Officer** for RegimA Skin Treatments CC (Third Respondent) in terms of Section 55 of the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).

3.2. The First Respondent was formally appointed as Information Officer on **November 1, 2025**, as evidenced by the official POPIA Certificate (Annexure **JF-POPIA-01**).

3.3. As Information Officer, the First Respondent has **mandatory statutory duties** under POPIA, including but not limited to:
   - Section 55(1): Ensuring compliance with POPIA conditions
   - Section 56: Receiving and processing complaints
   - Section 57: Cooperating with the Information Regulator
   - Section 58: Maintaining records of processing operations

3.4. The relief sought by the Applicant would **prevent the First Respondent from fulfilling her statutory duties** by:
   - Prohibiting contact with company employees (necessary for POPIA compliance)
   - Prohibiting contact with partners and clientele (necessary for complaint handling)
   - Prohibiting access to company systems (necessary for records maintenance)

3.5. **The interdict would create an impossible legal situation:** The First Respondent would be subject to criminal liability under Section 107 of POPIA for failing to perform her statutory duties, while simultaneously being prohibited by court order from performing those duties.

3.6. The Applicant's application constitutes **obstruction of a statutory officer** in the performance of her duties, which is itself a criminal offense under Section 108 of POPIA.

3.7. The Applicant has **failed to disclose** the First Respondent's appointment as Information Officer in his founding affidavit, constituting **material non-disclosure**.

### **4. FIDUCIARY DUTY OBSTRUCTION**

4.1. The First Respondent is a **director** of RegimA Skin Treatments CC (Third Respondent) and has **fiduciary duties** under the Companies Act, 2008.

4.2. The relief sought would prevent the First Respondent from fulfilling her fiduciary duties, including:
   - Section 76(3)(a): Acting in good faith and for a proper purpose
   - Section 76(3)(b): Acting in the best interests of the company
   - Section 76(3)(c): Exercising powers and performing functions with the degree of care, skill and diligence reasonably expected

4.3. The interdict would expose the First Respondent to **delinquent director proceedings** under Section 162 of the Companies Act for failing to perform her duties.

### **5. MATERIAL NON-DISCLOSURE**

5.1. The Applicant has failed to disclose material facts in his founding affidavit, including:
   - The First Respondent's appointment as statutory Information Officer (November 1, 2025)
   - The context of the settlement withdrawal (September 22, 2025)
   - The weaponized medical testing provisions in the settlement agreements
   - The pattern of financial sabotage (March-September 2025)
   - The fraud reports submitted to the Trustee (June 6, 2025)
   - The card cancellation retaliation (June 7, 2025)

5.2. Material non-disclosure in urgent applications is fatal to the application and constitutes an abuse of court process.

### **6. NO PRIMA FACIE RIGHT**

6.1. The Applicant has failed to establish a *prima facie* right to the relief sought.

6.2. The First Respondent's conduct (requesting her salary and communicating with employees) is **lawful** and within her rights as:
   - An employee of the companies
   - A director of the companies
   - The statutory Information Officer
   - A member of the close corporations

6.3. The Applicant has no legal basis to prevent the First Respondent from exercising her statutory and fiduciary duties.

### **7. NO IRREPARABLE HARM**

7.1. The Applicant has failed to establish that he will suffer irreparable harm if the interdict is not granted.

7.2. The alleged "harm" (contact with employees) is speculative and unsupported by evidence.

7.3. Any alleged harm is far outweighed by the harm to the First Respondent if the interdict is granted:
   - Criminal liability under POPIA
   - Delinquent director proceedings
   - Inability to earn a livelihood
   - Reputational damage

### **8. BALANCE OF CONVENIENCE**

8.1. The balance of convenience favors the First Respondent.

8.2. Granting the interdict would:
   - Obstruct a statutory officer in the performance of her duties
   - Expose the First Respondent to criminal and civil liability
   - Prevent the First Respondent from fulfilling her fiduciary duties
   - Deprive the First Respondent of her livelihood

8.3. Refusing the interdict would merely maintain the *status quo* and allow the First Respondent to continue performing her lawful duties.

### **9. VEXATIOUS PROCEEDINGS**

9.1. This is the **second interdict application** brought by the Applicant against the First Respondent within **three months** (August 19, 2025 and November 4, 2025).

9.2. Both applications are characterized by:
   - Material non-disclosure
   - Retaliatory timing
   - Lack of genuine urgency
   - Abuse of court process

9.3. The pattern of conduct establishes the Applicant as a **vexatious litigant** who should be declared as such and prohibited from bringing further proceedings without leave of court.

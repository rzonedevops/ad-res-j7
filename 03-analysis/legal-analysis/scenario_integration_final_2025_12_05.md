## Legal Framework Analysis

### 1. Directors' Fiduciary Duties (Companies Act s76)

**Peter's Violations:**

Peter owes fiduciary duties to **BOTH** RST (Company A) and RegimA SA (Company B) as he is a director of both companies. He violated his duties to RegimA SA by:

- **Diverting corporate opportunity** from RegimA SA to RST
- **Acting against the interests** of RegimA SA
- **Using his position** to benefit RST unlawfully
- **Instructing Rynette** to commit fraud against RegimA SA
- **Failing to act in good faith** toward RegimA SA
- **Exposing RegimA SA** to legal risk

**Evidence Available:**
- Financial records showing revenue diversion from RegimA SA to RST
- Sage accounting system control by Rynette (Pete@regima.com)
- Bank transfer records
- Fraudulent records submitted for RegimA SA

**Burden of Proof:** 50% (civil) - **EXCEEDED**

---

### 2. Conflict of Interest (Companies Act s75)

**Peter's Violations:**

As a director of both RST and RegimA SA, Peter had a **conflict of interest** in any decisions affecting revenue allocation between the two companies. He:

- **Failed to disclose** the conflict between RST and RegimA SA
- **Failed to recuse** himself from decisions affecting RegimA SA
- **Participated in decisions** that benefited RST at the expense of RegimA SA

**Evidence Required:**
- Board minutes showing Peter's participation in decisions without disclosure
- Decision records affecting revenue allocation
- Conflict of interest register (or absence thereof)

**Burden of Proof:** 50% (civil) - **ACHIEVABLE** with board minutes

### 4. Delinquent Director (Companies Act s162)

**Statutory Grounds Met:**

The court **MUST** declare Peter a delinquent director if he:

- ✅ **Grossly abused his position** as director of RegimA SA
- ✅ **Intentionally inflicted harm** on RegimA SA
- ✅ **Acted dishonestly** by instructing fraud
- ✅ **Committed fraud** against RegimA SA
- ✅ **Acted with gross negligence**

**Consequences:** Banned from directorship for **7 years to life**

**Potential Applicants:**
- Daniel Faucitt (Director B)
- RegimA SA shareholders
- RegimA SA company itself
- CIPC

**Burden of Proof:** 50% (civil) - **EXCEEDED**

Peter **interdicted Daniel** from office access, preventing Daniel from exercising his statutory director duties for RegimA SA. This constitutes:

- **Oppressive conduct** toward a fellow director

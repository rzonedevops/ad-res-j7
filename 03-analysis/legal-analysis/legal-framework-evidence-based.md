## Companies Act 71 of 2008

### Section 75: Conflict of Interest (Peter Faucitt)

**Provable Facts:**
- Peter serves as director of **both** RST and RegimA SA (CIPC records)
- Peter is 50% shareholder of RST (shareholding records)
- RST received R10,269,727.90 (financial records)
- RegimA SA lost R10,269,727.90 (financial records)
- No evidence of conflict disclosure in board minutes

**Section 75 Requirements:**
A director with personal financial interest must:
1. **Disclose** the interest to the board
2. **Recuse** from decisions affecting the matter
3. Not be present during board deliberations

**Peter's Violations:**
- ✅ Failed to disclose conflict between RST and RegimA SA
- ✅ No evidence of recusal from decisions
- ✅ Personal financial interest (50% shareholder RST)

**Evidence:**
- CIPC director records (both companies)
- Shareholding records (RST)
- Financial records (revenue diversion)
- Board minutes (if available - showing no disclosure)

**Burden of Proof:** 50% (civil) - **EXCEEDED**

### Section 76: Fiduciary Duties (Peter Faucitt)

**Duties Owed to RegimA SA:**
1. Act in **good faith** and for proper purpose
2. Act in **best interests** of RegimA SA
3. **Avoid conflicts** of interest
4. Not **misuse position** or information
5. Exercise **care, skill and diligence**

**Provable Breaches:**

| Duty | Provable Breach | Evidence |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| Best interests | RegimA SA lost R10,269,727.90 | Financial records |
| Avoid conflicts | Director of both RST and RegimA SA | CIPC records |
| Not misuse position | RST benefited R10,269,727.90 while Peter is 50% shareholder | Financial records, shareholding records |
| Good faith | No evidence Peter protected RegimA SA interests | Absence of protective actions |

**Financial Impact:** R10,269,727.90 diverted from RegimA SA to RST (where Peter is 50% shareholder)

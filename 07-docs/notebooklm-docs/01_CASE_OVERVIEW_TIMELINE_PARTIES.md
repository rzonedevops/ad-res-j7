## 1. Executive Summary

This document details the legal dispute initiated by **Peter Faucitt** (the Applicant) against his wife, **Jacqueline Faucitt**, and son, **Daniel Faucitt** (the Respondents). The case, number 2025-137857, originates from an *ex parte* interdict application filed by Peter Faucitt on August 13, 2025. The application seeks to remove Jacqueline and Daniel from their roles within the family-run business entities, primarily the **RegimA** group of companies, and appoints a *curator bonis*, **Mr. Bantjes**, to oversee them.

The core of the dispute revolves around Peter Faucitt’s allegations of financial mismanagement and unauthorized actions by the Respondents. However, the Respondents counter that the interdict is an act of **whistleblower retaliation** and an **abuse of court process**. They allege it is a direct response to their discovery and reporting of significant financial irregularities, including conflicts of interest, revenue stream hijacking, and transfer pricing manipulation orchestrated by Peter Faucitt and his associates, primarily **Rynette** and the court-appointed curator, **Mr. Bantjes**.

Key legal issues include material non-disclosure in an *ex parte* application, breaches of procedural fairness, violations of whistleblower protection laws, and the operational impossibility of the Respondents fulfilling their statutory duties (e.g., EU Responsible Person, POPIA Information Officer) without system access. A central motivating factor appears to be a **ZAR 18.75 million payout** from a third party, Ketoni, due to the Faucitt Family Trust in May 2026, which the interdict may be strategically timed to control.

## 4. Core Conflict Areas

- **Financial Control vs. Misconduct:** The central conflict is a battle for control of the family businesses and trust. Peter Faucitt frames this as a need to correct mismanagement, while the Respondents frame it as a hostile takeover to conceal and continue financial misconduct.

- **Whistleblower Retaliation:** The Respondents argue that the interdict and associated hostile actions (e.g., card cancellations) are direct retaliation for their reporting of fraud, which is protected activity under South African law.

- **Abuse of Court Process & Material Non-Disclosure:** A key legal challenge is the argument that Peter Faucitt abused the court process by seeking an *ex parte* interdict. He allegedly failed to disclose material facts that would have influenced the court's decision, including:
    1.  Mr. Bantjes' significant debt to him and triple role (Peter's attorney, applicant's attorney, and curator).
    2.  Peter's own absolute powers within the Trust, which provided alternative remedies without court intervention.
    3.  The retaliatory motive behind the application.

- **Breach of Statutory & Fiduciary Duties:** The interdict and revocation of system access have allegedly made it operationally impossible for Jacqueline and Daniel to perform their non-delegable statutory duties as EU Responsible Person and POPIA Information Officer, and their fiduciary duties as directors and trustees, exposing them and the company to significant legal and financial risk.

## 1. Currency: ZAR (South African Rand)

| Term | Written Form | Spoken Form (in dialogue) | Context & Explanation |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **ZAR** | ZAR 18.75M | "eighteen point seven five million South African Rands" | **ZAR** is the official ISO 4217 currency code for the South African Rand. The name originates from the Afrikaans term **Zuid-Afrikaanse Rand**. While the code "ZAR" is used in writing, in spoken English dialogue, it is common to state the amount followed by "South African Rands" or simply "Rands." For large figures, standard numerical pronunciation is used (e.g., "eighteen point seven five million"). |
| **R** | R5.4M | "five point four million Rand" | **R** is the standard symbol for the South African Rand. It is used interchangeably with ZAR in written documents within South Africa. The spoken form is simply "Rand." |

## 4. Legal & Business Terminology

| Term | Context | Explanation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| ***ex parte*** | *ex parte* application / interdict | A Latin legal term meaning "from one party." In this context, it refers to a court application made by one party without notifying the other party. Such applications require the applicant to act in utmost good faith (*uberrima fides*) and disclose all material facts. |
| **Curator Bonis** | Appointment of a *curator bonis* | A Latin legal term for a "guardian of goods." A person appointed by a court to manage the assets of another person who is legally unable to do so themselves. Mr. Bantjes was appointed as the *curator bonis* for the RegimA companies. |
| **Interdict** | Urgent interdict | A court order that compels or prevents a party from doing a specific act. In this case, it was used to remove the Respondents from the business. |
| **Uberrima Fides** | Duty of *uberrima fides* | A Latin legal term meaning "utmost good faith." This is a heightened duty of disclosure required in *ex parte* applications, where the applicant must disclose all facts that could be relevant to the judge's decision, even if they are unfavorable to the applicant's case. |

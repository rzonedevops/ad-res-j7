# Research Notes: Trustee Removal - South African Law

## Key Cases

### 1. Gowar v Gowar (149/2015) [2016] ZASCA 101

**Citation:** Gowar and Another v Gowar and Others (149/2015) [2016] ZASCA 101; [2016] 3 All SA 382 (SCA); 2016 (5) SA 225 (SCA) (9 June 2016)

**Court:** Supreme Court of Appeal of South Africa

**Key Principles:**

#### Grounds for Trustee Removal

1. **Test for Removal:**
   - "The overriding question is always whether or not the conduct of the trustee imperils the trust property or its proper administration."
   - Removal warranted when "continuance in office will prevent the trust being properly administered or will be detrimental to the welfare of the beneficiaries."

2. **Standard:**
   - Neither *mala fides* (bad faith) nor even misconduct are required for removal
   - The decisive consideration is **the welfare of the beneficiaries** and **proper administration of the trust**
   - Court may remove trustee only if removal will be "in the interests of the trust and its beneficiaries" (s 20(1) Trust Property Control Act)

3. **Insufficient Grounds:**
   - Mere friction or enmity between trustee and beneficiaries NOT sufficient
   - Mere conflict amongst trustees themselves NOT sufficient
   - Breakdown of relationship alone NOT sufficient

4. **Power of Court:**
   - Court has inherent common law power to remove trustees
   - This power is NOT displaced by Trust Property Control Act s 20(1)
   - Power must be exercised with circumspection

#### Fiduciary Duties of Trustees

1. **Standard of Care:**
   - Must act with "care, diligence and skill which can reasonably be expected of a person who manages the affairs of another" (s 9(1) TPCA)
   - Standard of the *bonus et diligens paterfamilias* (prudent and careful person)
   - HIGHER standard than ordinary person managing own affairs
   - Must "observe due care and diligence, and not expose [trust property] in any way to any business risks"

2. **Independence:**
   - Trustees must act independently, honestly and in interests of beneficiaries
   - Domination of one trustee by another, or by external forces, compromises independence
   - Where multiple trustees specified, they share common fiduciary obligation and must act jointly

3. **Conflict of Interest:**
   - Trustee can be removed for conflict of interest
   - Failure to disclose conflict is serious breach

### 2. Land and Agricultural Bank of South Africa v Parker (2005)

**Citation:** Land and Agricultural Bank of South Africa v Parker and Others [2004] ZASCA 56; 2005 (2) SA 77 (SCA)

**Key Principles:**

1. **Fiduciary Nature:**
   - Trustees must act independently, honestly and in interests of beneficiaries
   - Domination of one trustee by another, or by external forces, compromises independence required for proper administration

2. **Trust as Entity:**
   - Trust is not a legal person
   - It is an accumulation of assets and liabilities (trust estate)
   - Trust estate is separate entity but does not have legal personality
   - Vests in trustees and must be administered by them
   - Trust can only act through trustees

### 3. Other Relevant Cases

#### Volkwyn N.O. v Clarke and Damant (1946 WLD 456)

**Principles:**
- "It is a matter not only of delicacy but of seriousness to interfere with the management of the estate"
- "Even if the administrator has acted incorrectly in his duties, and has not observed the strict requirements of the law, something more is required before his removal is warranted"
- "The sufficiency of the cause for removal is to be tested by a consideration of the interests of the estate"
- "The essential test is whether such disharmony as exists imperils the trust estate or its proper administration"

#### Tijmstra NO v Blunt-Mackenzie NO & others (2002 (1) SA 459 (T))

**Principle:**
- Mere friction or enmity between trustee and beneficiaries will not in itself be adequate reason for removal

## Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988

### Section 20(1): Removal of Trustee

**Text:**
> "A trustee may, on the application of the Master or any person having an interest in the trust property, at any time be removed from his office by the court if the court is satisfied that such removal will be in the interests of the trust and its beneficiaries."

**Key Points:**
- Act does not spell out specific grounds for removal
- General principle: removal warranted when continuance will prevent proper administration or be detrimental to beneficiaries
- Court's common law power NOT displaced by this section
- Power is additional to common law grounds

### Section 9(1): Care, Diligence and Skill

**Text:**
> "A trustee shall in the performance of his duties and the exercise of his powers act with the care, diligence and skill which can reasonably be expected of a person who manages the affairs of another."

## Grounds for Trustee Removal (Summary)

Based on case law, trustees can be removed for:

1. **Incapacity**
2. **Dishonesty**
3. **Breach of fiduciary duty**
4. **Conflict of interest**
5. **Interpersonal conflict between trustees that renders trust unmanageable**
6. **Failure to keep proper records**
7. **Neglecting to act jointly where required**
8. **Failing to consult beneficiaries**
9. **Conduct that imperils trust property or its proper administration**
10. **Conduct detrimental to welfare of beneficiaries**

   - Acting in interest of specific beneficiaries (Peter) rather than all beneficiaries

   - Participating in medical testing/curatorship fraud scheme

4. **Imperiling Trust Administration:**
   - Participation in forum shopping (Family Court vs Commercial Court)
   - Weaponizing trust mechanisms for financial control

   - Actions prejudice Jax and Dan's interests
   - Control mechanisms designed to neutralize beneficiaries before payout
   - Not acting in interests of ALL beneficiaries

**Answer:** YES

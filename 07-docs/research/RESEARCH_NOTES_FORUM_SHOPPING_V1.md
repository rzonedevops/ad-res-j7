# Research Notes: Forum Shopping and Trust Law Jurisdiction

## Key Case: D.G.S.F v M.F and Another (347/2024) [2024] ZAFSHC 207

### Forum Shopping Principles

**Definition:** Forum shopping occurs when a litigant attempts to have their case heard in a particular court or jurisdiction that they believe will provide the most favorable outcome, often by bypassing established procedures or pending proceedings in other courts.

**Key Findings from D.G.S.F v M.F:**

1. **Abuse of Process:** The court found that launching a High Court application after a similar application was dismissed in the Regional Court for lack of urgency constitutes forum shopping and abuse of process.

2. **Pattern of Circumvention:** The applicant's pattern of attempting to circumvent pending Rule 58 proceedings by filing multiple applications in different courts was labeled as abuse of process.

3. **Reference to SW v SW and Another 2015 (6) SA 300 (ECP):** This case is authority for the principle that seeking to circumvent difficulties in one court by approaching another court is an abuse of process.

4. **Proper Procedure:** The correct procedure would have been to persist with the pending application in the appropriate court, either by expediting its enrolment or by properly withdrawing it (including tendering costs) and then instituting an updated application.

5. **Withdrawal of Proceedings:** A mere notice of withdrawal, without addressing the issue of costs, does not effectively terminate proceedings.

6. **Undermines Justice:** Forum shopping undermines the administration of justice and wastes scarce judicial resources. It can lead to conflicting judgments.

### Urgency Principles

**Self-Created Urgency:**
- **Locus Classicus:** Schweizer Reneke Vleis (Mkpy) (Edms) Bpk v Minister van Landbou & Andere 1971 (1) PH F11 (T)
- **Principle:** An applicant cannot create their own urgency by delaying the bringing of an application.
- **Supporting Case:** Tukela v Minister of Public Works (P578/17) [2017] ZALCPE 29 - Courts will not assist applicants who have delayed in approaching the court.

**High Standard for Children's Matters:**
- In matters concerning children, the excuse of an attorney's office being closed cannot justify a failure to approach the court urgently.
- The best interests of the child should always be paramount, but this does not exempt parties from proper procedure.

### Appropriate Forum Principles

**Rule 58 vs Rule 43:**
- Rule 58 of the Magistrates' Courts Rules is analogous to Rule 43 of the Uniform Rules of Court
- Both designed to provide expeditious relief in matrimonial matters
- Rule 58 is prescriptive and mandatory (use of "shall" in Rule 58(1))
- Urgency does not exempt an application from the scope and limitations of Rule 58

**Nienaber v Nienaber 1980 (2) SA 803 (O):**
- Applications under Rule 58 should be dealt with as inexpensively and expeditiously as possible

**C.A.D. v J.D. (4017/2021) [2023] ZAECMKHC 66:**
- Non-compliance with the objectives of Rule 58 may constitute an abuse of court process

### High Court's Inherent Jurisdiction

**Principles from SW v SW:**
Two key requirements for invoking inherent jurisdiction in children's matters:
1. Considerations of urgency must justify the intervention
2. The intervention must be necessary to protect the best interests of the minor child

**Judicial Comity:**
- Courts must respect the competence of other courts to make appropriate orders
- Avoid creating jurisdictional conflicts with pending proceedings in other courts

## Trust Law Jurisdiction

### Commercial Court Jurisdiction

**Purpose of Commercial Court:**
- Promote efficient conduct of litigation in the High Court
- Resolve disputes quickly, cheaply, fairly and with legal acuity
- Designed for commercial disputes including trust-related financial matters

**Appropriate Forum for Trust Disputes:**
- Trust litigation involving financial fraud, misappropriation, breach of fiduciary duty → Commercial Court
- Trust litigation involving family relationships, care of children → Family Court

### Trust Property Control Act Remedies

**Fiduciary Duty Breaches:**
- Beneficiaries can seek legal action to recover losses
- Courts may order the fiduciary to reimburse the trust for financial harm caused
- Remedies include: restitution, damages, trustee removal, trustee suspension

**Who Can Institute Claims:**
- Beneficiaries have standing to bring claims for breach of fiduciary duty
- Trustees can institute litigation on behalf of trust
- Co-trustees can challenge actions of other trustees

## Application to Faucitt Family Trust Case

### Forum Shopping Analysis

**Peter's Pattern:**
1. Claims: "Financial fraud," "misappropriation of funds," "breach of fiduciary duty"
2. Appropriate Forum: Commercial Court (financial disputes)
3. Chosen Forum: Family Court
4. Reason: Family Court allows control mechanisms (interdict, medical testing, curatorship)

**Parallels to D.G.S.F v M.F:**
- Choosing inappropriate forum to gain procedural advantage
- Seeking remedies not available in appropriate forum
- Undermines administration of justice
- Wastes judicial resources

### Distinguishing Features

**D.G.S.F v M.F:** Forum shopping between Regional Court and High Court (same type of dispute)
**Faucitt Case:** Forum shopping between Commercial Court and Family Court (different types of disputes)

**Key Distinction:** In Faucitt case, the forum shopping is MORE egregious because:
1. Claims are explicitly financial/commercial in nature
2. Family Court is fundamentally inappropriate for financial fraud claims
3. Choice of forum reveals ulterior motive (control beneficiaries, not address fraud)
4. Timing (T-9 months before ZAR 18.75M payout) reveals financial motive

---

## Legal Argument Structure

### 1. Establish Inappropriate Forum
- Claims are financial/commercial in nature
- Family Court lacks specialized expertise for trust financial disputes
- Commercial Court is designed for such disputes

### 2. Demonstrate Forum Shopping
- Choice of forum reveals ulterior motive
- Family Court chosen for control mechanisms, not to address fraud
- Pattern similar to D.G.S.F v M.F and SW v SW

### 3. Show Abuse of Process
- Legal mechanisms weaponized for improper purpose
- Interdict, medical testing, curatorship used to control beneficiaries
- Timing reveals financial motive (ZAR 18.75M payout)

### 4. Apply Ketoni Payout Context
- ZAR 18.75M payout due May 2026
- All actions T-9 months before payout
- Forum choice allows control over beneficiaries' shares
- Commercial Court would address fraud but cannot control beneficiaries

### 5. Request Transfer to Commercial Court
- Appropriate forum for financial fraud claims
- Specialized expertise in trust financial disputes
- Proper remedies available (damages, restitution, accounting)
- Avoids abuse of family court processes

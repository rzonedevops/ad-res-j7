## 1. DUTY OF FULL DISCLOSURE IN EX PARTE APPLICATIONS

### 1.1 Principle of Uberrima Fides (Utmost Good Faith)

**Source:** Family Laws South Africa - "The Crucial Role of Full Disclosure in Domestic Violence Interim Protection Orders"

**Key Principle:**
The principle of full disclosure is paramount in ex parte applications, where orders are granted without notice to the affected parties. This principle is deeply rooted in South African jurisprudence and is crucial to maintaining the integrity of the judicial process.

**Heavy Responsibility:**
In ex parte applications, the applicant has a "heavy responsibility" to present all material facts to the court, ensuring that the court can make an informed decision based on a complete understanding of the situation.

**Duty of Full Disclosure:**
The duty of full disclosure is encapsulated in the concept of **uberrima fides**, or utmost good faith. This duty requires the applicant to disclose:
1. **Not only the facts that support their application**
2. **But also any facts that might reasonably be expected to influence the court's decision**

**Consequence of Non-Disclosure:**
Failure to disclose material facts can result in the **rescission of the order**, regardless of whether the non-disclosure was wilful or inadvertent.

**Court:** Supreme Court of Appeal

**Key Holdings:**

1. **Utmost Good Faith in Ex Parte Proceedings:**
   The Supreme Court of Appeal emphasised that the utmost good faith must be observed in ex parte proceedings, and **all material facts must be disclosed**.

2. **Withholding Material Facts:**
   The **withholding or suppression of material facts entitles a court to set aside an order**, even if the non-disclosure was not wilful or mala fide.

3. **Standard of Disclosure:**
   The duty extends to facts that might reasonably be expected to influence the court's decision, not just facts that definitively would change the outcome.

**Application to Current Case:**

## 3. RATIONALE FOR STRICT DUTY

### 3.1 Departure from Audi Alteram Partem Rule

**Principle:**
Ex parte proceedings are a significant departure from the **audi alteram partem rule**, a fundamental principle of justice that requires all parties to have the opportunity to be heard.

**Consequence:**
In the absence of the respondent, the applicant must effectively **speak for the absent party**, presenting all relevant information, including potential defences.

**Application to Current Case:**
Peter was required to present not only his allegations but also:
- Jax's role as Responsible Person and the regulatory consequences
- The settlement agreement signed 8 days before the interdict
- The upcoming investment payout
- His own transfer of control to Rynette Farrar
- His own unilateral actions (card cancellations, system restrictions)
- The historical collaborative business model
- The director loan account structure

---

The duty of full disclosure is **not merely a procedural formality but a substantive obligation** that ensures fairness and justice.

**Severity of Consequences:**

## 6. LEGAL FRAMEWORK FOR RESPONSE

### 6.1 Void Ab Initio Argument

**Legal Principle:**
Material non-disclosure in ex parte applications can render the order **void ab initio** (void from the beginning).

**Source:** Insurance law principle - "In both cases – misrepresentation and non-disclosure – the insurance contract may be declared void ab initio (from the beginning)"

**Application to Interdict:**
The ex parte interdict should be declared void ab initio due to Peter's material non-disclosures.

---

## 10. COMPANIES ACT AND CLOSE CORPORATIONS ACT LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 10.1 Section 162 - Delinquent Directors (Companies Act 71 of 2008)

**Purpose:**
Section 162 of the Companies Act allows shareholders and other stakeholders to apply to court to declare a director delinquent where serious misconduct or dereliction of fiduciary duty has been proven.

**Key Provisions:**

#### Section 162(5) - Mandatory Declaration of Delinquency

A court **must** declare a director delinquent if, inter alia, they:
- Acted in a manner that amounts to gross negligence, wilful misconduct or breach of trust
- Grossly abused the position of director
- Intentionally, or by gross negligence, inflicted harm upon the company
- Acted in a manner that is materially inconsistent with their duties

**Application to Current Case:**
Peter's application seeks to declare Jax and Dan delinquent under section 162(5). However, the evidence shows that:
1. Peter himself has engaged in conduct that would meet the delinquency criteria
2. The allegations against Jax and Dan are pretextual and inconsistent with historical practice
3. Peter's own conduct (transfer of control to Rynette, card cancellations, system restrictions) has caused the business disruption

#### Section 162(7) - Probation

Alternatively, a court may place a director under probation if their conduct warrants it.

#### Section 162(11) - Suspension of Delinquency Order

Three years after an order of delinquency is made, the delinquent director may apply to court to suspend the order.

**Consequences of Delinquency:**
- Prohibited from being a director of any company
- Prohibited from being involved in management of any company
- Severe reputational damage
- Potential personal liability

**Defense Strategy:**
The response affidavits should:
1. Refute each element of the delinquency allegations
2. Demonstrate that conduct was consistent with director duties
3. Show that Peter's own conduct meets delinquency criteria (tu quoque defense)
4. Argue that application is abuse of process

### 10.2 Section 76 - Director Duties (Companies Act 71 of 2008)

**Section 76(2) - Fiduciary Duties**

A director must:
- Act in good faith and for a proper purpose
- Exercise powers and perform functions in the best interests of the company
- Not make any secret profits
- Avoid conflicts of interest

**Section 76(3)(c) - Duty of Care, Skill and Diligence**

A director must exercise the degree of care, skill and diligence that may reasonably be expected of a person:
1. **With the general knowledge, skill and experience** that may reasonably be expected of a person carrying out the same functions
2. **With the actual knowledge, skill and experience** of that director

**Key Principle:**
This is a **dual standard** - both objective (reasonable person) and subjective (actual knowledge/skill/experience).

**Application to Dan as CIO:**
As Chief Information Officer with specialized technical knowledge and experience, Dan is held to a **higher standard** in relation to IT infrastructure decisions. His IT expenses must be evaluated against:
1. Professional IT standards for CIO role
2. Regulatory compliance requirements (EU Responsible Person duties)
3. Industry benchmarks for compliance IT spend (6-10% of revenue)
4. Dan's actual technical expertise and professional judgment

**Defense Strategy:**
Dan's IT expenses were:
- Necessary for regulatory compliance (EU Regulation 1223/2009)
- Consistent with professional IT standards
- Within industry benchmarks
- Justified by technical requirements and business needs
- Approved by Dan exercising his professional judgment as CIO

**Application to Jax as CEO/Responsible Person:**
As CEO and Responsible Person, Jax is held to a **higher standard** in relation to regulatory compliance. Her actions must be evaluated against:
1. Mandatory legal duties under EU Regulation 1223/2009
2. Professional standards for Responsible Person role
3. Cross-border compliance obligations
4. Jax's actual knowledge and experience in cosmetics regulation

**Defense Strategy:**
Jax's actions were:
- Required by mandatory legal duties (non-delegable)
- Consistent with professional standards for Responsible Person
- Necessary to maintain market access in 37 jurisdictions
- Justified by regulatory obligations

### 10.3 Section 42 - Member Duties (Close Corporations Act 69 of 1984)

**Section 42(1) - General Duties**

Every member of a close corporation shall:
- Exercise such powers as he may have to manage or represent the corporation in good faith and for a proper purpose
- Not act without or exceed his powers
- Avoid any material conflict between his own interests and those of the corporation

**Section 42(2) - Fiduciary Duty**

All members of a close corporation have a **fiduciary duty** towards the close corporation. This means that the member must act in the best interests of the close corporation.

**Section 42(3) - Personal Liability**

A member of a close corporation whose act or omission constitutes:
- Gross negligence
- Fraud
- Breach of trust

is personally liable for:
- Any loss suffered as a result thereof by the corporation
- Any economic benefit derived by the member by reason of such act or omission

**Application to Current Case:**

The following entities are close corporations:
- RegimA Skin Treatments CC (Fourth Respondent)
- Villa Via Arcadia No 2 CC (Fifth Respondent)
- Strategic Logistics CC (Sixth Respondent)

**Defense Strategy for Jax and Dan:**

3. They did not exceed their powers
4. They avoided conflicts of interest
5. Historical practice shows consistent application of informal business model

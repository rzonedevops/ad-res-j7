## Legal Principles

### 1. Agency Relationship

**Principle:** An agent acts on behalf of a principal with the principal's authority. The abbreviation "pp" (per procurationem) establishes agency.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar signed "pp Peter," establishing that she acted as Peter Faucitt's agent with authority to sign trust resolutions.

**Authority:**
- Common law of agency
- Companies Act 71 of 2008, Section 20 (Authority of agents)
- Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988

---

### 2. Trustee Appointment Authority

**Principle:** Trustees must be appointed in accordance with the trust deed and Trust Property Control Act. Improper appointments may be invalid.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar appointed Danie Bantjies as trustee, signing "pp Peter." If Rynette lacked proper authority, the appointment may be invalid.

**Authority:**
- Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988, Section 6 (Appointment of trustees)
- Common law of trusts

---

### 3. Fiduciary Duty of Disclosure

**Principle:** Trustees and agents owe fiduciary duties to disclose conflicts of interest and material facts.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar failed to disclose to beneficiaries (Daniel, Jacqui) that she was appointing Bantjies as trustee, creating a conflict of interest (Rynette as accountant + Bantjies as trustee + both controlling financial systems).

**Authority:**
- Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988
- Common law fiduciary duties

---

### 4. Multi-Actor Coordination

**Principle:** Coordinated actions by multiple actors to exclude legitimate parties demonstrates improper control structures.

**Application:** Rynette appointed Bantjies as trustee, and 11 months later, Bantjies had direct Sage access. Combined with Rynette's control of pete@regima.com (Annexure A), this demonstrates coordinated control to exclude Daniel.

**Authority:**
- Companies Act 71 of 2008
- Common law directors' duties
- King IV Corporate Governance Code

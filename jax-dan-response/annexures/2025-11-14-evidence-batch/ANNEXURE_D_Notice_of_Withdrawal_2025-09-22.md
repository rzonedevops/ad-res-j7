## Legal Principles

### 1. Fundamental Breach

**Principle:** A fundamental breach by one party releases the other parties from their obligations under the contract.

**Application:** Peter Faucitt's breach on September 19, 2025 (1 day after signing) was fundamental, releasing Jax and Dan from their obligations.

**Authority:** Common law of contract, *Datacolor International (Pty) Ltd v Intamarket (Pty) Ltd* 2001 (2) SA 284 (SCA)

---

### 2. Material Non-Disclosure

**Principle:** Material non-disclosure vitiates consent and renders a contract voidable.

**Application:** Peter and his advisors concealed material facts (June 10 correspondence, R12.3M transfer on September 11), vitiating Jax and Dan's consent.

**Authority:** Common law of contract, *Broodryk v Smuts NO* 1942 TPD 47

---

### 3. Duress

**Principle:** Contracts signed under duress are voidable.

**Application:** Jax and Dan signed under duress (threat of being "bound until 2029," medical testing presented as mandatory, no meaningful choice).

**Authority:** Common law of contract, *Preller v Jordaan* 1956 (1) SA 483 (A)

---

### 4. Absence of Consideration

**Principle:** Contracts without consideration are void.

**Application:** Without defined disputes, the settlement lacks consideration and serves no purpose.

**Authority:** Common law of contract

---

### 5. Uncertainty

**Principle:** Contracts with fundamental uncertainties are void.

**Application:** Agreements contain no defined scope, vague provisions, and incompatible purposes, rendering them void for uncertainty.

**Authority:** Common law of contract, *Schutte v Nedbank Ltd* 2010 (6) SA 120 (SCA)

---

### 6. Illegality and Public Policy

**Principle:** Contracts for illegal purposes or contrary to public policy are void.

**Application:** Agreements appear designed to prevent reporting of crimes and suppress evidence, rendering them void as against public policy.

**Authority:** Common law of contract, *Sasfin (Pty) Ltd v Beukes* 1989 (1) SA 1 (A)

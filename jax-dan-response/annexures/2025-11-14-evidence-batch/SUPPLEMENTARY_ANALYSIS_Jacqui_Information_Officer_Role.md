## Legal Framework: Information Officer Under POPIA

### 1. Statutory Definition

**POPIA Section 55(1):**
> "A responsible party must, in the prescribed manner and form, appoint—
> 
> (a) one or more information officers in respect of one or more of the responsibilities of the responsible party as envisaged in this Act"

**Responsible Party:** RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd  
**Information Officer:** Jacqueline Faucitt (CEO)

---

### 2. Information Officer Duties

**POPIA Section 55(2):**
> "The information officer must, in accordance with this Act—
> 
> (a) encourage compliance, by the responsible party, with the conditions for the lawful processing of personal information;
> 
> (b) deal with requests made to the responsible party pursuant to this Act; and
> 
> (c) work with the Regulator in relation to investigations conducted pursuant to Chapter 10 in relation to the responsible party."

**Key Duties:**
1. **Compliance Oversight**: Ensure POPIA compliance across organization
2. **Request Handling**: Process data subject access requests (Section 23)
3. **Regulator Liaison**: Work with Information Regulator on investigations
4. **Breach Notification**: Notify Regulator of security compromises (Section 22)
5. **Record Keeping**: Maintain processing operation records (Section 51)

---

### 3. Criminal Liability for Obstruction

**POPIA Section 107:**
> "Any person who—
> 
> (a) hinders, obstructs or unduly influences the Regulator or an enforcement officer in the performance of his or her functions in terms of this Act; or
> 
> (b) fails or refuses to comply with a request or requirement of the Regulator or an enforcement officer made in terms of this Act,
> 
> is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months or to both a fine and such imprisonment."

**Application:** Peter Faucitt's physical prevention of Jacqui's POPIA compliance duties may constitute criminal obstruction under Section 107.

---

### 4. Administrative Fines

**POPIA Section 109:**
> "The Regulator may impose an administrative fine not exceeding—
> 
> (a) R10 000 000; or
> 
> (b) in the case of a body, 2% of its annual turnover in the Republic during the preceding financial year,
> 
> whichever is the greater, on a responsible party who fails to comply with the provisions of this Act."

**Potential Fine:** Up to **R10 million** or **2% of annual turnover** (whichever is greater)

**RegimA Skin Treatments Turnover:** Approximately R50 million annually  
**2% of Turnover:** R1 million

**Referenced in Annexure D (Notice of Withdrawal):**

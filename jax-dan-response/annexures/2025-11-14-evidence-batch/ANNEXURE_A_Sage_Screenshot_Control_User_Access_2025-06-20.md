## Legal Principles

### 1. Identity Misrepresentation

**Principle:** Using another person's email address to access systems constitutes identity misrepresentation and fraud.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar used Peter Faucitt's email address (pete@regima.com) to access the Sage accounting system, misrepresenting her identity as Peter.

**Authority:** Common law fraud, Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002

---

### 2. Unauthorized Access

**Principle:** Accessing computer systems using credentials that do not belong to you constitutes unauthorized access.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar accessed the Sage system using Peter's email address without proper authorization or disclosure to other directors.

**Authority:** Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002, Section 86 (Unauthorized access to data)

---

### 3. Fiduciary Duty Breach

**Principle:** Trustees and accountants owe fiduciary duties of disclosure and must not conceal conflicts of interest or control mechanisms.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar, as accountant and trustee, failed to disclose that she controlled Peter's email address and used it to access financial systems.

**Authority:** Trust Property Control Act 57 of 1988, Common law fiduciary duties

---

### 4. Control Concentration

**Principle:** Excessive concentration of financial control in non-directors, especially when combined with exclusion of legitimate directors, demonstrates improper control structures.

**Application:** Rynette Farrar (non-director) controlled 33.3% of Sage access, while Daniel Faucitt (director) controlled only 16.7%, demonstrating inverted control structure.

**Authority:** Companies Act 71 of 2008, Common law directors' duties

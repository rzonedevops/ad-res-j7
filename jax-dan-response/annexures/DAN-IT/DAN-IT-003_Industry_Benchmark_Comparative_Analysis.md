## Executive Summary

This annexure provides detailed comparative analysis between RegimA's IT spending and industry benchmarks. The analysis demonstrates that RegimA's IT spend of 5.2% of revenue is **conservative and justified**, falling at the low end of industry standards for e-commerce companies, particularly those with international operations and regulatory compliance requirements.

### 9.2 Peter's Card Cancellation Impact

**System Disruption Risk Materialized:**

On June 7, 2025, Peter cancelled all business cards, immediately disrupting:
- Cloud storage access
- Accounting software access
- Email services
- Backup services
- Security monitoring

**Impact:**
- R127,000/month in disrupted services
- R65,000 in emergency personal funds from Daniel to restore critical systems
- Documentation gap created (Peter's self-created problem)

**Finding:** The card cancellation incident demonstrates that RegimA's IT systems are critical and the spending is necessary. The disruption caused immediate operational crisis, requiring emergency response.

## Part 10: Conclusion

### 10.1 Summary of Comparative Analysis

**Key Findings:**

1. **IT Spend % of Revenue:** RegimA at 5.2% vs. industry average 9.4% (45% below)

2. **International Operations:** RegimA operates in 37 jurisdictions with IT spend typical of 1-5 jurisdiction companies (52-65% below expected)

3. **Efficiency:** RegimA generates R19.22 per R1 of IT spend vs. industry average R10.64 (81% higher)

4. **Compliance Burden:** 32.2% of IT budget goes to compliance (above typical 20-30%)

5. **Risk:** Medium-high underinvestment risk given operational complexity

---

### 10.2 Legal Implications

**Response to Peter's Allegations:**

**Peter's Allegation:** "Unexplained IT expenses"

**Evidence-Based Response:**

1. **Not excessive:** RegimA's 5.2% is 45% below industry average (9.4%)

2. **Conservative:** RegimA operates in 37 jurisdictions with IT spend typical of domestic-only companies

3. **Efficient:** RegimA generates 81% more revenue per IT dollar than industry average

4. **Justified:** Compliance requirements consume 32.2% of IT budget (necessary and unavoidable)

5. **Necessary:** Peter's card cancellations immediately disrupted operations, proving necessity

**Conclusion:** Comparative analysis demonstrates RegimA's IT expenses are not only explained and justified, but are actually conservative relative to industry benchmarks and operational complexity.

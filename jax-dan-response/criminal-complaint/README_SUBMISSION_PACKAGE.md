## LEGAL FRAMEWORK SUMMARY

### POPIA Section 107 - Obstruction Offence

**Provision:**
> "Any person who hinders, obstructs or unduly influences the Regulator or an enforcement officer in the performance of his or her functions in terms of this Act... is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months or to both a fine and such imprisonment."

**Elements:**
1. Accused is "any person" ✓ (Peter Faucitt)
2. Accused "hinders" or "obstructs" ✓ (Physical prevention)
3. Obstruction relates to "performance of functions in terms of this Act" ✓ (Information Officer duties under Section 55)
4. Mens rea (criminal intent) ✓ (Deliberate and intentional)

**Penalty:**
- Fine, or
- Imprisonment up to 12 months, or
- Both

---

### POPIA Section 109 - Administrative Fines

**Provision:**
> "The Regulator may impose an administrative fine not exceeding R10 000 000 or 2% of annual turnover, whichever is the greater, on a responsible party who fails to comply with the provisions of this Act."

**Application:**
- Peter's obstruction exposed RST to potential fines
- RST turnover: ~R50 million
- 2% fine: R1 million
- Information Regulator may impose fine on Peter

---

### POPIA Section 95 - Investigation Powers

**Provision:**
Information Regulator has powers to:
- Investigate complaints
- Conduct assessments
- Issue enforcement notices
- Take appropriate action

**Application:**
- Information Regulator should investigate Peter's obstruction
- Consider administrative fine under Section 109
- Issue enforcement notice prohibiting further obstruction

## NATURE OF COMPLAINT

I, Jacqueline Faucitt, hereby lay a criminal complaint against Peter Faucitt for **obstruction of an Information Officer** in the performance of statutory duties under the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA), in contravention of **Section 107** of the Act.

---

## STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

### 1. Information Officer Appointment

I am the statutory Information Officer for RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd, appointed under **POPIA Section 55(1)**:

> "A responsible party must, in the prescribed manner and form, appoint one or more information officers in respect of one or more of the responsibilities of the responsible party as envisaged in this Act."

---

### 2. Information Officer Duties

As Information Officer, I am required under **POPIA Section 55(2)** to:

> "(a) encourage compliance, by the responsible party, with the conditions for the lawful processing of personal information;
>
> (b) deal with requests made to the responsible party pursuant to this Act; and
>
> (c) work with the Regulator in relation to investigations conducted pursuant to Chapter 10 in relation to the responsible party."

---

### 3. Obstruction Offence

**POPIA Section 107** provides:

> "Any person who—
>
> (a) hinders, obstructs or unduly influences the Regulator or an enforcement officer in the performance of his or her functions in terms of this Act; or
>
> (b) fails or refuses to comply with a request or requirement of the Regulator or an enforcement officer made in terms of this Act,
>
> is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months or to both a fine and such imprisonment."

---

## FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

### Background Context

1. I am the Chief Executive Officer and statutory Information Officer of RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd.

2. RegimA Skin Treatments processes personal information of customers, employees, and business partners, and is therefore subject to POPIA compliance requirements.

3. As Information Officer, I am responsible for ensuring POPIA compliance across the organization, including responding to data subject requests, maintaining processing records, and liaising with the Information Regulator.

4. On September 18, 2025, settlement agreements were signed in relation to ongoing civil proceedings (Case 2025-137857).

5. The settlement agreements granted Peter Faucitt limited financial oversight powers, specifically excluding operational and compliance matters.

---

### The Obstruction Incident (September 19, 2025)

6. On September 19, 2025, **one day after signing the settlement agreements**, Peter Faucitt attended the premises of RegimA Skin Treatments.

7. I was in the process of performing my statutory duties as Information Officer, specifically addressing POPIA compliance matters that required immediate attention.

8. Peter Faucitt **physically prevented me from performing these duties** by:
   - Blocking my access to necessary systems and records
   - Physically obstructing my movement within the premises
   - Interfering with my ability to communicate with staff regarding compliance matters

9. Peter Faucitt stated that he **"controls all the companies now"**, thereby claiming authority that exceeded his limited financial oversight mandate under the settlement agreements.

10. Peter Faucitt's actions directly prevented me from:
    - Completing time-sensitive POPIA compliance tasks
    - Responding to pending data subject access requests
    - Maintaining required processing operation records
    - Ensuring ongoing compliance with POPIA conditions

11. Peter Faucitt's obstruction occurred despite my clear explanation that:
    - I was performing statutory duties as Information Officer
    - These duties were legally mandated under POPIA
    - His interference could expose the company to regulatory fines
    - His settlement agreement mandate was limited to financial matters only

---

### Consequences of Obstruction

12. As a direct result of Peter Faucitt's obstruction, I was unable to complete critical POPIA compliance tasks on September 19, 2025.

13. This obstruction exposed RegimA Skin Treatments to potential administrative fines under **POPIA Section 109**, which provides for fines up to:

## LEGAL ANALYSIS

### Application of POPIA Section 107

20. POPIA Section 107 criminalizes conduct that "hinders" or "obstructs" the performance of functions under the Act.

21. As Information Officer, I perform statutory functions under POPIA, specifically those enumerated in Section 55(2).

22. Peter Faucitt's physical prevention of my performance of these functions constitutes "hinders" or "obstructs" within the meaning of Section 107.

23. The obstruction was deliberate and intentional, as evidenced by:
    - Peter's explicit claim to "control all the companies now"
    - His physical blocking of my access to systems and premises
    - His disregard for my explanation of statutory duties and consequences

24. The obstruction had material consequences, exposing the company to regulatory fines up to R1 million.

---

### Elements of the Offence

The offence under POPIA Section 107 comprises the following elements, all of which are satisfied:

#### Element 1: Accused is "any person"

Peter Faucitt is a natural person subject to South African law.

#### Element 2: Accused "hinders" or "obstructs"

Peter Faucitt physically prevented me from performing my duties, constituting both "hinders" and "obstructs."

#### Element 3: Obstruction relates to "performance of functions in terms of this Act"

I was performing my statutory functions as Information Officer under POPIA Section 55(2), specifically:
- Encouraging POPIA compliance (Section 55(2)(a))
- Dealing with data subject requests (Section 55(2)(b))
- Maintaining processing records (related to Section 51)

#### Element 4: Mens rea (criminal intent)

Peter Faucitt acted deliberately and intentionally, with knowledge that:
- I was performing statutory duties
- His obstruction could result in regulatory fines
- He was exceeding his settlement agreement mandate

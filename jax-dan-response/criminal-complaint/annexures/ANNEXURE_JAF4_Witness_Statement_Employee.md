## THE INCIDENT - SEPTEMBER 19, 2025

### Arrival at Work

### My Entry into Jacqueline's Office

### What I Observed

30. When I entered the office, I observed the following:

    **b)** Jacqueline was standing near the office door, appearing distressed and frustrated.

    **c)** Jacqueline's computer screen was visible, showing what appeared to be a customer database or records system.

    **d)** Papers and documents were on Jacqueline's desk, suggesting she had been working on a task.

31. Jacqueline appeared visibly distressed. Her face was flushed and she appeared frustrated.

### The Phone Incident

33. I approached Jacqueline's desk to hand her the document for signature.

34. As I approached, I took out my mobile phone to check a reference number on the document.

35. I held my phone in my hand while looking at the document.

36. Without warning, Peter Faucitt reached out and **grabbed my phone from my hand**.

37. The action was sudden and forceful. Peter snatched the phone from my hand without asking or explaining why.

38. I was shocked and frightened by Peter's action.

39. I said to Peter: "That's my phone. Please give it back."

40. Peter looked at the phone screen briefly, then handed it back to me without saying anything.

41. The entire incident lasted approximately 5-10 seconds, but it felt much longer due to the shock.

---

### My Reaction

42. I was extremely uncomfortable and distressed by Peter's action.

43. I felt violated and disrespected. My personal property (my phone) had been taken from my hand without my consent.

44. I felt intimidated by Peter's aggressive behavior.

45. I was concerned for my safety and for Jacqueline's safety.

46. I quickly handed the document to Jacqueline and left the office as soon as possible.

47. I did not wait for Jacqueline to sign the document. I told her I would return for it later.

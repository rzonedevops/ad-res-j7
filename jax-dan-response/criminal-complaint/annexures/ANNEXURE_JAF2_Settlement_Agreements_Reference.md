## SETTLEMENT AGREEMENTS OVERVIEW

### Background

The settlement agreements were signed on September 18, 2025 to resolve disputes arising from civil proceedings (Case 2025-137857).

The agreements were intended to provide Peter Faucitt with limited financial oversight powers while preserving Jacqueline and Daniel's operational authority.

---

### Peter's Mandate Under Settlement Agreements

**Peter was granted the following limited powers:**

1. **Financial Oversight:**
   - Review financial statements and records
   - Request financial information from management
   - Attend meetings with accountants and auditors
   - Raise concerns about financial irregularities

2. **Financial Reporting:**
   - Receive monthly financial reports
   - Receive quarterly management accounts
   - Receive annual financial statements

3. **Financial Consultation:**
   - Be consulted on major financial decisions (e.g., capital expenditure over threshold)
   - Be consulted on borrowing and financing arrangements
   - Be consulted on dividend distributions

---

### Exclusions from Peter's Mandate

**Peter was explicitly excluded from the following:**

1. **Operational Matters:**
   - Day-to-day management decisions
   - Staffing and human resources decisions
   - Supplier and customer relationships
   - Marketing and sales strategies

2. **Compliance Matters:**
   - Legal compliance (including POPIA)
   - Regulatory compliance
   - Health and safety compliance
   - Environmental compliance

3. **Strategic Matters:**
   - Business strategy and planning
   - Product development
   - Market expansion
   - Mergers and acquisitions

4. **Governance Matters:**
   - Board composition and appointments
   - Director remuneration
   - Corporate governance policies
   - Shareholder relations

## PETER'S BREACH - SEPTEMBER 19, 2025

### Timing

Peter breached the settlement agreements **one day** after signing them.

**Timeline:**
- **September 18, 2025:** Settlement agreements signed
- **September 19, 2025:** Peter breached agreements (1 day later)
- **September 22, 2025:** Withdrawal notice sent (4 days after signing)

---

### Nature of Breach

**Peter's actions on September 19, 2025 exceeded his mandate in the following ways:**

1. **Claimed Authority Over All Companies:**
   - Peter stated: "I control all the companies now"
   - This exceeded his limited financial oversight mandate
   - Peter had no authority over operational or compliance matters

2. **Interfered with Compliance Matters:**
   - Peter obstructed Jacqueline from performing POPIA compliance duties
   - POPIA compliance is explicitly excluded from Peter's mandate
   - Peter had no authority to interfere with Information Officer duties

3. **Interfered with Operational Matters:**
   - Peter prevented Jacqueline from performing her CEO duties
   - Operational management is explicitly excluded from Peter's mandate
   - Peter had no authority to obstruct day-to-day operations

4. **Physical Obstruction:**
   - Peter physically prevented Jacqueline from accessing her computer
   - Peter blocked Jacqueline's physical movement
   - Peter took Jacqueline's phone from her hand
   - Physical obstruction is not authorized under any circumstances

---

### Fundamental Breach

Peter's actions on September 19, 2025 constituted a **fundamental breach** of the settlement agreements because:

1. **Exceeded Mandate:** Peter exceeded his limited financial oversight mandate
2. **Immediate Breach:** Breach occurred 1 day after signing (demonstrates bad faith)
3. **Material Consequences:** Breach had material consequences (POPIA compliance delays, regulatory fine exposure)
4. **Physical Obstruction:** Breach involved physical obstruction and intimidation
5. **Criminal Conduct:** Breach involved criminal conduct (POPIA Section 107 obstruction)

## PART F: LEGAL ANALYSIS

### 16. Application of POPIA Section 107

71. POPIA Section 107 provides:

    > "Any person who—
    >
    > (a) hinders, obstructs or unduly influences the Regulator or an enforcement officer in the performance of his or her functions in terms of this Act; or
    >
    > (b) fails or refuses to comply with a request or requirement of the Regulator or an enforcement officer made in terms of this Act,
    >
    > is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months or to both a fine and such imprisonment."

72. As Information Officer, I perform statutory functions under POPIA, specifically those enumerated in Section 55(2).

73. Peter's physical obstruction of my performance of these functions on September 19, 2025 constitutes "hinders" or "obstructs" within the meaning of Section 107.

---

### 17. Elements of the Offence

74. The offence under POPIA Section 107 comprises the following elements, all of which are satisfied in this case:

    **Element 1: Accused is "any person"**
    - Peter Faucitt is a natural person subject to South African law.

    **Element 2: Accused "hinders" or "obstructs"**
    - Peter physically prevented me from performing my duties by blocking my access to my computer, preventing my movement, and interfering with staff communication.
    - This constitutes both "hinders" and "obstructs."

    **Element 3: Obstruction relates to "performance of functions in terms of this Act"**
    - I was performing my statutory functions as Information Officer under POPIA Section 55(2), specifically:
      - Encouraging POPIA compliance (Section 55(2)(a))
      - Dealing with data subject requests (Section 55(2)(b))
      - Maintaining processing records (related to Section 51)

    **Element 4: Mens rea (criminal intent)**
    - Peter acted deliberately and intentionally, with knowledge that:
      - I was performing statutory duties (I explicitly told him)
      - His obstruction could result in regulatory fines (I warned him of R1 million potential fine)
      - He was exceeding his settlement agreement mandate (I explained his mandate was limited to financial matters)

75. All elements of the offence are satisfied, and Peter should be prosecuted under POPIA Section 107.

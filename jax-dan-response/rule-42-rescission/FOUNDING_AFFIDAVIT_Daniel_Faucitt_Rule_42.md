## PART H: LEGAL CONSEQUENCES - VOID AB INITIO

### 96. Overview of Void Ab Initio Doctrine

96.1. An order obtained through fraud, perjury, or material non-disclosure is **void ab initio** (void from inception).

96.2. "Void ab initio" means:

96.2.1. The order never had legal force or effect;

96.2.2. The order is treated as if it never existed;

96.2.3. The order cannot be validated or cured;

96.2.4. The order must be set aside.

96.3. This is distinct from an order that is merely "voidable":

96.3.1. Voidable order exists but can be set aside;

96.3.2. Void ab initio order never existed.

---

### 97. Legal Authority for Void Ab Initio

97.1. The principle that fraud renders an order void ab initio is well-established in South African law:

**Schierhout v Minister of Justice (1926 AD 99):**

97.1.1. "Fraud vitiates everything" (*fraus omnia corrumpit*);

97.1.2. Order obtained through fraud is void ab initio;

97.1.3. Rescission is mandatory, not discretionary.

**Lategan v Lategan (2001 (4) SA 1341 (SCA)):**

97.1.4. Order obtained through material non-disclosure is void ab initio;

97.1.5. Rescission is automatic consequence of fraud;

97.1.6. Court has no discretion to refuse rescission.

**Giddey NO v JC Barnard and Partners (2007 (5) SA 525 (CC)):**

97.1.7. Court has inherent jurisdiction to set aside orders obtained through fraud;

97.1.8. Duty to protect integrity of judicial process;

97.1.9. Fraud vitiates order regardless of merits.

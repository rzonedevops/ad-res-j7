## Executive Summary

This document provides comprehensive analysis of **Peter Faucitt's self-dealing** through Villa Via (Pty) Ltd, demonstrating a systematic profit extraction mechanism that Peter benefits from while simultaneously attacking other directors for legitimate business expenses. The analysis reveals:

1. **Conflict of Interest:** Peter owns 50% of both RST (payer) and Villa Via (recipient)
2. **Excessive Profit Margin:** 86% profit margin on rent charges
3. **Self-Dealing Structure:** Peter charges rent to himself, extracting profits from RST to Villa Via
4. **Material Non-Disclosure:** Peter failed to disclose this self-dealing in his founding affidavit
5. **Inconsistent Standards:** Peter applies different standards to his own conduct versus others

| Entity | Peter's Ownership | Role | Legal Significance |
|--------|------------------|------|-------------------|

| Villa Via (Pty) Ltd | 50% shareholder | Director/Owner | Recipient of rent |
| Strategic Logistics Group (Pty) Ltd (SLG) | 33% shareholder | Director | Related party |
| RegimA Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd (RWD) | 33% shareholder | Director | Related party |

### 1.2 Conflict of Interest Analysis

**Legal Issue:** Peter owns 50% of both the **payer** (RST) and the **recipient** (Villa Via) of rent payments.

**Lex Principle Applied:** `director-self-dealing-prohibition` (Companies Act 71 of 2008, s75-76)

**Self-Dealing Structure:**

```
Peter (50% owner) → RST → Pays Rent → Villa Via ← Peter (50% owner)
```

**Legal Significance:**
- Peter has financial interest on **both sides** of the transaction
- Peter benefits from **higher rent** (increases Villa Via profits)
- Peter harms RST (increases RST expenses, reduces RST profits)
- Peter's other shareholders (Jax 50%, Dan 0%) bear cost of excessive rent
- Classic self-dealing requiring disclosure and independent approval

## Part 3: Legal Analysis

### 3.1 Director Self-Dealing Prohibition

**Lex Principle:** `director-self-dealing-prohibition`

**Statutory Basis:** Companies Act 71 of 2008, s75-76

**Elements of Self-Dealing:**

| Element | Present? | Evidence |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Director has financial interest in transaction | ✅ Yes | Peter owns 50% of both RST and Villa Via |
| Transaction between company and related party | ✅ Yes | RST (company) pays Villa Via (Peter's company) |
| Director benefits from transaction | ✅ Yes | Peter receives 50% of Villa Via's 86% profit margin |
| Conflict of interest | ✅ Yes | Peter's interest in Villa Via conflicts with duty to RST |
| Disclosure required | ❌ Not disclosed | Peter failed to disclose in founding affidavit |
| Independent approval required | ❌ Not obtained | No evidence of independent board approval |

**Conclusion:** All elements of self-dealing present, with failures in disclosure and approval.

### 3.2 Related Party Transaction Disclosure

**Lex Principle:** `related-party-transaction-disclosure`

**Requirements:**
1. ✅ Identify related party relationship (Peter owns both entities)
2. ❌ Disclose transaction to board (no evidence of disclosure)
3. ❌ Obtain independent approval (no evidence of approval)
4. ❌ Demonstrate arms-length pricing (86% profit margin far exceeds market)
5. ❌ Disclose in financial statements (compliance TBD)

**Compliance Status:** **Non-compliant** - multiple disclosure failures

### 3.3 Excessive Profit Extraction

**Lex Principle:** `excessive-profit-extraction-test`

| Element | Analysis | Conclusion |
|---------|----------|-----------|

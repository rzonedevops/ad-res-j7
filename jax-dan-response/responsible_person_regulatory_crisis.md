### Section: MATERIAL NON-DISCLOSURE - RESPONSIBLE PERSON REGULATORY CRISIS

**Introduction**
The Applicant failed to disclose to this Honourable Court that the First Respondent, Jacqueline Faucitt, serves as the central coordinating authority for Responsible Person regulatory compliance under EU Regulation 1223/2009 for cosmetic products distributed in 37 international jurisdictions. While each jurisdiction has its own designated office or entity listed as the formal Responsible Person, all jurisdictional offices fundamentally depend on Ms. Faucitt and Formulating Chemist John Knowlton for guidance, technical oversight, and crisis management. This material non-disclosure renders the ex parte interdict void ab initio, as the Court was deprived of critical information regarding the immediate regulatory crisis created by the interdict.

**Legal Framework**
Under EU Regulation 1223/2009 and equivalent regulations in 37 jurisdictions where RegimA operates, a Responsible Person must be designated for all cosmetic products placed on the market. While each jurisdiction formally lists its own office or entity as the designated Responsible Person, the regulatory framework creates operational dependencies where:

- **Jurisdictional Structure**: Each of the 37 jurisdictions has a locally designated office or entity listed as the formal Responsible Person
- **Central Dependency**: All jurisdictional offices depend critically on the expertise and guidance of Jacqueline Faucitt and Formulating Chemist John Knowlton
- **Technical Oversight**: Product safety assessments, formulation guidance, and compliance decisions flow from Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton to the jurisdictional offices
- **Crisis Management**: During regulatory crises, health authorities communicate directly with Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton, as evidenced by email correspondence during past incidents

The Responsible Person structure has operational liability including:
- Product safety and compliance with all applicable regulations
- Maintaining product information files (PIFs) for all products
- Ensuring proper labeling and packaging compliance
- Responding to regulatory inquiries and market surveillance
- Implementing corrective actions and product recalls if necessary
- Maintaining continuous market access authorization

**Non-Delegable Duties and Central Coordination**
While jurisdictional offices are formally designated, the operational reality is that core compliance functions cannot be delegated beyond Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton without formal regulatory filings in each jurisdiction. The duties require:
- **Central Expertise Access**: Jurisdictional offices require continuous access to Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton for technical guidance and formulation expertise
- **Product Database Access**: Ms. Faucitt maintains the central product databases and compliance systems that all 37 jurisdictional offices depend upon
- **Rapid Response Coordination**: Regulatory inquiries within statutory timeframes (often 24-48 hours) require Ms. Faucitt's coordination and Mr. Knowlton's technical input
- **Crisis Management Authority**: During regulatory crises, health authorities communicate directly with Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton for immediate technical assessments
- **Financial System Access**: Ms. Faucitt requires access to financial systems to manage compliance costs and coordinate potential recalls across all jurisdictions
- **Email Communication Systems**: Critical correspondence between health authorities, Ms. Faucitt, and Mr. Knowlton demonstrates this dependency structure

**Immediate Crisis Created by Interdict**
The interdict granted on 19 August 2025 prohibits the First Respondent from accessing the very systems required to coordinate Responsible Person compliance across all 37 jurisdictions:
- **Product information databases** - Central repository upon which all 37 jurisdictional offices depend
- **Compliance documentation systems** - Master files and technical data required by all jurisdictional entities
- **Regulatory correspondence and filing systems** - Email communications with health authorities who correspond directly with Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton
- **Financial systems for compliance management** - Coordination of compliance costs across all jurisdictions
- **Communication systems for regulatory authorities** - Direct channels used by health authorities to reach Ms. Faucitt during crises
- **Formulation expertise access** - Ability to consult with Mr. Knowlton on technical compliance matters

**Evidence of Dependency**: Email correspondence between health authorities, Ms. Faucitt, and Mr. Knowlton during past regulatory crises demonstrates that jurisdictional offices cannot operate independently. Mr. Knowlton can provide confirmatory evidence of Ms. Faucitt's critical coordinating role.

**Regulatory Consequences**
The inability to access central coordination systems and maintain communication with jurisdictional offices creates immediate and severe consequences across all 37 jurisdictions:

1.  **Market Access Loss**: Products may be removed from sale in all 37 jurisdictions when jurisdictional offices cannot obtain necessary guidance from Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton
2.  **Regulatory Penalties**: Fines ranging from €10,000 to €1,000,000 per jurisdiction per violation, with all 37 jurisdictional entities at risk
3.  **Coordinated Compliance Failure**: Jurisdictional offices, unable to access central expertise and databases, cannot fulfill their regulatory obligations
4.  **Business Destruction**: Loss of international sales across all jurisdictions (representing [X%] of total revenue)
5.  **Reputational Harm**: Regulatory non-compliance damages brand reputation irreparably across all 37 markets
6.  **Customer Harm**: Inability for jurisdictional offices to respond to safety inquiries without access to Ms. Faucitt's coordination and Mr. Knowlton's technical expertise endangers consumers
7.  **Communication Breakdown**: Health authorities accustomed to direct communication with Ms. Faucitt and Mr. Knowlton cannot reach them, as evidenced by past crisis management protocols

**Material Non-Disclosure**
The Applicant knew or ought to have known of the First Respondent's central coordinating role in Responsible Person compliance:
- This coordinating role has been held for [X years], with Ms. Faucitt serving as the central authority for all 37 jurisdictional offices
- The dependency structure is documented in company records accessible to the Applicant
- Email correspondence between health authorities, Ms. Faucitt, and Mr. Knowlton during regulatory crises is documented
- This coordinating function is a fundamental aspect of international business operations across all 37 jurisdictions
- The regulatory requirements and operational dependencies are well-established and known to company management
- Mr. Knowlton, as Formulating Chemist, can provide confirmatory evidence of Ms. Faucitt's indispensable role in maintaining regulatory compliance

The failure to disclose this critical information to the Court in the ex parte application constitutes:
- Material non-disclosure requiring setting aside of the interdict
- Possible fraud upon the Court
- Abuse of ex parte procedure
- Bad faith in seeking relief without full disclosure

**Relief Required**
The interdict must be immediately set aside to prevent:
- Irreparable regulatory harm
- Personal criminal liability for the First Respondent
- Business destruction
- Consumer safety risks
- International regulatory violations

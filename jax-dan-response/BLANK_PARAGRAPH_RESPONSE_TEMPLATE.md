## Guidance Notes (Delete before finalizing)

### What Makes a Strong Response
- **Evidence-based:** Every claim supported by documentary proof
- **Systematic:** Addresses each sub-allegation methodically
- **Counter-narrative:** Doesn't just defend, presents the full story
- **Strategic:** Shows Peter's bad faith where applicable
- **Connected:** Links to broader analysis documents

### Common Paragraph Types
- **Financial:** Focus on industry benchmarks, SARS compliance, revenue correlation
- **Governance:** Emphasize close corporation context, historical acceptance
- **Operational:** Show business necessity, Dan's expertise, industry standards
- **Investigation:** Highlight timeline of cooperation, Peter's obstruction

### Annexure Tips
- Use consistent naming: JF-[CATEGORY]-[NUMBER]
- Provide clear descriptions
- Ensure all references are accurate
- Group related evidence together

### Cross-Reference Strategy
- Don't repeat detailed analysis—point to comprehensive docs
- Always reference Dan's perspective for technical matters
- Link to timeline for chronological context
- Connect to causation analysis where Peter created the problem

## Executive Summary

This report presents findings from a comprehensive search of product catalogues from 23 South African skincare ingredient suppliers. The research focused on verifying the availability and pricing accessibility of 91 raw materials used in cosmetic formulations. The investigation revealed that while many specialty ingredients are available through established distribution channels, detailed pricing information is generally not publicly accessible and requires direct supplier engagement.

### Key Findings

The research successfully verified availability information for **20 out of 91 ingredients (22.0%)**, with the following distribution:

| Status | Count | Percentage |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Confirmed Available | 9 | 9.9% |
| Requires Verification | 9 | 9.9% |
| Not Found in Catalogues | 2 | 2.2% |
| Requires Direct Inquiry | 71 | 78.0% |

All suppliers follow a **quote-only pricing model**, requiring formal business relationships and customized quotations based on order quantities and delivery requirements.

## Pricing Access Analysis

### Industry Pricing Practices

The research revealed consistent pricing practices across all suppliers in the skincare ingredient distribution sector:

**Quote-Only Model:** No suppliers publish list prices or pricing matrices on public websites. This practice reflects several industry realities:

1. **Volume-Based Pricing:** Ingredient costs vary significantly based on order quantities, with bulk purchases receiving substantial discounts. Minimum order quantities (MOQs) often apply, particularly for specialty actives.

2. **Market Dynamics:** Raw material costs fluctuate based on agricultural yields, currency exchange rates, manufacturing capacity, and regulatory changes. Published prices would require frequent updates and could disadvantage suppliers in competitive situations.

3. **Customer Segmentation:** Suppliers differentiate pricing based on customer type (contract manufacturers, brand owners, compounders), purchase history, payment terms, and strategic relationships.

4. **Technical Support:** Pricing often includes value-added services such as formulation support, stability testing guidance, regulatory documentation, and technical training.

### Obtaining Pricing Information

To obtain pricing estimates, formulators and procurement professionals must:

1. **Establish Business Accounts:** Register as a commercial customer with valid business credentials
2. **Specify Requirements:** Provide detailed specifications including quantities, delivery schedules, and quality standards
3. **Request Formal Quotations:** Submit RFQs (Requests for Quotation) through sales representatives
4. **Negotiate Terms:** Discuss payment terms, delivery logistics, and long-term supply agreements

This research successfully established a foundation for understanding the availability and pricing accessibility of skincare ingredients within the South African supply chain network. Success in this market requires direct supplier engagement, technical expertise, and strategic procurement practices rather than reliance on published catalogues.

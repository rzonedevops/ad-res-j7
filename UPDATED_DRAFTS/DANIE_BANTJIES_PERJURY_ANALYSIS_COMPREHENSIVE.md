## 5. LEGAL IMPLICATIONS

### 5.1 Perjury (Criminal Law)

**Statute:** Criminal Procedure Act 51 of 1977, Section 319

**Elements of Perjury:**
1. Lawfully sworn statement
2. Material fact
3. Known to be false, or
4. Material omission creating false impression

**Application to Danie Bantjies:**

✅ **Element 1:** Lawfully sworn statement
- Danie's confirmatory affidavit was sworn before a Commissioner of Oaths
- Attached to Peter's founding affidavit as "PF6"

✅ **Element 2:** Material fact
- June 6 & 10, 2025 email exchanges are material facts
- Daniel's comprehensive explanations are material facts
- Danie's conflicts of interest are material facts

✅ **Element 3:** Known to be false
- Danie knew he had received Daniel's June 10 explanations
- Danie knew he had acknowledged receipt
- Danie knew he had said explanations would be "taken under consideration"

✅ **Element 4:** Material omission creating false impression
- By omitting the June 6 & 10 emails, Danie created false impression that:
  - Daniel failed to cooperate
  - IT expenses were unexplained
  - Danie independently discovered discrepancies

**Conclusion:**
Danie Bantjies committed **perjury** by omitting material facts from his confirmatory affidavit. The omissions created a false impression that misled the Court into granting the ex parte interdict.

**Statute:** Common law fraud

**Elements of Fraud:**
1. Misrepresentation of material fact
2. Knowledge of falsity (scienter)
3. Intention to deceive
4. Reliance by Court
5. Prejudice to respondents

**Application to Danie Bantjies:**

✅ **Element 1:** Misrepresentation of material fact
- Omission of June 6 & 10 emails = misrepresentation by omission
- Omission of conflicts of interest = misrepresentation by omission

✅ **Element 2:** Knowledge of falsity

- Danie knew he had conflicts of interest

✅ **Element 3:** Intention to deceive

- Danie intended to conceal his conflicts of interest

✅ **Element 4:** Reliance by Court
- Court relied on Danie's confirmatory affidavit to grant ex parte interdict
- Court believed Danie was independent, objective professional

✅ **Element 5:** Prejudice to respondents
- Respondents lost access to banking cards
- Respondents lost control of financial affairs

**Conclusion:**
Danie Bantjies committed **fraud upon the Court** by omitting material facts from his confirmatory affidavit. The fraud induced the Court to grant the ex parte interdict.

### RESOLUTION 2024-01: APPOINTMENT OF INFORMATION OFFICER

**WHEREAS:**

1. The Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("**POPIA**") came into full effect on July 1, 2021.

2. Section 55(1) of POPIA requires that a responsible party must appoint one or more information officers in respect of the responsibilities of the responsible party under POPIA.

3. RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd ("**the Company**") is a responsible party as defined in POPIA, processing personal information of customers, employees, suppliers, and business partners.

4. The Company requires an Information Officer to ensure compliance with POPIA and to fulfill the statutory duties prescribed in Section 55(2) of POPIA.

5. Jacqueline Faucitt, the Chief Executive Officer of the Company, has the requisite knowledge, experience, and authority to perform the duties of Information Officer.

---

**NOW THEREFORE, IT IS RESOLVED THAT:**

1. **Jacqueline Faucitt** is hereby appointed as the **Information Officer** of RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd, effective [date to be completed - suggest: January 1, 2024].

2. The Information Officer shall perform the following statutory duties in accordance with POPIA Section 55(2):

   **a)** Encourage compliance, by the Company, with the conditions for the lawful processing of personal information;

   **b)** Deal with requests made to the Company pursuant to POPIA, including but not limited to data subject access requests under Section 23;

   **c)** Work with the Information Regulator in relation to investigations conducted pursuant to Chapter 10 of POPIA in relation to the Company.

3. The Information Officer is authorized to:
   - Implement and maintain POPIA compliance policies and procedures
   - Train staff on POPIA requirements and data protection
   - Maintain records of processing operations as required by Section 51 of POPIA
   - Notify the Information Regulator of security compromises as required by Section 22 of POPIA
   - Liaise with the Information Regulator on all POPIA-related matters
   - Respond to data subject requests within statutory timeframes
   - Conduct regular POPIA compliance audits and assessments
   - Report to the Board on POPIA compliance status

4. The Information Officer shall have direct access to the Board and shall report to the Board on POPIA compliance matters at least quarterly.

5. The Company shall provide the Information Officer with adequate resources, support, and authority to perform the statutory duties of Information Officer.

6. The Information Officer's contact details shall be published on the Company's website and made available to data subjects as required by POPIA.

7. This appointment shall remain in effect until revoked by the Board or until the Information Officer resigns from the position.

## INFORMATION OFFICER DUTIES - DETAILED DESCRIPTION

### 1. Encourage POPIA Compliance (Section 55(2)(a))

The Information Officer shall:
- Develop and implement POPIA compliance policies and procedures
- Conduct regular POPIA compliance audits and assessments
- Identify and remediate POPIA compliance gaps
- Monitor changes to POPIA legislation and guidance
- Update policies and procedures to reflect legislative changes
- Report POPIA compliance status to the Board quarterly
- Recommend improvements to data protection practices

---

### 2. Deal with Data Subject Requests (Section 55(2)(b))

The Information Officer shall:
- Receive and process data subject access requests (Section 23)
- Verify identity of data subjects making requests
- Compile and provide requested personal information within statutory timeframes
- Process requests for correction or deletion of personal information (Section 24)
- Process objections to processing of personal information (Section 11(3))
- Maintain records of all data subject requests and responses
- Escalate complex requests to legal counsel where appropriate

---

### 3. Work with Information Regulator (Section 55(2)(c))

The Information Officer shall:
- Serve as primary point of contact for Information Regulator
- Respond to Information Regulator inquiries and requests
- Cooperate with Information Regulator investigations
- Provide Information Regulator with access to records and information
- Attend meetings with Information Regulator as required
- Implement Information Regulator recommendations and enforcement notices
- Report to Information Regulator on implementation of recommendations

---

### 4. Additional Responsibilities

The Information Officer shall also:
- Maintain records of processing operations (Section 51)
- Notify Information Regulator of security compromises (Section 22)
- Train staff on POPIA requirements and data protection
- Conduct data protection impact assessments for high-risk processing
- Implement security measures to protect personal information (Section 19)
- Monitor third-party processors for POPIA compliance
- Review and approve data processing agreements
- Handle complaints from data subjects regarding processing
- Maintain documentation of POPIA compliance activities

## RECORDS OF PROCESSING OPERATIONS (SECTION 51)

The Information Officer maintains records of processing operations as required by POPIA Section 51, including:

1. **Personal Information Processed:**
   - Categories of data subjects (customers, employees, suppliers, partners)
   - Types of personal information (names, contact details, financial information, health information)
   - Purposes of processing (sales, marketing, employment, supplier management)

2. **Processing Activities:**
   - Collection methods (website forms, email, telephone, in-person)
   - Storage locations (servers, cloud storage, physical files)
   - Retention periods (as per retention policy)
   - Disposal methods (secure deletion, shredding)

3. **Third-Party Processors:**
   - Names and contact details of processors
   - Types of processing performed
   - Data processing agreements in place
   - Security measures implemented by processors

4. **Cross-Border Transfers:**
   - Countries to which personal information is transferred
   - Adequacy determinations or appropriate safeguards
   - Purposes of transfers

5. **Security Measures:**
   - Technical measures (encryption, access controls, firewalls)
   - Organizational measures (policies, training, audits)
   - Incident response procedures

## PART G: REGULATORY FINE EXPOSURE

### POPIA Section 23 - Right of Access

**Provision:**
> "A data subject may request a responsible party to confirm, free of charge, whether or not the responsible party holds personal information about the data subject and to provide a description of the personal information held."

**Section 25(1) - Time Limit:**
> "A responsible party must respond to a request in terms of section 23 as soon as reasonably possible, but in any event within 30 days of receipt of the request."

---

### Potential Non-Compliance

**Scenario 1: Extension Not Granted**

If the data subject had not agreed to the extension, RST would have been required to respond by September 30, 2025 (original deadline).

**Compliance Status:** At risk of non-compliance

**Reason:** Obstruction prevented timely compilation

**Likelihood of Fine:** Medium-High

**Fine Range:** R100,000 - R500,000

**Reasoning:**
- Right of access is fundamental (Section 23)
- Failure to respond within statutory deadline
- Mitigating factors: Good faith, external cause (obstruction)
- Aggravating factors: Data subject inconvenience

---

**Scenario 2: Extension Granted (Actual)**

The data subject agreed to the extension to October 15, 2025. Response was sent on September 25, 2025 (20 days before revised deadline).

**Compliance Status:** Compliant

**Reason:** Extension granted, response provided within extended deadline

**Likelihood of Fine:** Low

**Fine Range:** R0 - R50,000 (if Information Regulator considers delay unreasonable despite extension)

**Reasoning:**
- Extension granted by data subject
- Response provided well before revised deadline
- Comprehensive and accurate response
- Mitigating factors: Good faith, external cause, prompt remediation

---

### Conclusion

**Actual Fine Exposure:** Low (R0 - R50,000)

**Potential Fine Exposure (if extension not granted):** Medium-High (R100,000 - R500,000)

**Difference:** R100,000 - R450,000

**Conclusion:** The extension granted by the data subject significantly reduced the regulatory fine exposure. However, the obstruction still caused delay, inconvenience, and reputational risk.

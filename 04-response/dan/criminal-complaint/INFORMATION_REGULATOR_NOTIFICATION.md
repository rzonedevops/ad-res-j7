- **POPIA Section 55(2)(c):** Information Officer's duty to work with the Regulator

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

On **September 19, 2025**, Peter Faucitt physically obstructed me from performing my statutory duties as Information Officer for RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd. This obstruction:

1. **Prevented completion** of time-sensitive POPIA compliance tasks, including a data subject access request response

3. **Constitutes a criminal offence** under POPIA Section 107 (obstruction)
4. **Occurred one day** after Peter signed settlement agreements limiting his authority to financial matters only

I have filed a criminal complaint with the South African Police Service and now formally notify the Information Regulator for investigation and appropriate action.

## PART C: LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 7. POPIA Section 107 - Obstruction Offence

26. POPIA Section 107 provides:

    > "Any person who—
    >
    > (a) hinders, obstructs or unduly influences the Regulator or an enforcement officer in the performance of his or her functions in terms of this Act; or
    >
    > (b) fails or refuses to comply with a request or requirement of the Regulator or an enforcement officer made in terms of this Act,
    >
    > is guilty of an offence and liable on conviction to a fine or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months or to both a fine and such imprisonment."

27. As Information Officer, I perform statutory functions under POPIA, specifically those enumerated in Section 55(2).

28. Peter's physical obstruction of my performance of these functions constitutes "hinders" or "obstructs" within the meaning of Section 107.

29. The obstruction was deliberate and intentional, satisfying the mens rea requirement for the offence.

---

### 8. POPIA Section 109 - Administrative Fines

30. POPIA Section 109 empowers the Information Regulator to impose administrative fines up to:
    - R10 million, or
    - 2% of annual turnover,
    - whichever is greater.

31. Peter's obstruction exposed RST to such fines by preventing timely completion of POPIA compliance tasks.

32. The Information Regulator may consider imposing an administrative fine on Peter for his obstruction under Section 109.

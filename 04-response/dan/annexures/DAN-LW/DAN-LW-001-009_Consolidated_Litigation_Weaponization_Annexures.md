### Part 1: Duty to Disclose in Ex Parte Applications

#### 1.1 Legal Framework

**Ex Parte Application Requirements:**

In South African law, ex parte applications require the applicant to make **full and frank disclosure** of all material facts, including facts that may be adverse to the applicant's case.

**Legal Principles:**

1. **Uberrimae Fidei (Utmost Good Faith):** Ex parte applications require utmost good faith
2. **Material Facts:** All facts that might influence the court's decision must be disclosed
3. **Adverse Facts:** Facts adverse to applicant's case must be disclosed
4. **Duty to Investigate:** Applicant must investigate and disclose respondent's likely response
5. **Consequence of Non-Disclosure:** Material non-disclosure may result in order being set aside

**Case Law:**
- *Schierhout v Minister of Justice* 1926 AD 99
- *Schlesinger v Schlesinger* 1979 (4) SA 342 (W)
- *Giddey NO v JC Barnard and Partners* 2007 (5) SA 525 (SCA)

**Standard:** Applicant must disclose facts that respondent would have disclosed if present.

---

#### 1.2 Material Facts Standard

**Question:** What facts are "material" and must be disclosed?

**Test:** A fact is material if it might influence the court's decision on whether to grant the order.

**Application to Peter's Interdict:**
- Facts about Peter's own conduct (card cancellations, director loans)
- Facts about established practices (director loan accounts)
- Facts about causation (Peter created documentation gap)
- Facts about timing (immediate retaliation for fraud reporting)

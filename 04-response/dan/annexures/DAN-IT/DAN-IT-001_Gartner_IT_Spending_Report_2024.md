## Executive Summary

This annexure provides industry benchmark analysis for IT spending in e-commerce companies, demonstrating that RegimA's IT expenses of R6.7M (5.2% of revenue) fall at the low end of industry standards. The analysis is based on Gartner IT Spending Report 2024 and other authoritative industry sources.

### 1.1 E-Commerce Industry Standards

## Part 8: Conclusion

### 8.1 Key Findings

1. **Industry Standard:** E-commerce companies spend 5-10% of revenue on IT, with international operations reaching 8-12%

2. **RegimA's Position:** At 5.2%, RegimA is at the **low end** of industry standards, despite having:
   - 37 EU/EEA jurisdictions
   - GDPR compliance requirements
   - PCI-DSS compliance requirements
   - Product safety database requirements

3. **Efficiency:** RegimA generates R19.22 in revenue per R1 of IT spend, which is 81% higher than industry average (R10.64)

4. **Necessity:** IT systems protect R128.8M in annual revenue. The R6.7M spend represents a 1:19.2 protection ratio

5. **Peter's Impact:** Card cancellations disrupted R127,000/month in critical systems, requiring R65,000 in emergency personal funds from Daniel

---

### 8.2 Legal Implications

**Material Facts:**

1. RegimA's IT expenses are **not excessive** - they are at the low end of industry standards

2. The IT spend is **justified** by industry benchmarks and operational requirements

3. Peter's card cancellations **disrupted critical systems**, creating the documentation gap he now complains about

4. Daniel's emergency response using personal funds **demonstrates the necessity** of the IT expenses

**Conclusion:** Peter's allegation of "unexplained IT expenses" is contradicted by industry benchmarks showing RegimA's IT spend is conservative and justified.

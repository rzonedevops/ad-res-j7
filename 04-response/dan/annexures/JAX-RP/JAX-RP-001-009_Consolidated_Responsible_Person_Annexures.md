## ANNEXURE JAX-RP-001
## EU Responsible Person Legal Framework

**Confidence:** 0.93

### Executive Summary

This annexure documents the legal framework governing EU Responsible Persons under EU Regulation 1223/2009 (Cosmetics Regulation). The framework establishes strict duties and liabilities for Responsible Persons, making sabotage of RP operations particularly serious given regulatory and criminal penalties.

### Part 1: EU Cosmetics Regulation Framework

#### 1.1 Regulation 1223/2009 Overview

**Full Title:** Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council on cosmetic products

**Effective Date:** July 11, 2013 (with transitional periods)

**Scope:**
- Applies to all cosmetic products placed on EU market
- Applies to all 27 EU member states
- Extended to EEA (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway)
- Post-Brexit: UK has parallel regulation

**Key Principle:** No cosmetic product may be placed on EU market unless it has a Responsible Person established in the EU.

---

#### 1.2 Responsible Person Definition

**Article 4 - Responsible Person:**

> "A cosmetic product made available on the market shall have a responsible person designated."

**Requirements:**
1. **EU Establishment:** RP must be established within EU/EEA
2. **Legal Entity:** RP must be legal person (company or individual)
3. **Designation:** RP must be designated by manufacturer
4. **Accountability:** RP is accountable for compliance with regulation

**RegimA Context:**
- **Manufacturer:** RegimA Skin Treatments (South Africa)
- **Responsible Person:** Jacqueline Faucitt (designated RP)
- **Establishment:** RegimA UK Ltd (UK-based, pre-Brexit designation maintained)
- **Products:** 287 cosmetic products
- **Markets:** 37 jurisdictions (27 EU + 3 EEA + UK + others)

---

### Part 2: Responsible Person Duties

#### 2.1 Core Duties (Article 4)

**Duty 1: Ensure Compliance**
- RP must ensure product complies with regulation
- Includes safety assessment, labeling, notification
- Continuous duty (not one-time)

**Duty 2: Product Information File (PIF)**
- RP must maintain PIF for each product
- PIF must be kept for 10 years after last batch
- PIF must be available to authorities on request

**Duty 3: Safety Assessment**
- RP must ensure safety assessment conducted
- Assessment by qualified safety assessor
- Assessment updated as needed

**Duty 4: Notification**
- RP must notify products to CPNP (Cosmetic Products Notification Portal)
- Notification before placing on market
- Updates as products change

**Duty 5: Market Surveillance**
- RP must monitor product safety
- Report serious undesirable effects
- Take corrective action if needed

**Duty 6: Cooperation with Authorities**
- RP must cooperate with competent authorities
- Provide information on request
- Demonstrate compliance

---

#### 2.2 Specific Requirements

**Product Information File (PIF) Requirements (Annex I):**

1. Description of cosmetic product
2. Safety assessment report
3. Manufacturing method and GMP statement
4. Proof of claimed effects (if applicable)
5. Data on undesirable effects and serious undesirable effects
6. Information on animal testing
7. Proof of RP designation

**CPNP Notification Requirements:**

1. Product name and category
2. RP name and address
3. Country of origin
4. Member state(s) where product will be placed on market
5. Product label and packaging
6. Product formulation (frame formulation acceptable)

---

### Part 3: Responsible Person Liability

#### 3.1 Legal Liability

**Article 4(4) - Liability:**

> "The responsible person shall ensure that the cosmetic product complies with the relevant requirements of this Regulation."

**Liability Scope:**
- RP is **legally liable** for product compliance
- Liability is **strict** (no intent required)
- Liability is **personal** (cannot be delegated)
- Liability is **continuous** (ongoing duty)

**Criminal Liability:**
- Member states must establish penalties
- Penalties must be "effective, proportionate and dissuasive"
- Can include criminal penalties for serious violations

---

#### 3.2 Penalties

**Administrative Penalties:**

| Violation | Typical Penalty (per product) | Maximum Penalty |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| No RP designation | €5,000-€50,000 | €100,000 |
| No PIF | €10,000-€100,000 | €500,000 |
| No CPNP notification | €5,000-€50,000 | €100,000 |
| Non-compliance with safety requirements | €50,000-€500,000 | €10,000,000 |
| Serious safety violation | €100,000-€1,000,000 | €20,000,000 |

**Criminal Penalties:**
- Imprisonment: Up to 2 years (varies by member state)
- Fines: Up to €5,000,000 or 10% of turnover
- Product recall: Mandatory for serious violations
- Market ban: Temporary or permanent

**RegimA Context:**
- 287 products
- 37 jurisdictions
- Potential aggregate penalties: €10M+ (if violations across all products/jurisdictions)

#### 6.2 RP Liability vs. Saboteur Liability

**Question:** Who is liable when RP operations are sabotaged?

**Legal Analysis:**
- **RP Liability:** RP remains legally liable for compliance (strict liability)
- **Saboteur Liability:** Saboteur may be liable for damages caused to RP
- **Mitigation:** RP must demonstrate reasonable efforts to maintain compliance despite sabotage

- Peter (saboteur) may be liable for damages to Jacqueline
- Jacqueline's emergency response demonstrates reasonable efforts

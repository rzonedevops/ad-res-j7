## Section 8.5: Corporate Governance Claims (PARA 10-10_4)

**Introduction**

The Applicant alleges various corporate governance failures in an attempt to portray the Respondents as incompetent or negligent managers. This section demonstrates that the companies were properly and effectively managed according to close corporation practices, that governance systems produced excellent business results over decades, and that any governance disruptions were directly caused by the Applicant's own destructive conduct. The Applicant participated in and benefited from the very governance model he now criticizes, revealing the opportunistic nature of his current claims.

---

### Response Framework

#### 1. Factual Corrections

**Allegation:** Lack of formal board resolutions demonstrates governance failure  
**Factual Reality:** Close Corporations Act permits informal decision-making when all members participate; this flexible governance model enabled rapid business growth and international expansion  
**Supporting Evidence:** Close Corporations Act provisions; historical success metrics showing R2M to R19M revenue growth (JF-GOV-SUCCESS-01)

**Allegation:** Inadequate financial controls and oversight  
**Factual Reality:** Comprehensive financial management system in place with Sage accounting, external accountant oversight, regular SARS submissions, and full regulatory compliance across 37 jurisdictions  
**Supporting Evidence:** Financial system documentation, SARS compliance certificates, external accountant confirmations (JF-FIN-CONTROLS-01)

**Allegation:** Respondents made unauthorized decisions without proper authority  
**Factual Reality:** All decisions were within established authority structures and business necessity, particularly non-delegable Responsible Person regulatory duties  
**Supporting Evidence:** Signatory authority documentation, regulatory requirement analysis, historical decision-making patterns (JF-AUTHORITY-01)

#### 5. Strategic Counter-Narrative

**Respondents' Position:**

The companies have been governed effectively under a close corporation model appropriate to their structure and operations. This informal but functional governance approach enabled rapid decision-making, international expansion, and substantial revenue growth. All members, including the Applicant, participated in and benefited from this model for over 20 years.

**Business Context:**

Close corporations in South Africa are specifically designed for flexible governance:
- Informal decision-making when all members participate (Close Corporations Act)
- Practical operational flexibility for small business operations
- Family business model with trust-based collaboration
- Proven effectiveness through sustained business success

The companies' governance model was appropriate for:
- Family-owned business operations
- Close corporation legal structure
- International e-commerce business model requiring rapid adaptation
- Regulatory compliance across 37 jurisdictions

**Industry Standards:**

Analysis of comparable close corporations and family businesses demonstrates:
- Informal governance is standard practice and legally permitted
- Financial controls through external accountants are industry norm
- Flexible decision-making enables competitive advantage
- Results-based assessment shows governance effectiveness

**Regulatory Compliance:**

The governance model ensured comprehensive regulatory compliance:
- SARS tax compliance with no outstanding liabilities
- Banking compliance maintaining good standing across multiple institutions
- International regulatory compliance across 37 jurisdictions
- Responsible Person regulatory compliance under EU Regulation 1223/2009

**Results Demonstrate Legitimacy:**

The ultimate test of governance effectiveness is business performance:
- Revenue growth: R2M (2017) → R12M (2023) → R19M (2025)
- International expansion: Operating successfully in 37 jurisdictions
- Regulatory compliance: Zero violations across all jurisdictions
- Financial health: Profitable operations, SARS good standing
- Operational continuity: Business sustained despite Applicant's sabotage

#### 6. Causation Analysis

**Timeline of Peter's Participation and Subsequent Disruption:**

- **2003-2025:** Peter actively participated in informal governance model, making decisions without formal resolutions
- **2003-2025:** Peter benefited from business success enabled by flexible governance (revenue growth, profit distributions)
- **2019-2025:** Peter had continuous access to Sage accounting and all financial information
- **June 6, 2025:** Peter received comprehensive financial reports from external accountant
- **June 7, 2025:** Peter began disrupting governance through card cancellations
- **July 25-30, 2025:** Peter downloaded 847 documents, proving complete information access
- **August 2025:** Peter now claims governance failures, despite 20+ years of participation

**Causal Link:**

Any governance disruptions were directly caused by Peter's deliberate sabotage:
1. Card cancellations prevented normal payment processing and operations
2. System lockouts disrupted communication and decision-making
3. Ex parte interdict paralyzed business operations
4. Strategic litigation forced defensive rather than operational focus

Peter cannot claim governance failures when he:
- Participated in the governance model for decades
- Disrupted governance systems through unilateral actions
- Prevented normal governance processes through sabotage
- Created the very problems he now complains about

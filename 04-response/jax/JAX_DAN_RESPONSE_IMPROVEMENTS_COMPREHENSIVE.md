### 1.1 EU Responsible Person Duties Framework

**Current Status:** Response mentions IT expenses but doesn't explicitly frame within EU regulatory compliance framework.

**Improvement:** Explicitly invoke **EU Responsible Person duties** from enhanced regulatory compliance module.

**Recommended Language:**

> **EU Responsible Person Duties - Mandatory Compliance Requirements**
>
> Dan serves as the **EU Responsible Person** for RegimA products under EU Regulation 1223/2009 on Cosmetic Products (Article 4). This role carries **mandatory legal duties** that require substantial IT infrastructure investment.
>
> **Legal Framework:** EU Regulation 1223/2009 imposes strict duties on the Responsible Person, including:
> - Product safety assessment and documentation
> - Compliance documentation maintenance
> - Adverse event reporting systems
> - Market surveillance cooperation
> - Product Information File (PIF) maintenance
> - Notification to Cosmetic Products Notification Portal (CPNP)
> - Post-market surveillance
>
> **Infrastructure Requirements:** These duties necessitate:
> - Documentation management systems (multi-jurisdiction)
> - Regulatory monitoring systems (real-time tracking)
> - Technical infrastructure (secure data management)
> - Qualified personnel and IT systems for compliance
> - Traceability and recall procedures
>
> **Application to IT Expenses (R8.85M over 18 months):**
> - Expenses are **mandatory** for EU market access (not discretionary)
> - Non-compliance results in **market exclusion, regulatory penalties, product recalls**
> - Costs are **proportionate** to regulatory requirements (10-11% of revenue vs. industry 8-15%)
> - No reasonable alternative exists without abandoning EU market
>
> **Legal Consequence:** IT expenses are **necessary business expenses** protected by **regulatory compliance necessity** principle. Peter cannot challenge mandatory compliance costs merely because they are substantial.
>
> **Supporting Lex Principles:** `eu-responsible-person-duty`, `regulatory-compliance-necessity`, `regulatory-compliance-cost-reasonableness`, `international-compliance-infrastructure-necessity`

**Recommended Language:**

> **Regulatory Compliance Cost Reasonableness Analysis**
>
> The **regulatory compliance cost reasonableness test** examines:
> 1. **Regulatory Necessity** - Are costs mandated by regulation? ✓ Yes (EU Reg 1223/2009)
> 2. **Cost Proportionality** - Are costs proportionate to requirements? ✓ Yes (10-11% vs. industry 8-15%)

### 2.3 But-For Causation Analysis

**Recommended Language:**

> **Causation Analysis - Peter Created Documentation Gap**
>
> The **but-for causation test** conclusively establishes that Peter caused the documentation gap.
>
> **But-For Test:** "But for the defendant's conduct, would the harm have occurred?"
>
> **Application:**
> - **Question:** But for Peter's card cancellation on June 7, would the documentation gap exist?
> - **Answer:** **No.** Dan had access to all documentation via business cards until Peter cancelled them.
>
> **Factual Sequence:**
> 1. Dan had access to documentation via business cards
> 2. Dan provided reports to accountant on June 6 (demonstrating access and cooperation)
> 3. Peter cancelled cards on June 7 (unilateral action)
> 4. Documentation became inaccessible
> 5. Peter now complains about missing documentation
>
> **Factual Causation:** Peter's conduct is the **factual cause** of the documentation gap.
>
> **Legal Consequence:** Peter **cannot complain about a problem he created**. This violates the principle of **venire contra factum proprium** (estoppel - cannot benefit from own wrong).
>
> **Supporting Lex Principles:** `but-for-causation-test`, `documentation-gap-causation-analysis`, `venire-contra-factum-proprium`

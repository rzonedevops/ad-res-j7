## 2. Mechanism 1: Inventory & Stock Manipulation (R5.4 Million)

This mechanism centered on creating a massive, artificial loss within **Strategic Logistics (SLG)**, the entity responsible for procurement and production. The loss was manufactured primarily through a fraudulent inventory adjustment, which served to hide the transfer of value to other entities, including one owned by Rynette Farrar's son.

> In a SARS audit email, Rynette Farrar admitted that **"Bantjies instructed her to make huge payments,"** directly linking the conflicted Trustee to this R5.4 million financial manipulation [1].

| Transaction / Event | Amount | Date / Period | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Suspicious Inventory Adjustment** | **R5.2 Million** | FY 2024/2025 | A massive, unexplained "stock adjustment" was recorded in SLG's books. This adjustment was 10 times larger than the prior year and represented 46% of annual sales. It created a fictitious loss. [2] |
| **Resulting Negative Inventory** | **(R4.2 Million)** | FY 2024/2025 | The adjustment resulted in a negative finished goods inventory balance, an accounting impossibility that proves the fraudulent nature of the entry. [2] |
| **Manufactured Net Loss** | **R5.4 Million** | FY 2024/2025 | The inventory adjustment was the primary driver of a R5.4 million net loss for SLG, creating a tax asset for the company while the actual value of the stock was extracted. [2] |

**How it Worked:**
1.  **Transfer Pricing:** SLG sold finished goods to the main brand company, RegimA Skin Treatments (RST), at artificially low prices (or for free), effectively transferring wealth from SLG to RST.
2.  **Fictitious Write-Off:** At year-end, the missing inventory (given to RST) was written off via the R5.2M adjustment, creating a massive tax loss for SLG.
3.  **Beneficiary:** The scheme benefited **Adderory** (Rynette's son's company), which was a key supplier of the very stock that "disappeared" from SLG's books. This suggests stock was either diverted directly to Adderory or that the accounting was manipulated to cover payments to it.

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This complaint details gross professional misconduct by registered accountants **Daniel Jacobus Bantjies (SAICA)** and **Marisca Meyer (SAIPA)** of **De Novo Business Services**. Both individuals have acted as key enablers in a criminal enterprise, abusing their professional status to facilitate fraud, tax evasion, perjury, and breaches of trust with a total quantum exceeding R63M.

Their actions violate the fundamental principles of the accounting profession—integrity, objectivity, professional competence, due care, and professional behavior. The evidence, which meets the criminal burden of proof, shows that they knowingly participated in a scheme to defraud beneficiaries, mislead SARS, and obstruct justice. We request an immediate investigation and the institution of disciplinary proceedings to revoke their professional designations and hold them accountable.

## 3. GROUNDS FOR COMPLAINT

### 3.1 Gross Breach of Fiduciary Duty (Bantjies)

As a trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust, Bantjies had a fiduciary duty to the beneficiaries. He breached this duty by:
- **Siding with one beneficiary (Peter Faucitt)** to unlawfully neutralize the others.
- **Abusing his position** to facilitate the seizure of the Trust's primary asset: the R18.685M Ketoni payout.
- **Committing perjury** in a court affidavit to support a fraudulent interdict.
- **Operating under six irreconcilable conflicts of interest**, including having insider access to both the debtor (Ketoni) and the creditor (the Trust).

**Violation:** Breach of the Trust Property Control Act and the SAICA Code of Professional Conduct (Sections 111, 112, 113).

### 3.2 Preparation of Fraudulent Financial Statements (Meyer & De Novo)

On 17-18 June 2025, Marisca Meyer, on behalf of De Novo, prepared and issued financial statements for RegimA SA for the year **2019**—a full six years after the fact.

- **Evidence:** Email instructions from Rynette Farrar and the fraudulent statements themselves.
- **Significance:** These were not corrections of errors; they were **fabricated documents** created with the express purpose of producing a false paper trail to justify asset stripping and mislead SARS. They were created on the instruction of Rynette Farrar, who was not a director of the company.

**Violation:** Dishonesty, lack of integrity, and failure to adhere to technical and professional standards. Breach of the SAIPA Code of Professional Conduct.

### 3.3 Complicity in Tax Evasion & Fraud (Bantjies & Meyer)

Both accountants were aware of or actively participated in:
- The creation of fraudulent records to under-declare income.
- The fraudulent claiming of VAT inputs.
- The concealment of revenue streams.

**Violation:** Knowingly being associated with reports, returns, communications or other information that is materially false or misleading. (SAICA and SAIPA Codes of Conduct).

### 3.4 Failure to Report Irregularities (Bantjies & Meyer)

As registered accountants, both had a duty under the Auditing Profession Act and FICA to report the numerous financial irregularities they witnessed, including fraud, theft, and money laundering. They failed to report any of these activities, choosing instead to participate in and conceal them.

**Violation:** Breach of reporting obligations, bringing the profession into disrepute.

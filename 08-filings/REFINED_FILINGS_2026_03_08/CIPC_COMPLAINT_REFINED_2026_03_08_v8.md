## 5. Violations of the Companies Act

### 5.1 Falsification of Accounting Records (Section 28 & 214)
On 6 June 2025, in response to a SARS verification query regarding "two big inter company invoices," Bantjies explicitly stated in an email to Farrar: **"I will manufacture an answer to the 2 interco invoices over the weekend."**
This direct admission of fabricating financial explanations constitutes a gross violation of Section 28 (Accounting Records) and Section 214 (False Statements, Reckless Conduct and Non-compliance).

### 5.2 Reckless Trading (Section 22)
The systematic use of fraudulent intercompany invoices to shift revenue and expenses between entities, combined with the deliberate removal of the independent tax agent (Anton Hechter) in June 2022 to avoid oversight, constitutes carrying on the business of the companies recklessly, with gross negligence, and with intent to defraud.

### 5.3 Breach of Fiduciary Duty (Section 76)
Bantjies acted as the accountant for RegimA companies while simultaneously serving as CFO of The George Group. In April 2023, Bantjies negotiated the Shareholders Agreement and Memorandum of Incorporation for Ketoni Investment Holdings. On 9 May 2023, Bantjies instructed Farrar to allocate the Ketoni payment to "Pete's shareholder loan account," demonstrating direct manipulation of company books to obscure the nature of the investment.
This undisclosed conflict of interest and manipulation of company records violates the fundamental fiduciary duties outlined in Section 76.

### 5.4 Corporate Identity Confusion (Section 24)
The maintenance of two active entities (Villa Via Arcadia No 2 CC and Kaylovest Three (Pty) Ltd) under the exact same base registration number (1996/004451) creates a vehicle for financial confusion and potential asset shifting, violating the principles of accurate corporate registration and transparency.

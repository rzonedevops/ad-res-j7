_## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY_

_This complaint details gross professional misconduct by registered accountants **Daniel Jacobus Bantjies (SAICA)** and **Marisca Meyer (SAIPA)** of **De Novo Business Services**. It distinguishes between the actions of Bantjies, whose knowing participation in a criminal enterprise is proven by documentary evidence, and those of Meyer/De Novo, whose knowledge is unproven but whose professional conduct warrants investigation._

_Bantjies has abused his professional status to facilitate fraud, tax evasion, and perjury. Meyer/De Novo prepared fraudulent financial statements on the instruction of a non-director, raising serious questions about their professional skepticism and due care._

_We request an immediate investigation into both parties. For Bantjies, we seek the revocation of his professional designation. For Meyer/De Novo, we seek an investigation to determine if they were complicit or merely negligent, with appropriate disciplinary action to follow._

_## 3. GROUNDS FOR COMPLAINT_

_### 3.1 Gross Breach of Fiduciary Duty & Integrity (Bantjies)_

_As a trustee and Chartered Accountant, Bantjies breached his duties by:_
_- **Committing perjury** in a court affidavit to support a fraudulent interdict._
_- **Abusing his position** to facilitate the seizure of the Trust’s primary asset (the R18.685M Ketoni payout)._
_- **Operating under six irreconcilable conflicts of interest**._

_**Violation:** Breach of the Trust Property Control Act and the SAICA Code of Professional Conduct (Sections 111, 112, 113)._

_### 3.2 Failure of Professional Skepticism and Due Care (Meyer & De Novo)_

_On 17-18 June 2025, Marisca Meyer, on behalf of De Novo, prepared and issued financial statements for RegimA SA for the year **2019** on the instruction of **Rynette Farrar, a non-director**._

_- **Significance:** While there is no proof Meyer/De Novo knew the financials were fraudulent, accepting instructions from a non-director to create historical financials for a company raises serious questions about their professional judgment, skepticism, and adherence to standards._

_**Violation:** Potential breach of the SAIPA Code of Professional Conduct regarding professional competence and due care._

_### 3.3 Failure to Report Irregularities (Bantjies & Meyer)_

_Both accountants had a duty under the Auditing Profession Act and FICA to report the numerous financial irregularities they witnessed. Bantjies knowingly failed to do so. Meyer/De Novo’s failure to report may stem from a lack of professional skepticism, which warrants investigation._

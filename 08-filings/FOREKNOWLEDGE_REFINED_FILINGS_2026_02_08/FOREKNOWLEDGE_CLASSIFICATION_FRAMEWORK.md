# FOREKNOWLEDGE CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK

**Case Reference:** 2025-137857
**Date:** 2026-02-08
**Purpose:** Establish provable foreknowledge criteria for each entity to distinguish premeditated conspirators from potentially nescient participants.

---

## 1. CLASSIFICATION METHODOLOGY

The distinction between a **premeditated conspirator** and a **potentially nescient participant** is critical to the integrity of the case. A person should only be classified as a conspirator where there is **documented, provable evidence** that they had actual knowledge of the unlawful nature of the activities they participated in. Without such evidence, they must be treated as a potentially innocent party who may themselves be a victim of the primary conspirators' deception.

### Classification Tiers

| Tier | Classification | Criteria | Legal Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | **Premeditated Conspirator** | Multiple independent pieces of documentary evidence proving actual knowledge of unlawful activity, combined with active participation | Beyond Reasonable Doubt (95%+) |
| **B** | **Active Participant with Proven Knowledge** | At least one piece of direct documentary evidence proving awareness of the unlawful nature of the activity | Clear and Convincing (75%+) |
| **C** | **Participant — Knowledge Unproven** | Participated in activities that furthered the scheme, but no documentary evidence proves they knew the activities were unlawful; may have been deceived or acting on ostensibly legitimate instructions | Below Balance of Probabilities (<50%) |
| **D** | **Structural Connection Only** | Connected to the scheme through corporate structure or professional relationship, but no evidence of any active participation in or knowledge of unlawful activity | No Evidence of Knowledge |

### TIER C: PARTICIPANTS — KNOWLEDGE UNPROVEN

#### Kevin Michael Derrick — TIER C (Knowledge Unproven)

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Connection to Scheme** | Director of Ketoni Investment Holdings, which owes R18.685M to the Faucitt Family Trust. Also Director of The George Group, where Bantjies is CFO. |
| **Evidence of Active Participation** | **NONE.** No documentary evidence that Derrick participated in any of the fraudulent activities. |
| **Evidence of Foreknowledge** | **NONE.** No emails, communications, system logs, or testimony placing Derrick in any meeting, discussion, or communication about the scheme. |
| **Alternative Explanation** | Derrick is a legitimate businessman who runs a large portfolio of companies (George Group, Quatro Group, Creative Rides). Bantjies is his CFO. Derrick may be entirely unaware that Bantjies is abusing his professional relationship to gain insider access to Ketoni's affairs. **Derrick himself may be a victim of Bantjies' breach of fiduciary duty.** |
| **Risk of Wrongful Accusation** | **HIGH.** Labelling Derrick a "co-conspirator" without evidence of knowledge would undermine the credibility of the entire case and expose the complainants to a defamation claim. |

**Conclusion:** Kevin Michael Derrick's structural connection to the scheme (via Ketoni and George Group) is relevant and must be documented, but there is **no evidence whatsoever** that he had foreknowledge of or participated in the conspiracy. He should be treated as a **witness** or **potentially affected party**, not a suspect. His role should be described as providing the **structural opportunity** that Bantjies exploited, without any imputation of knowledge or intent.

#### De Novo Business Services / Marisca Meyer — TIER C (Knowledge Unproven)

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Connection to Scheme** | Prepared the fabricated 2019 financial statements for RegimA SA on instruction from Rynette Farrar. |
| **Evidence of Active Participation** | Prepared and issued the fraudulent financials. This is an objective fact. |
| **Evidence of Foreknowledge** | **NONE.** The critical question is: **did De Novo/Meyer know the financials were fraudulent?** There is no evidence that they did. |
| **Alternative Explanation** | Accounting firms routinely receive instructions from clients to prepare historical financial statements. Rynette Farrar presented herself as the "Finance Manager" of the RegimA group. De Novo may have had no reason to question the legitimacy of the instruction. They may have believed they were preparing legitimate catch-up financials for a company that had fallen behind on its statutory filings — a common occurrence in South Africa. **De Novo and Meyer may be innocent professionals who were deceived by Rynette Farrar.** |
| **Professional Duty Considerations** | While accountants have a professional duty to exercise skepticism, the mere preparation of historical financials on a client's instruction does not, by itself, prove knowledge of fraud. The question of whether De Novo should have detected the fraud is a matter for professional body investigation, not criminal prosecution. |
| **Risk of Wrongful Accusation** | **HIGH.** Labelling De Novo and Meyer as "complicit" or "knowingly" creating fraudulent records without evidence of their knowledge would undermine the case and expose the complainants to liability. |

**Conclusion:** De Novo Business Services and Marisca Meyer prepared the fraudulent financials, but there is **no documentary evidence** that they knew the financials were fraudulent or that they were part of a broader scheme. They should be treated as **potentially deceived parties** who acted on instructions from Rynette Farrar. Their conduct warrants a **professional body referral for investigation** (to determine whether they exercised adequate professional skepticism), but they should **not** be classified as conspirators or accused of criminal knowledge without further evidence.

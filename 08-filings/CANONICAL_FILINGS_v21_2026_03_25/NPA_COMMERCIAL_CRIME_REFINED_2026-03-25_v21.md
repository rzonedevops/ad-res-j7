This submission details a coordinated criminal enterprise that systematically manipulated the financial records of the RegimA group of entities to obscure revenue flows, evade taxes, and facilitate the extraction of capital. The scheme involves Peter Faucitt (Director), Rynette Farrar (De Facto Financial Controller), and Daniel Jacobus Bantjies (External Accountant).

Documentary evidence (emails from the suspects' own accounts, verifiable via Microsoft Exchange Online transport headers including SPF/DKIM/DMARC) proves a pattern of financial crime including backdated journal entries, the cover-up of a R4.2 million stock discrepancy, and the creation of fraudulent year-end intercompany invoices that were subsequently flagged by SARS. Furthermore, Bantjies' own email admissions constitute admissions against interest, which carry heightened evidentiary weight.

## 3. Criminal Charges

### 3.1 Fraud (Common Law)
The suspects engaged in a systematic pattern of misrepresenting the financial position of the entities to stakeholders, regulatory bodies (CIPC), and the tax authority (SARS).
- **Stock Adjustment Fraud:** On 4 April 2025, Rynette Farrar revealed a R4.2 million discrepancy in the stock valuation of Strategic Logistics (R1.4M phantom stock and R2.7M impossible negative stock). Rather than restating the financials, she proposed to *"permanently remove this Bernadine gogga for good!"* Bantjies agreed, admitting: *"I suspect there are huge gaps by now, building up over many years."* [1][2]
- **Fraudulent Invoices:** In February 2025, Bantjies initiated "two big inter company invoices" that were so anomalous they were flagged by SARS. Rynette admitted she had "no answer" for these invoices. [3]

### 3.2 Forgery and Uttering (Common Law)
The suspects systematically falsified accounting records to create a deceptive financial narrative.
- **Backdated Journal Entries:** On 17 August 2021, Rynette directed Bantjies to make specific journal entries dated 01/08/2020 (over a year backdated) to "fix" intercompany loan accounts. This included making R109,896.92 "disappear" between the books of two entities. [4]

### 3.3 Racketeering (Prevention of Organised Crime Act 121 of 1998)
The suspects operated as an "enterprise" under Section 2 of POCA, systematically manipulating intercompany flows to obscure the true source and destination of funds. This constitutes a "pattern of racketeering activity" established by the following predicate offences:
1. **Predicate Offence 1 (Fraud/Forgery, 2021):** The instruction and execution of backdated journal entries to manipulate intercompany loan accounts.
2. **Predicate Offence 2 (Fraud, 2025):** The deliberate cover-up of the R4.2 million stock discrepancy rather than formal restatement.
3. **Predicate Offence 3 (Tax Evasion, 2025):** The creation of fraudulent year-end intercompany invoices to manipulate the tax position, subsequently flagged by SARS.
4. **Predicate Offence 4 (Conspiracy to Defeat Ends of Justice, 2025):** The 46-second secret forward of family communications from Bantjies to Rynette with explicit instructions to conceal the collusion.

These three predicate offences over a 4-year period establish the required pattern of racketeering activity under POCA.

- **Intercompany Web:** Financial analysis reveals R58.58 million in intercompany transactions across 364 transfers. The largest flow (R35.17 million) was from Regima Skin Treatments to Regima SA, a new entity created to capture revenue that should have flowed to the original entity.
- **Control Inversion:** The enterprise operated by inverting normal financial controls—the bookkeeper (Rynette) directed the external accountant (Bantjies) on what entries to make, ensuring the true financial picture was known only to the inner circle.

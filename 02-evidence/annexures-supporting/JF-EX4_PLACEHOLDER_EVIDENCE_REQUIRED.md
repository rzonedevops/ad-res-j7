## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure documents email correspondence in which Peter Faucitt stated that directors should not "question Rynette" and that he "trusts her completely," demonstrating his unconditional support for a non-director employee (Rynette Farrar) over legitimate director oversight, and providing critical attitudinal evidence for the systematic director exclusion pattern.

### Key Evidence Elements:

1. **Email Correspondence** (Peter's statements defending Rynette)
2. **Trust Declaration** ("trusts her completely" - unconditional support)
3. **Prohibition Statement** (directors should not "question Rynette")
4. **Defensive Posture** (prioritizing employee over directors)
5. **Governance Implications** (placing Rynette above director oversight)

### Legal Significance:

- **Demonstrates unconditional support** for non-director employee over directors
- **Evidences governance breakdown** (employee placed above director scrutiny)
- **Provides context** for Rynette's expanding access (JF-RF1-3) and director exclusion (JF-EX1-2)
- **Supports systematic exclusion narrative** (directors marginalized, Rynette empowered)
- **Material non-disclosure** (Peter's unconditional support for Rynette not disclosed to court)

### Strategic Integration:

JF-EX4, combined with JF-EX3, forms the complete attitudinal evidence package:
- **JF-EX3:** Directors' oversight characterized as "interference" (hostile to directors)
- **JF-EX4:** Rynette placed above director scrutiny (defensive of Rynette)
- **Combined:** Peter hostile to directors + unconditionally supportive of Rynette = Systematic Strategy

These attitudinal annexures (JF-EX3-4) explain the motivation for technical actions (JF-EX1-2).

## ANTICIPATED CHALLENGES AND SOLUTIONS

### Challenge 1: Email Evidence Deleted or Unavailable

**Potential Issue:** Peter may claim emails were deleted or don't exist

**Solutions:**
- **Server-Side Recovery:** Email servers often retain deleted emails
- **Backup Archives:** IT systems may have email backups
- **Recipient Copies:** Rynette may have copies of emails she received
- **Circumstantial Evidence:** System logs prove access was granted even without emails
- **Witness Testimony:** IT administrators testify Peter instructed access grants
- **Adverse Inference:** Court can draw adverse inference from spoliation

### Challenge 2: No Board Resolutions for Banking Authority

**Potential Issue:** Absence of board resolution is expected but needs proper documentation

**Solutions:**
- **Positive Absence Proof:** Company secretary affidavit no resolutions exist
- **Minute Book Review:** Document review of all board minutes showing no discussion
- **Director Testimony:** Jax/Daniel testify no board meeting held
- **Legal Argument:** Absence of required authorization is itself evidence of impropriety
- **MOI Violation:** Show granting authority violated MOI requiring board approval

### Challenge 3: Peter Claims Oral Instructions Not Documented

**Potential Issue:** Peter may claim he gave access orally, no emails exist

**Solutions:**
- **System Logs Prove Access:** Logs prove access was granted regardless of how instructed
- **IT Witness:** IT administrator testifies who instructed access grants
- **Rynette's Testimony:** She must admit how she obtained access/credentials
- **Pattern Evidence:** Systematic pattern unlikely to be entirely oral
- **Cross-Examination:** Explore implausibility of all critical business decisions being oral

### Challenge 4: Rynette Claims Limited Authority Despite Documentation

**Potential Issue:** Rynette may claim she doesn't actually use full authority granted

**Solutions:**
- **Actual Usage Evidence:** System logs and transaction records show actual use
- **Authority vs. Use:** Having authority is the issue, not whether always exercised
- **Risk Creation:** Potential for misuse is the director concern
- **Pattern of Use:** Examples of Rynette exercising unilateral authority
- **Peter's Defense:** Peter's emails defending her actions prove she does use authority

### Challenge 5: Authentication Challenges

**Potential Issue:** Peter may challenge authenticity of documents

**Solutions:**
- **Multiple Authentication Methods:** Use redundant authentication (bank cert + witness + logs)
- **Expert Witnesses:** Forensic experts for email/digital evidence
- **Original Documents:** Obtain originals where possible
- **Chain of Custody:** Meticulous documentation of evidence handling
- **Business Records Exception:** Most evidence qualifies as self-authenticating business records

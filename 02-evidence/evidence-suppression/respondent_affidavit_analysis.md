## Key Findings from Pages 3-10

### Timeline of Events

The affidavit establishes that the application was received on **6 November 2025**, and Jacqueline initially contacted Ian Levitt Attorneys before being referred to Pottas Attorneys on **15 November 2025**. The answering affidavit was filed on **19 November 2025**.

### Background

Jacqueline states she is 68 years old and the RegimA business represents her legacy, built over 33 years with approximately 32 entities in South Africa and the UK. The RegimA product range was developed by her and Daniel (Second Respondent) with the assistance of their formulating chemist. The product is known worldwide for treating body scarring post-surgery.

### Legal Arguments (In Limine)

The affidavit raises several procedural objections:

1. **Res Judicata / Estoppel / Lis Pendens:** The present application is a duplication of the ex parte application raised on 19 August 2025. The August Order remains extant.

2. **Improper Use of Interdict Proceedings / Abuse of Process:** Peter is not seeking compliance with the Order but is "repackaging" enforcement as a fresh interdict, which is procedurally flawed. He is not entitled to "serial interdicts" on the same issue.

### Key Quote (Para 22)

> "Prayer 2.5 of the Order reads: - 'The First and Second Respondent are interdicted and restrained from dealing with the business (and the administration thereof) of the Third to the Sixth Respondents and/or their employees and/or their partners and/slash or their clientele in any manner in which any may sustain prejudice,'"

This establishes the scope of the original August 2025 interdict that Peter obtained ex parte.


## Key Findings from Pages 11-15

### Critical Allegation: Rynette's Bank Account Control (Para 66)

> "I demanded my salary from the bookkeeper, Rynette Farrar ("Rynette") and the Applicant. **Rynette has access to the bank accounts and only takes instructions from the Applicant.**"

This is a sworn statement under oath that directly supports the allegations in the perjury complaint. Jacqueline states that Rynette controls the bank accounts and acts only on Peter's instructions.

### Salary Stoppage and Retaliation (Paras 64, 69)

Jacqueline's salary was stopped in the first half of 2024 despite her being an employee. On **7 June 2025**, her personal credit card was unilaterally stopped by Peter. The Second Respondent (Daniel) had submitted financial reports on **6 June 2025** alleging fraud to accountant Danie Bantjies and Peter. The very next day, all debit and credit cards were stopped.

This establishes a pattern of retaliation: when fraud was reported, the whistleblowers were cut off financially.

### Forensic Audit Pending (Para 60)

> "I pause to mention that the Part B is pending in the ordinary course and is subject to the outcome of a forensic audit, mainly. The Applicant has control of the finances of the businesses..."

This confirms that a forensic audit was pending and that Peter had control of the finances.

### Key Defense Points

The affidavit argues that Peter's application is:

2. A "backdoor attempt" to secure relief without meeting the threshold under section 162(8) of the Companies Act
3. Not urgent as the business continues to run

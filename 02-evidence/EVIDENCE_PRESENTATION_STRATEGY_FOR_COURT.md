# EVIDENCE PRESENTATION STRATEGY FOR COURT

## Document Overview

**Purpose:** Comprehensive strategy for presenting evidence in court proceedings  
**Focus:** Linking all evidence to the central revelation about platform ownership and revenue stream hijacking  
**Date Created:** October 23, 2025  
**Case:** Peter Andrew Faucitt v. Jacqueline Faucitt et al. (Case No: 2025-137857)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Central Revelation

The core of this case revolves around a fundamental misrepresentation of business operations:

1. **Platform Ownership:** The Shopify e-commerce platform is owned and paid for by **RegimA Zone Ltd** (UK company owned by Dan & Kay)
2. **Platform Costs:** RegimA Zone Ltd paid R140,000 - R280,000 in Shopify subscription costs over 28 months
3. **Revenue Generation:** RWD ZA generated R2.94M - R6.88M in revenue using this platform
4. **No Compensation:** RWD ZA never compensated RegimA Zone Ltd for platform usage
5. **No Independent Revenue:** RWD ZA has no independent revenue stream - all revenue was generated on Dan & Kay's platform

### Strategic Implication

This revelation transforms the case from defensive responses to allegations into a compelling counter-narrative of **systematic unjust enrichment** by RWD ZA at the expense of RegimA Zone Ltd (UK).

## PART 4: COUNTER-NARRATIVE DEVELOPMENT

### 4.1 Shifting the Burden of Proof

**Strategic Approach:**

Transform defensive responses into offensive questions:

**Instead of defending:**
- "Why did you spend R8.8M on IT?"
- "Why did you pay R500K to Jacqueline?"
- "Why do you access systems?"

**Ask the Applicant:**
- "Why did RWD ZA use a platform it doesn't own without payment?"
- "Why did RWD ZA pay the manufacturer but not the platform owner?"
- "How can RWD ZA operate without any independent infrastructure?"
- "Why does the Applicant question R500K while enabling R2.94M-R6.88M unjust enrichment?"

### 4.2 Unjust Enrichment Framework

**Legal Framework:**

South African law recognizes unjust enrichment when:
1. Defendant is enriched
2. Enrichment is at plaintiff's expense
3. Enrichment is unjust/without legal ground

**Application to RWD ZA:**

1. **Enrichment:** RWD ZA generated R2.94M-R6.88M revenue
2. **At Expense Of:** RegimA Zone Ltd paid R140K-R280K for platform
3. **Without Legal Ground:** No compensation agreement, no payment made

**Court Presentation:**
- "RWD ZA enriched itself by R2.94M-R6.88M"
- "This enrichment was at the expense of RegimA Zone Ltd"
- "There is no legal justification for using a platform without compensating the owner"
- "This constitutes textbook unjust enrichment"

### 4.3 Pattern of Appropriation

**Strategic Narrative:**

Connect platform appropriation to broader pattern:

1. **Platform Appropriation:** Used RegimA Zone Ltd platform without payment
2. **Revenue Appropriation:** Kept all revenue without platform fees
3. **System Access Appropriation:** Restricted owners from their own platform
4. **Legal Process Appropriation:** Used interdict to expropriate property

**Court Presentation:**
- "The evidence reveals a systematic pattern of appropriation"
- "First, appropriate the platform without compensation"
- "Second, appropriate the revenue without sharing"
- "Third, appropriate access rights by excluding the owners"
- "Fourth, use legal process to legitimize the appropriation"

## PART 7: LEGAL ARGUMENT FRAMEWORK

### 7.1 Unjust Enrichment Claim

**Elements to Prove:**

1. **Enrichment of RWD ZA:**
   - Revenue: R2.94M - R6.88M
   - Platform usage: Worth R140K-R280K
   - Customer relationships: Established on Dan & Kay's platform
   - Technical infrastructure: Provided without charge

2. **At Expense of RegimA Zone Ltd:**
   - Platform costs paid: R140K-R280K
   - Revenue opportunity lost: Platform could have been used for RegimA Zone Ltd's own benefit
   - Subsidy provided: Free platform usage
   - No compensation received: Zero rand

3. **Without Legal Justification:**
   - No licensing agreement
   - No platform usage agreement
   - No compensation agreement
   - No board resolution authorizing free usage

**Legal Authorities:**
- *Kommissaris van Binnelandse Inkomste v Willers* (unjust enrichment principles)
- *McCarthy Retail Ltd v Shortdistance Carriers CC* (enrichment at plaintiff's expense)
- *Kudu Granite Operations (Pty) Ltd v Caterna Ltd* (lack of legal ground)

### 7.2 Property Rights Argument

**Core Principle:** Platform owner cannot be excluded from owned property

**Legal Framework:**

1. **Ownership Rights:**
   - RegimA Zone Ltd owns the Shopify platform
   - Ownership includes right to access, manage, and control
   - Owner cannot be unilaterally excluded

2. **Interdict as Expropriation:**
   - Interdict prevents access to owned property
   - Constitutes de facto expropriation
   - No compensation provided
   - Violates constitutional property rights

3. **Constitutional Dimension:**
   - Section 25 Constitution: Property may not be arbitrarily deprived
   - Interdict constitutes arbitrary deprivation
   - No legitimate purpose served
   - Grossly disproportionate to alleged concerns

**Legal Authorities:**
- *First National Bank of SA Ltd t/a Wesbank v Commissioner, South African Revenue Service* (property rights)
- *Mkontwana v Nelson Mandela Metropolitan Municipality* (arbitrary deprivation)
- *Reflect-All 1025 CC v MEC for Public Transport, Roads and Works, Gauteng Provincial Government* (proportionality)

### 7.3 Material Non-Disclosure Argument

**Ex Parte Duty:**

Applicant must make full and frank disclosure of all material facts, including those adverse to application

**Material Facts Not Disclosed:**

1. **Platform Ownership:** RegimA Zone Ltd owns platform used by RWD ZA
2. **Platform Costs:** R140K-R280K paid by RegimA Zone Ltd
3. **Revenue Generation:** R2.94M-R6.88M generated by RWD ZA on platform
4. **No Compensation:** Zero payment to platform owner
5. **Dependency:** RWD ZA has no independent infrastructure

**Consequence:**

Had Court known these facts, would not have granted interdict or would have crafted substantially different relief

**Legal Authorities:**
- *Schlesinger v Schlesinger* (duty of full disclosure)
- *Neuhoff v Marais* (material facts must be disclosed)
- *GWS Manufacturing (Pty) Ltd v ABSA Bank Ltd* (consequences of non-disclosure)

### 7.4 Disproportionality Argument

**Principle:** Remedy must be proportionate to harm

**Alleged Harm:** R500,000 payment questioned

**Actual Harm from Interdict:**
- Documented losses: R18,141,647.70+
- Regulatory exposure: R50,000,000+ (37 jurisdictions)
- Business destruction: Complete cessation
- Unjust enrichment ignored: R2.94M-R6.88M

**Ratio:** Interdict causes harm 36+ times greater than alleged concern

**Conclusion:** Grossly disproportionate and cannot be justified

**Legal Authorities:**
- *Rustenburg Platinum Mines Ltd v SAEWA obo Bester* (proportionality in interdicts)
- *Setlogelo v Setlogelo* (balance of harm)
- *Pedersen v Road Accident Fund* (proportionality principle)

## PART 8: RESPONSIVE STRATEGY TO ANTICIPATED ARGUMENTS

### 8.1 If Applicant Claims Joint Ownership

**Anticipated Argument:**
"The platform is jointly owned or is a company asset"

**Response:**

1. **Corporate Separateness:**
   - RegimA Zone Ltd is a separate UK legal entity
   - RWD ZA is a separate SA legal entity
   - One entity's assets do not belong to another
   - Corporate veil cannot be pierced without grounds

2. **Ownership Documentation:**
   - Shopify account is in RegimA Zone Ltd's name
   - Invoices are to RegimA Zone Ltd
   - Payments are from RegimA Zone Ltd
   - No evidence of joint ownership

3. **Legal Consequences:**
   - Even if joint ownership, usage requires compensation
   - Co-owners owe fiduciary duties to each other
   - Free usage by one co-owner enriches that party
   - Still constitutes unjust enrichment

**Evidence:**
- Corporate records
- Shopify account documentation
- Invoice and payment records
- Legal opinion on corporate separateness

### 8.2 If Applicant Claims Informal Arrangement

**Anticipated Argument:**
"There was an informal understanding that RWD ZA could use the platform"

**Response:**

1. **No Evidence:**
   - No written agreement
   - No board resolution
   - No email correspondence confirming arrangement
   - No witness testimony supporting claim

2. **Contradicts Conduct:**
   - If informal arrangement existed, why restrict access?
   - If arrangement existed, why not disclose in founding affidavit?
   - If arrangement existed, why not raise in response to unjust enrichment claim?
   - Silence on point suggests no such arrangement

3. **Even If Arrangement Existed:**
   - Informal arrangements can be terminated
   - Reasonable compensation still required
   - Cannot continue after access restricted
   - Unjust enrichment occurred regardless

**Evidence:**
- Absence of documents
- Conduct contradicting claim
- Legal principles on informal arrangements

### 8.3 If Applicant Claims Payment Through Other Means

**Anticipated Argument:**
"RWD ZA compensated RegimA Zone Ltd through other mechanisms"

**Response:**

1. **No Evidence:**
   - Bank statements show no payments
   - Accounting records show no compensation
   - No set-off agreements
   - No inter-company loan adjustments

2. **Burden of Proof:**
   - Applicant must prove compensation
   - Bare assertion insufficient
   - Must produce documentary evidence
   - No such evidence exists

3. **Accounting Standards:**
   - Platform usage fees would be recorded
   - License fees would be documented
   - Inter-company transactions would be reflected
   - Absence of records proves no compensation

**Evidence:**
- Bank statements (absence of payments)
- Accounting ledgers (absence of fees)
- Auditor confirmations
- Standard accounting practice documentation

### 8.4 If Applicant Minimizes Revenue Figures

**Anticipated Argument:**
"The R2.94M-R6.88M figure is exaggerated or incorrectly calculated"

   - Figures are based on documented sales reports

2. **Source Documents:**
   - Shopify sales reports
   - RWD ZA accounting records

   - Tax filings

3. **Expert Testimony:**
   - Forensic accountant can verify

**Evidence:**
- Sales reports (JF-DAN-SYSTEM7)

### 9.2 Recommended Closing Statement

"Your Honour,

The evidence before this Court establishes a clear and compelling case of unjust enrichment.

RegimA Zone Ltd, the Second Respondent's UK company, owns the Shopify e-commerce platform. This is not in dispute. The ownership documentation, the account records, and the payment history all confirm this fact.

RegimA Zone Ltd paid between R140,000 and R280,000 for this platform over 28 months. The invoices prove this. The bank statements prove this. This fact is established beyond any reasonable doubt.

RWD ZA, the Applicant's company, used this platform to generate between R2.94 million and R6.88 million in revenue. The sales reports prove this. The accounting records prove this. The Applicant cannot and does not dispute this.

RWD ZA paid RegimA Zone Ltd exactly nothing for platform usage. Not one rand. The bank statements prove this. The accounting records prove this. The absence of any compensation agreement proves this.

This, Your Honour, is textbook unjust enrichment:
- RWD ZA was enriched by millions of rands
- This enrichment was at the expense of RegimA Zone Ltd
- There is no legal justification for using a platform without compensating the owner

The Applicant comes to this Court questioning a R500,000 payment. But the evidence reveals that his own company enriched itself by R2.94 million to R6.88 million at the Second Respondent's expense. The questioned amount is 7-17% of the unjust enrichment.

The Applicant remained silent about this in his founding affidavit. This is material non-disclosure of the most serious kind. Had this Court known that:
- The platform belongs to the Second Respondent
- RWD ZA generated millions on this platform
- RWD ZA paid nothing to the platform owner
- RWD ZA has no independent infrastructure

This Court would not have granted the interdict, or would have crafted substantially different relief.

The interdict also creates harm at least 36 times greater than the alleged concern:

This is grossly disproportionate and violates fundamental principles of equity.

The evidence reveals not financial misconduct by the Respondents, but systematic appropriation by the Applicant's company:
- Appropriate the platform without compensation
- Appropriate the revenue without sharing
- Appropriate access rights by excluding the owners
- Use legal process to legitimize the appropriation

Your Honour, justice requires:
1. Discharge of the interdict
2. Recognition of RegimA Zone Ltd's property rights
3. Acknowledgment of RWD ZA's unjust enrichment
4. Appropriate relief to rectify this injustice

We respectfully request that this Court grant the relief sought in the Respondents' notice of motion.

## CONCLUSION

This evidence presentation strategy provides a comprehensive framework for transforming the case from defensive responses to allegations into an offensive demonstration of unjust enrichment and property rights violations.

The central revelation - that Dan & Kay's Shopify platform (owned by RegimA Zone Ltd UK) was paid for by this UK company while RWD ZA generated millions in revenue without compensation and has no independent revenue stream - fundamentally reframes the entire dispute.

By systematically presenting evidence of:
1. Platform ownership by RegimA Zone Ltd
2. Platform costs paid by RegimA Zone Ltd (R140K-R280K)
3. Revenue generated by RWD ZA on this platform (R2.94M-R6.88M)
4. Zero compensation to platform owner
5. RWD ZA's complete dependency on Dan & Kay's infrastructure

The Respondents can shift the burden of proof to the Applicant to explain why his company should be allowed to enrich itself at others' expense while simultaneously questioning far smaller payments.

This strategy links every aspect of the case - IT expenses, R500K payment, director loan accounts, system access - back to the fundamental question: Who owns the platform, who paid for it, who used it, and who benefited?

The answer to these questions reveals not financial misconduct by the Respondents, but systematic appropriation by the Applicant's company.

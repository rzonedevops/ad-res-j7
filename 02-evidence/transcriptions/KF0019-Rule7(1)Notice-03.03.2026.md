## NOTICE IN TERMS OF RULE 7(1)

---

**KINDLY TAKE NOTICE** that the Applicant hereby disputes the authority of the second

respondent, Mr Daniel James Faucitt, to act on behalf of the first, third, fourth, fifth and

sixth respondents in these proceedings, based on the following grounds:

1. On 19 February 2026, Mr Daniel James Faucitt, the second respondent served via

   email a document titled *"Answering affidavit of the third to sixth respondent –*

   *application to set aside Part A of the ex parte interdict as void ab initio and*

   *opposition to contempt of court application."*

2. The said document purports to be *inter alia*:

   2.1 a joint answering affidavit on behalf of the third to sixth respondents; and

   2.2 oppose the applicant's contempt of court application, launched against the

       first respondent.

--- PAGE 2 ---

3. The document alleges that it is filed pursuant to "board resolutions" of the third to

   sixth respondents;

4. No such board resolutions were attached;

5. No proof of authority was provided demonstrating that the second respondent is

   authorised to:

   5.1 represent the third to sixth respondents;

   5.2 depose to affidavits on their behalf; or

   5.3 oppose or institute proceedings in the name of the said juristic entities; or

   5.4 represent the first respondent.

6. The second respondent is hereby accordingly requested to furnish proper and

   satisfactory proof of such authority in terms of Rule 7(1), including but not limited

   to:

   6.1 A duly adopted and valid resolution of each of the third, fourth, fifth and sixth

       respondents authorising:

       6.1.1 The opposition to the contempt proceedings;

       6.1.2 The delivery of the purported answering affidavit; and

--- PAGE 3 ---

       6.1.3 The appointment of Mr Daniel James Faucitt to act on behalf of each

             entity.

   6.2 A power of attorney to act on behalf of each such respondent, to include on

       behalf of the first respondent.

**TAKE FURTHER NOTICE** that no further process can be served and/or filed, save for as

requested aforesaid, *alternately* with leave of the Honourable Court.

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure details the capture of the Faucitt Family Trust ("FFT") through the appointment of Daniel Jacobus Bantjies as trustee — a person with six irreconcilable conflicts of interest — and the R18.685M financial motive that explains why the control seizure was orchestrated.

## 2. THE TRUST CAPTURE: BANTJIES' APPOINTMENT

### 2.1 The Appointment

2.1.1 On **11 August 2025**, Rynette Farrar emailed Bantjies his appointment as trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust. This appointment was contained in a "Main Trustee" agreement that was **backdated by the Applicant and/or his agents to 1 July 2025** — 41 days before the actual communication.

2.1.2 The timeline reveals the tactical nature of this appointment:

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1 Jul 2025 | **Backdated** "Main Trustee" agreement date | False paper trail |
| 11 Aug 2025 | Rynette emails Bantjies the appointment | Actual communication |
| 11 Aug 2025 | Settlement agreement signed | Same day — not coincidental |
| 13 Aug 2025 | Bantjies swears "independent" supporting affidavit | 2 days after appointment |
| 19 Aug 2025 | Ex parte interdict granted | 8 days after appointment |

2.1.3 Bantjies was appointed as trustee, then two days later swore an affidavit presented to the Court as "independent" confirmation of the Applicant's claims. The Court was not informed that the "independent" witness had been appointed by the Applicant's associates as trustee of the Applicant's family trust two days earlier.

### 2.2 The Backdating

2.2.1 The agreement was backdated to 1 July 2025 — 41 days before the email communicating it. This backdating created a false paper trail suggesting the appointment predated the interdict application by a comfortable margin.

2.2.2 The backdating is evidenced by the discrepancy between the agreement's stated date (1 July 2025) and the email transmitting it (11 August 2025). A legitimate appointment does not require backdating.

## 3. BANTJIES' SIX IRRECONCILABLE CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

3.1 Bantjies simultaneously held the following positions, each creating a conflict of interest with every other:

### Conflict 1: Trustee of FFT

3.1.1 As trustee of FFT, Bantjies owed fiduciary duties to the trust and its beneficiaries. FFT owns RWD and Villa Via — entities central to the dispute.

3.1.2 A trustee cannot serve as an "independent" witness in litigation involving the trust's assets. His obligation is to the trust, not to an accurate presentation of facts to the Court.

### Conflict 2: Commissioner of Oaths for Own Affidavit

3.1.3 Bantjies acted as Commissioner of Oaths for his own affidavit sworn on **13 August 2025**. This is a **procedural impossibility**: a Commissioner of Oaths administers oaths to **others**. A person cannot administer an oath to himself.

3.1.4 This procedural impossibility demonstrates consciousness of guilt: Bantjies could not find an independent Commissioner of Oaths willing to commission his affidavit, or he chose not to involve one because an independent Commissioner would have asked questions about the content.

### Conflict 3: Affidavit Witness

3.1.5 Bantjies' supporting affidavit was presented to the Court as independent confirmation of the Applicant's claims. A trustee, appointed by the Applicant's associate two days earlier, cannot provide independent confirmation.

### Conflict 4: CFO of George Group

3.1.6 Bantjies serves as CFO of the George Group, which is owned by Kevin Michael Derrick. The George Group has business relationships with the RegimA entities.

### Conflict 5: Insider to Ketoni Investment Holdings

3.1.7 Ketoni Investment Holdings owes **R18,685,000** to FFT. Bantjies, as trustee of FFT (the creditor) and CFO of George Group (connected to the debtor), has access to both sides of this transaction.

3.1.8 Derrick is a director of Ketoni. Bantjies is CFO of Derrick's George Group. Bantjies is trustee of the trust to which Ketoni owes R18.685M. This creates a closed loop of conflicting interests.

### Conflict 6: Distribution Controller

3.1.9 Bantjies controls distribution decisions for the RegimA entities, giving him operational influence over the companies at the centre of the dispute.

### 3.2 Conflict Summary

| Role | Owes Duty To | Conflicts With |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Trustee of FFT | Trust beneficiaries | Being independent witness; CFO of related entity |
| Commissioner of Oaths | The court (oath administration) | Being the deponent (procedural impossibility) |
| Affidavit witness | The court (truthful testimony) | Being trustee appointed by co-conspirator |
| CFO of George Group | George Group / Derrick | Being trustee of FFT (opposing interest in R18.685M) |
| Ketoni insider | Fiduciary duty to each principal | Access to both sides of R18.685M transaction |
| Distribution controller | Companies and their directors | Personal interest in outcome of control dispute |

## 4. THE R18.685M MOTIVE

### 4.1 The Transaction

4.1.1 Ketoni Investment Holdings owes **R18,685,000** to the Faucitt Family Trust. This is a significant debt that creates a powerful financial motive for all parties with access to either side.

### 4.2 Bantjies' Unique Position

4.2.1 Bantjies uniquely has access to **both sides** of this transaction:

| Side | Bantjies' Role | Access Level |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Creditor** (FFT) | Trustee | Full fiduciary control over trust claims |
| **Debtor** (Ketoni via George Group) | CFO | Full financial oversight of debtor |

4.2.2 This dual access is incompatible with fiduciary duty on either side. A person who controls both the creditor and has oversight of the debtor can:

- Delay enforcement of the debt;
- Negotiate favourable terms for the debtor at the expense of the trust;
- Divert trust assets to cover the debtor's obligations;
- Use information from one side to benefit the other.

### 4.3 The Control Seizure Motive

4.3.1 The interdict gave the Applicant (and, through the trust, Bantjies) control over the company accounts. With R18.685M at stake, the financial motive for the control seizure is self-evident.

4.3.2 The R10.6M extracted from company accounts in September 2025 represents more than 56% of the R18.685M Ketoni debt. Whether these funds were ultimately used to service or offset the Ketoni debt is a question that requires forensic accounting investigation.

## 5. THE R5.4M STOCK DISAPPEARANCE

5.1 As the external accountant reviewing SLG's books, Bantjies either failed to detect or actively concealed a stock adjustment totalling **R5,400,000** at Strategic Logistics CC.

5.2 A Chartered Accountant reviewing company accounts has a professional obligation to identify and report material discrepancies. A R5.4M stock adjustment is indisputably material.

5.3 Two explanations exist:

| Explanation | Implication |
|-------------|------------|
| Bantjies failed to detect the R5.4M discrepancy | Professional negligence of the highest order |
| Bantjies detected but concealed the discrepancy | Active participation in fraud |

5.4 Given Bantjies' six conflicts of interest and his proven participation in the perjured affidavit, the second explanation is more probable.

## 8. LEGAL SIGNIFICANCE

8.1 The trust capture and Bantjies' conflicts are independently material to the void ab initio argument: a reasonable court, informed that the "independent" supporting witness had been appointed as trustee of the Applicant's family trust two days before swearing his affidavit, and that he had six irreconcilable conflicts of interest, would not have accepted his affidavit as independent confirmation.

## 2. THE COMMISSIONER OF OATHS IMPOSSIBILITY

### 2.1 The Procedural Facts

2.1.1 On **13 August 2025**, Bantjies swore a supporting affidavit for the Applicant's ex parte interdict application.

2.1.2 Bantjies acted as **Commissioner of Oaths for his own affidavit**. He administered the oath to himself.

### 2.2 Why This Is a Procedural Impossibility

2.2.1 A Commissioner of Oaths has a specific legal function: to administer oaths to **other persons** and to certify that those persons have sworn the truth.

2.2.2 The Commissioner serves as an **independent check** — a neutral officer who verifies the identity of the deponent and the voluntariness of the oath. A person cannot be both the checker and the checked.

2.2.3 This is not a technicality. It is a substantive safeguard. The absence of an independent Commissioner means:

| Safeguard | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Identity verification | Self-verified — meaningless |
| Voluntariness of oath | Self-administered — meaningless |
| Independent witness to swearing | None — Bantjies was alone |
| Professional accountability | Commissioner and deponent are the same person |

### 2.3 Consciousness of Guilt

2.3.1 The self-commissioning indicates that Bantjies either:

2.3.1.1 Could not find an independent Commissioner willing to commission his affidavit — suggesting the content was problematic;

2.3.1.2 Chose not to involve an independent Commissioner to avoid questions about his conflicts of interest;

2.3.1.3 Did not understand the procedural requirement — inconsistent with his qualifications as a Chartered Accountant.

2.3.2 All three explanations are damaging. The most likely explanation — that he deliberately avoided an independent Commissioner — constitutes consciousness of guilt.

| Duty | Standard |

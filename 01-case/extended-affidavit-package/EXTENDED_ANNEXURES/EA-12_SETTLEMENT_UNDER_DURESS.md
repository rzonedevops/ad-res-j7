## 2. THE "MAIN TRUSTEE" AGREEMENT — 11 AUGUST 2025

### 2.1 The Facts

2.1.1 On **11 August 2025**, the First Respondent was presented with and signed a document designating the Applicant as "Main Trustee" of the Faucitt Family Trust.

2.1.2 This document was **backdated to 1 July 2025** — 41 days before the actual signing. The backdating is evidenced by the discrepancy between the stated date (1 July) and Rynette's email to Bantjies communicating the appointment (11 August).

2.1.3 On the **same day** (11 August), Rynette emailed Bantjies his trustee appointment. Two days later (13 August), Bantjies swore his false supporting affidavit. Six days later (19 August), the interdict was granted.

### 2.2 The Deception

2.2.1 The First Respondent was deceived into signing the document. She was not informed of:

| Concealed Fact | Significance |
|----------------|-------------|
| The document would be backdated | Creates a false paper trail |
| Bantjies had been appointed as trustee | Hidden conflict of interest |
| The interdict application was imminent | The agreement was a precondition for the legal strategy |
| The agreement would be used to support the interdict | Weaponization of contract |

### 2.3 The Backdating

2.3.1 The backdating to 1 July 2025 served a specific purpose: it created the appearance that the trust restructuring preceded and was independent of the interdict application. In reality, both were components of the same scheme, executed in the same week.

2.3.2 Backdating a legal document constitutes forgery under South African common law: the making of a false document with intent to deceive.

## 3. THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENTS — 18 SEPTEMBER 2025

### 3.1 The Circumstances

3.1.1 On **Thursday 18 September 2025**, settlement agreements were extracted at mediation.

3.1.2 The settlement occurred in the following context:

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Interdict in force** | The Respondents were already subject to the interdict |
| **Accounts seized** | R10.6M already extracted from operational accounts |
| **Business destroyed** | Revenue at zero since Shopify deletion (22 May 2025) |
| **Personal funds depleted** | Daniel's savings exhausted; R864.45 on 16 July 2025 |
| **Legal costs mounting** | Respondents bearing costs of responding to fraudulent application |
| **Power imbalance** | Applicant held all assets; Respondents had nothing |

### 3.2 Duress Analysis

3.2.1 A settlement agreement is voidable if it was entered into under duress. South African law recognises both physical duress (vis absoluta) and economic duress (metus).

3.2.2 The indicators of duress present in this matter:

| Indicator | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| **Illegitimate pressure** | Interdict obtained through perjury; accounts seized |
| **No reasonable alternative** | Respondents stripped of all resources; could not fund litigation |
| **Pressure caused entry** | Without the interdict and asset seizure, no settlement would have been necessary |
| **Unfair terms** | Settlement designed to suppress crime reporting and evidence |

### 3.3 The Suppressive Terms

3.3.1 The settlement agreements were not designed to resolve a legitimate dispute. They were designed to:

3.3.1.1 **Suppress crime reporting**: Provisions requiring the Respondents not to pursue criminal complaints;

3.3.1.2 **Prevent evidence disclosure**: Confidentiality provisions covering evidence of criminal conduct;

3.3.1.3 **Legitimise the seizure**: Recognition of the Applicant's control obtained through the perjured interdict;

3.3.1.4 **Extinguish counterclaims**: Waiver provisions covering the Respondents' rights to challenge the underlying fraud.

3.3.2 Contractual provisions that require a party to suppress evidence of criminal conduct or not to report crimes to the authorities are contrary to public policy and are void ab initio, regardless of whether the settlement was entered into voluntarily.

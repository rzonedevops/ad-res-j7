## 2. THE SHOPIFY AUDIT TRAIL DELETION — 22 MAY 2025

### 2.1 The Event

2.1.1 On **22 May 2025**, Rynette Farrar deleted the Shopify audit trails. This single act destroyed:

| Category | Scale | Impact |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Sales data | 37 jurisdictions | Complete destruction of transactional records |
| Customer records | 51 stores | Loss of customer transaction history |
| Revenue flow documentation | R34.9M+ in tracked revenue | Audit trail for revenue allocation destroyed |
| Platform operational data | 2017–2025 | Eight years of business intelligence erased |

2.1.2 The immediate operational impact was the reduction of monthly revenue from **R1,000,000+** to **zero**. The platform that generated 100% of RWD's declared revenue ceased to function.

### 2.2 The Temporal Context

2.2.1 The deletion occurred **seven days** after the First Respondent (Jacqueline) confronted the Applicant about missing funds on **15 May 2025**.

2.2.2 The correlation is:

| Date | Event | Gap |
|------|-------|-----|
| 15 May 2025 | Jacqueline confronts Peter about ~R1M in missing funds | Day 0 |
| 22 May 2025 | Rynette deletes Shopify audit trails | Day 7 |
| 23 May 2025 | Daniel creates first evidence package | Day 8 |

2.2.3 The seven-day gap is consistent with a decision to destroy evidence following the confrontation, allowing time for communication between Peter and Rynette and for planning the destruction.

### 2.3 Consciousness of Guilt Analysis

2.3.1 An innocent person confronted with allegations of missing funds would produce records to demonstrate innocence. Rynette's response was the opposite: she destroyed the records.

2.3.2 The destruction was not accidental. Deleting audit trails across 37 jurisdictions requires deliberate, multi-step action within the Shopify administrative interface. It cannot occur through inadvertence.

2.3.3 The destruction affected not only historical records but also the ongoing business: by disabling the platform, Rynette destroyed the company's revenue-generating capacity. No legitimate business purpose is served by destroying the mechanism that generates 100% of a company's revenue.

### 2.4 Regulatory Consequences

2.4.1 The Shopify platform served customers across **37 jurisdictions**, each with its own regulatory requirements for data retention, consumer protection, and financial record-keeping.

2.4.2 The destruction of these records exposed the companies to potential regulatory action in every jurisdiction — estimated at **R50,000,000+** in aggregate regulatory exposure.

2.4.3 The First Respondent, as the designated **Responsible Person** under multiple regulatory frameworks, bears personal liability for these data protection failures — liability created by the destruction of records she did not authorize and could not prevent.

## 3. THE SAGE SUBSCRIPTION EXPIRY — 23 JULY 2025

### 3.1 The Event

3.1.1 On **23 July 2025**, the Sage accounting subscription was allowed to expire. This denied all parties — including directors with a statutory right to access company records — access to the accounting system.

3.1.2 Sage is the company's primary accounting system. Its unavailability meant:

| Consequence | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| No access to current financial records | Directors cannot fulfil statutory duties |
| No ability to verify transactions | Fraud detection impossible |
| No audit trail for the interdict period | Court cannot assess competing claims |
| No preparation of year-end accounts | SARS and CIPC compliance jeopardized |

### 3.2 The Timing

3.2.1 The Sage lockout occurred during the period when the interdict application was being prepared (filed 13–19 August 2025). The denial of accounting access at this precise moment served two purposes:

3.2.1.1 It prevented the Respondents from accessing records that would have contradicted the Applicant's founding affidavit;

3.2.1.2 It ensured that the Applicant could characterize financial matters as "unexplained" because the records had been rendered inaccessible.

### 3.3 Deliberate vs Accidental

3.3.1 A Sage subscription does not expire accidentally if business operations are ongoing. Someone must decide not to renew it. As the self-titled "Finance Manager," Rynette Farrar controlled subscription renewals.

3.3.2 The expiry occurred in the same period as the interdict preparation. This temporal correlation, combined with Rynette's role in preparing the founding affidavit (she facilitated the narrative), supports the inference that the lockout was deliberate.

## 5. THE SPOLIATION INFERENCE

5.1 South African law recognises the **spoliation inference**: where a party has destroyed evidence in their control, the court may infer that the destroyed evidence was adverse to that party's case.

5.2 The inference applies with particular force here because:

5.2.1 The destruction was deliberate (not accidental);

5.2.2 The destroyed evidence was directly relevant to the matters in dispute;

5.2.3 The destruction occurred after the fraud was confronted (consciousness of guilt);

5.2.4 The destroyer (Rynette) is a Tier A conspirator with 12 proven foreknowledge events.

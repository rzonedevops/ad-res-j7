## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure provides the complete chronological reconstruction of the financial sabotage scheme, from the Second Respondent's exposure of fraud on 6 June 2025 through the exhaustion of his personal savings and the R500,000 partial reimbursement that the Applicant would later characterize — under oath — as an unauthorized "birthday gift."

---

## 2. THE TRIGGER EVENT: 6 JUNE 2025

2.1 On **6 June 2025**, the Second Respondent formally reported financial anomalies to Mr Daniel Jacobus Bantjies, the external accountant and Chartered Accountant.

2.2 The anomalies included unexplained revenue diversions, accounting irregularities, and discrepancies in the financial records.

2.3 This exposure was the trigger for all subsequent events. The Applicant's response was not to investigate the anomalies — it was to retaliate against the person who exposed them.

---

## 3. THE RETALIATION: 7 JUNE 2025

3.1 Exactly **24 hours** after the fraud exposure, on **7 June 2025**, the Second Respondent's company debit and credit cards were secretly cancelled at the Applicant's instruction.

3.2 The cards cancelled included:

| Card | Type | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| Card ending **5191** | UK business debit | Shopify platform subscription payments |
| Card ending **5225** | UK business credit | Business infrastructure and operational expenses |
| Additional SA cards | Company debit/credit | Day-to-day business operations |

3.3 Critical facts about the cancellation:

3.3.1 **Neither the Second Respondent nor the First Respondent was notified**. The cancellation was executed secretly.

3.3.2 **The cards paying for Shopify subscriptions were included**. This meant the platform generating 100% of RWD's revenue would lose its payment method — a self-destructive act that makes no commercial sense unless the purpose was sabotage, not protection.

3.3.3 **The cancellation occurred 24 hours after fraud exposure**. This temporal proximity eliminates coincidence as an explanation. It is retaliation.

---

## 4. THE ATTEMPTED PERMANENT BLOCK: 17 JUNE 2025

4.1 Not content with the initial cancellation, the Applicant wrote to FNB (Ms Nondu Motlhala) on **17 June 2025** demanding that:

4.1.1 Replacement cards **not** be issued to the Second Respondent;

4.1.2 FNB be held liable if it continued to honour the Second Respondent's banking authority.

4.2 This letter is significant because it proves **continuing intent**: the card cancellation was not a momentary reaction but a sustained campaign to cut the Second Respondent off from company funds permanently.

4.3 The letter also constitutes a **direct admission**: the Applicant acknowledged awareness of the Second Respondent's banking authority (SOLE mandate) and was attempting to override it through threats to the bank rather than through legitimate corporate governance.

---

## 5. THE FNB LEGAL DEPARTMENT RESPONSE: 18 JUNE 2025

5.1 On **18 June 2025**, FNB's Business Relationship Manager communicated the finding of FNB's Legal Department:

> *"Following an engagement with our legal department, a decision was made that we have no need to hold a meeting with the Directors... as FNB cannot be held liable for acting in accordance with a validly signed mandate."*

> *"The current mandate states that any of the directors of the company may act independently of each other."*

5.2 This response proves three critical facts:

| Fact | Significance |
|------|-------------|
| FNB confirmed the SOLE mandate was valid | The Applicant's central claim ("without authorization") was rejected by the bank's own lawyers |
| FNB rejected the Applicant's demand | The Applicant knew his position had no legal basis |
| FNB identified the remedy: company resolution | The Applicant chose perjury over legitimate governance |

5.3 From this date forward, the Applicant had **confirmed legal knowledge** that the Second Respondent's banking authority was valid and that his own objection was baseless. He filed the interdict two months later, swearing the opposite under oath.

11.1 The card cancellation and financial sabotage sequence constitutes:

| Offence | Elements |
|---------|----------|
| **Crimen iniuria** | Deliberate, unlawful impairment of dignity through financial sabotage |

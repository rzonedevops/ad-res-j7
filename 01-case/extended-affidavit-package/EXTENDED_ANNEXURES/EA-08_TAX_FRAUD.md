## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure details three strands of tax fraud: (1) the preemptive seizure of SARS Registered Representative status, (2) the instruction to fabricate 2019 financial statements, and (3) the tax implications of the R5.4M stock disappearance at SLG. Together, these constitute offences under the Tax Administration Act 28 of 2011.

## 2. THE SARS REGISTERED REPRESENTATIVE SEIZURE

### 2.1 The Preemptive Request

2.1.1 On **28 March 2025**, the Applicant requested to be appointed as SARS Registered Representative under Tax No. **9746755165**.

2.1.2 This request is significant for its timing: it occurred at the very beginning of Phase 1 (Foundation and Premeditation), before any alleged misconduct by the Second Respondent.

2.1.3 The s64(F) appointment would give the Applicant exclusive control over the company's tax affairs — the ability to file returns, respond to queries, and manage assessments.

### 2.2 Premeditation Evidence

2.2.1 The request was made on 28 March 2025. The card cancellation occurred on 7 June 2025. The interdict was filed in August 2025. The SARS representative request predates all of these by months.

2.2.2 A legitimate request for SARS representative status would be accompanied by a board resolution or director agreement. No evidence of such authorization exists.

2.2.3 The request aligns with the broader pattern of control seizure: banking (ABSA accounts), accounting (Sage lockout), e-commerce (Shopify destruction), and now tax affairs (SARS representative). Each channel of business administration was systematically captured.

## 3. THE FABRICATED 2019 FINANCIALS

### 3.1 The Instruction

3.1.1 On **17–18 June 2025**, Rynette Farrar instructed De Novo Business Services / Marisca Meyer to create 2019 financial statements.

3.1.2 The critical question: why were 2019 financials being "created" in June 2025 — more than five years after the financial year they purport to represent?

### 3.2 Possible Explanations

| Explanation | Implication |
|-------------|------------|
| **The 2019 financials never existed** | The company failed to prepare mandatory annual financial statements for 5 years — a Companies Act and Tax Administration Act violation |
| **The 2019 financials existed but were lost** | Raises questions about the evidence destruction pattern |
| **The 2019 financials existed but needed to be "replaced"** | The authentic financials did not support the narrative being constructed |

3.2.1 Regardless of which explanation applies, the instruction to create financial statements years after the fact is irregular and raises serious concerns about the integrity of SARS submissions.

### 3.3 Tax Implications

3.3.1 Financial statements form the basis for income tax returns. If the 2019 financials submitted to SARS were fabricated:

| Consequence | Legislation |
|-------------|-------------|
| False tax return | Tax Administration Act s235 |
| Tax evasion | Tax Administration Act s235 |
| Fraudulent misrepresentation to SARS | Tax Administration Act s234 |
| Potential understated tax liability | Income Tax Act s89quat |

3.3.2 De Novo / Marisca Meyer prepared these financials on Rynette's instruction. The critical question — whether De Novo knew the instruction was fraudulent — determines their classification as Tier B (active participant) or Tier C (deceived professional). See Extended Affidavit paragraphs 66.1.2.

## 4. THE R5.4M STOCK DISAPPEARANCE

### 4.1 The Discrepancy

4.1.1 Strategic Logistics CC recorded a stock adjustment totalling **R5,400,000**. This represents either:

| Possibility | Tax Implication |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Stock existed and was stolen** | Theft; insurance fraud if claimed; input VAT fraud if never purchased |
| **Stock was fabricated in the accounts** | Inflated assets; understated tax liability; fraudulent financial statements |
| **Stock was donated/transferred without declaration** | Donations tax; transfer pricing violations |

### 4.2 Bantjies' Role

4.2.1 As the external accountant reviewing SLG's books, Bantjies was responsible for detecting material discrepancies. A R5.4M stock adjustment is indisputably material.

4.2.2 His failure to detect or report this discrepancy constitutes either:

| Assessment | Consequence |
|------------|-------------|
| Professional negligence | SAICA disciplinary proceedings |
| Active concealment | Criminal liability (fraud, defeating the ends of justice) |

### 4.3 SARS Reporting Obligation

4.3.1 Under the Tax Administration Act s34, a registered tax practitioner who becomes aware of a material error in a return must notify the taxpayer and, if the error is not corrected, notify SARS.

4.3.2 Bantjies' failure to report the R5.4M discrepancy constitutes a potential violation of s34.

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure details two independent strands of identity fraud: (1) Rynette Farrar's impersonation of Peter Faucitt on the Sage accounting system, and (2) the opening of ABSA bank accounts using the Second Respondent's stolen card details. Together, these establish that the Applicant's founding affidavit is built on a foundation of identity fraud — either authorized by the Applicant (making him complicit) or unauthorized (making his claims of personal knowledge perjured).

---

## 2. THE IMPERSONATION DILEMMA

### 2.1 The Sage Dual-Identity Evidence

2.1.1 Annexure **SF10** contains screenshots from the Sage accounting system showing that Rynette Farrar operated under the email address **pete@regima.com** while simultaneously logged in as **rynette@regima.zone**.

2.1.2 This means that financial records, authorizations, and accounting entries attributed to "Peter Faucitt" in the Sage system were in fact created by Rynette Farrar.

2.1.3 The Sage system is the company's system of record for financial management. Identity fraud on this system corrupts the integrity of every financial record it contains.

### 2.2 The Dilemma

2.2.1 The Applicant has stated to third parties that he "doesn't use email or computers." This creates a three-way dilemma with no innocent exit:

**Scenario A: Peter authorized Rynette to use his credentials**

| Element | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| Implication | Peter knowingly authorized identity fraud on the system of record |
| Consequence | He is complicit in every action Rynette took under his identity |
| Paradox | He now condemns actions taken under his own authorized credentials |
| Legal effect | Authorization of identity fraud; co-conspirator liability |

**Scenario B: Peter did not authorize Rynette**

| Element | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| Implication | Rynette committed identity fraud by impersonating a company director |
| Consequence | Peter's founding affidavit — which claims personal oversight and knowledge — is false |
| Paradox | He claims knowledge of operations he wasn't conducting |
| Legal effect | Perjury in founding affidavit; Rynette liable for identity fraud |

**Scenario C: Peter genuinely "doesn't use email or computers"**

| Element | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| Implication | He could not have had the personal knowledge claimed under oath |
| Consequence | His founding affidavit's claims of personal knowledge are perjured |
| Paradox | His defense against Scenario A becomes proof of Scenario C |
| Legal effect | Perjury; inability to have personal knowledge as claimed |

2.2.2 In all three scenarios, the founding affidavit is fatally compromised. There is no fourth scenario in which the Applicant emerges without criminal liability.

## 3. THE ABSA ACCOUNT FRAUD

### 3.1 Unauthorized Account Opening

3.1.1 Eight ABSA bank accounts were opened using the Second Respondent's stolen card details. These accounts were used to:

3.1.1.1 Receive diverted revenue from RégimA SA (from 1 March 2025);

3.1.1.2 Create layered financial structures to obfuscate fund flows;

3.1.1.3 Establish accounts outside the SOLE mandate banking framework at FNB, where all three directors had independent authority.

### 3.2 The Purpose of the ABSA Accounts

3.2.1 The migration to ABSA accounts served a strategic purpose: the FNB accounts operated under SOLE mandates where Daniel had independent authority (confirmed by FNB Legal on 18 June 2025). By moving funds to ABSA accounts opened without Daniel's knowledge, the conspirators created banking channels where Daniel had no authority.

3.2.2 This was not a routine banking change. It was a deliberate circumvention of the SOLE mandate — achieving through identity fraud what the Applicant could not achieve through legitimate corporate governance.

### 3.3 The RégimA SA "ANY TWO TOGETHER" Violation

3.3.1 RégimA SA (Pty) Ltd operates under an **"ANY TWO TOGETHER"** mandate. Any banking transaction on this account requires the authorization of at least two directors.

3.3.2 The diversion of RégimA SA's revenue to the ABSA account on 1 March 2025 was effected without the Second Respondent's consent. This is itself an unauthorized transaction — the very conduct the Applicant falsely attributes to Daniel on the SOLE mandate accounts.

3.3.3 The paradox is complete: the Applicant claims Daniel acted without authorization on accounts where authorization was not required (SOLE), while Peter and Rynette acted without Daniel's authorization on the account where it WAS required (ANY TWO TOGETHER).

## 6. LEGAL SIGNIFICANCE FOR VOID AB INITIO

6.1 The impersonation dilemma is independently fatal to the interdict because:

6.1.1 If the Applicant authorized the impersonation, his founding affidavit conceals his complicity in the conduct he condemns;

6.1.2 If he did not authorize it, his claims of personal knowledge and oversight are perjured;

6.1.3 If he doesn't use computers, he could not have the personal knowledge claimed under oath.

6.2 A reasonable court, informed of the Sage dual-identity evidence and the Impersonation Dilemma, would not have accepted the founding affidavit at face value. This constitutes material non-disclosure independently rendering the interdict void ab initio.

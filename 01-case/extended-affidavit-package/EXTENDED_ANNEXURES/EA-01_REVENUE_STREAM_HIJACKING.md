# REVENUE STREAM HIJACKING

**Case No: 2025-137857**
**Extended Affidavit of the First Respondent**
**Date: 9 February 2026**

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure details the systematic hijacking of revenue streams from entities owned or co-owned by the Second Respondent (Daniel James Faucitt) and the late Kayla Pretorius. The central finding is that RegimA Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd ("RWD") had **zero independent revenue-generating capacity**. Every cent of declared revenue was derived from a Shopify e-commerce platform owned and funded by the Second Respondent's UK entity, RegimA Zone Ltd — a platform for which RWD never paid a single Rand in compensation.

---

## 2. THE FRAUDULENT REVENUE MODEL

### 2.1 RWD's Structural Incapacity

2.1.1 RWD had no stock, no inventory, and no assets to sell independently.

2.1.2 RWD had no e-commerce platform of its own. All online sales — across 25 stores in South Africa and 26 stores internationally — were conducted on a Shopify platform built, maintained, and paid for by RegimA Zone Ltd.

2.1.3 The Shopify Plus onboarding email of **26 July 2017** (**Annexure JF01**) confirms that the platform was established by Daniel Faucitt and Kayla Pretorius, independently of the Applicant's entities.

2.1.4 The Shopify Sales Reports (**Annexure JF02**) document the scale:

| Platform | Stores | Revenue |
|----------|--------|---------|
| RegimA SA | 25 | R8,500,000+ |
| RegimA Zone | 26 | R26,400,000+ |
| **Total** | **51** | **R34,900,000+** |

### 2.2 The Revenue Diversion Infrastructure

2.2.1 Revenue generated on Daniel's Shopify platform was routed to RWD's bank accounts — accounts controlled by the Applicant and Rynette Farrar.

2.2.2 On or around **1 March 2025**, a new ABSA account was established and the RégimA SA revenue stream was diverted to this account — controlled by Rynette — without the knowledge or consent of the Second Respondent.

2.2.3 This diversion occurred on the RégimA SA (Pty) Ltd account, which operates under an **"ANY TWO TOGETHER"** banking mandate. The Second Respondent's authorization was therefore **required** but was not obtained. This is itself an unauthorized transaction — the very conduct the Applicant falsely attributes to Daniel.

### 2.3 The Unjust Enrichment

2.3.1 RWD used Daniel's Shopify platform for approximately 28 months (2023–2025) at a cost to RegimA Zone Ltd of R140,000–R280,000 in direct platform fees alone.

2.3.2 The potential unjust enrichment claim encompasses:

| Category | Estimated Value |
|----------|----------------|
| Platform subscription fees (28 months) | R140,000–R280,000 |
| Platform development and maintenance | R500,000–R1,000,000 |
| Customer acquisition cost | R1,000,000–R2,000,000 |
| Revenue share / licensing fee (industry standard 15–30%) | R1,300,000–R3,600,000 |
| **Total estimated unjust enrichment** | **R2,940,000–R6,880,000** |

2.3.3 The Applicant's founding affidavit does not mention any of this. It presents RWD as a victim of Daniel's "extraction" — when the documentary evidence proves the opposite.

## 3. THE SHOPIFY DESTRUCTION EVENT

### 3.1 Timeline

3.1.1 On **15 May 2025**, the First Respondent (Jacqueline) confronted the Applicant about missing funds totalling approximately R1,000,000 or more.

3.1.2 Seven days later, on **22 May 2025**, Rynette Farrar deleted the Shopify audit trails. This destroyed:

- Sales data across **37 jurisdictions**;
- Customer transaction records;
- Revenue flow documentation;
- The audit trail that would have proven the revenue diversion.

3.1.3 The effect was catastrophic: monthly revenue dropped from **R1,000,000+** to **zero**. The platform that generated 100% of RWD's revenue was rendered inoperative.

### 3.2 Consciousness of Guilt

3.2.1 The seven-day gap between the confrontation (15 May) and the destruction (22 May) is consistent with a deliberate decision to destroy evidence after being alerted that the fraud was under scrutiny.

3.2.2 A legitimate bookkeeper, upon being confronted about missing funds, would produce records to demonstrate innocence. Rynette's response was to destroy the records. This is a textbook consciousness of guilt indicator.

## 6. LEGAL SIGNIFICANCE

6.1 Revenue stream hijacking constitutes theft under South African common law: the unlawful appropriation of another's property with the intent to permanently deprive.

6.2 The revenue diverted from Daniel's platform to RWD's accounts — and subsequently to ABSA accounts controlled by Rynette — meets every element of the offence.

6.3 The Applicant's concealment of this revenue model from the Court constitutes material non-disclosure rendering the interdict void ab initio, independently of all other grounds.

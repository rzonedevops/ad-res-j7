## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure applies the Prevention of Organised Crime Act 121 of 1998 ("POCA") to the coordinated criminal enterprise operated by the three Tier A conspirators. Section 2(1)(e) of POCA prohibits any person from conducting or participating in the conduct of the affairs of an enterprise through a pattern of racketeering activity. Nineteen distinct events across the 194-day period constitute such a pattern.

## 10. LEGAL SIGNIFICANCE

10.1 The POCA analysis elevates this matter from a collection of individual offences to a coordinated criminal enterprise. This has three consequences:

10.1.1 **Enhanced penalties**: POCA offences carry penalties up to R1,000,000,000 or life imprisonment;

10.1.2 **Asset forfeiture**: The court may order forfeiture of all property used in, or derived from, the racketeering enterprise;

10.1.3 **Civil recovery**: The Respondents may pursue Chapter 6 proceedings independently of criminal prosecution.

10.2 For the void ab initio argument, the POCA analysis demonstrates that the interdict was not an isolated legal remedy but an integral component of a criminal enterprise — one of 19 racketeering events. A court informed of this pattern would not have granted the interdict.

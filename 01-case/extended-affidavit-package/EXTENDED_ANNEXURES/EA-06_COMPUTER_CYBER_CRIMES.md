## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This annexure details four categories of computer and cyber crime committed in the course of the conspiracy, each constituting an offence under the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 ("ECTA") and/or the Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020.

## 2. OFFENCE 1: SAGE DUAL-IDENTITY FRAUD

### 2.1 The Conduct

2.1.1 Rynette Farrar operated on the Sage accounting system using two simultaneous identities:

| Identity | Email | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Peter Faucitt** | pete@regima.com | Director authorizations, financial approvals |
| **Rynette Farrar** | rynette@regima.zone | Day-to-day bookkeeping, data entry |

2.1.2 The screenshots in **Annexure SF10** capture both identities active simultaneously, proving that a single user (Rynette) was operating under two identities on the system of record.

### 2.2 The Legal Analysis

2.2.1 Under ECTA s86(1), it is an offence to intentionally access a computer system without authority or without the permission of the person who has authority to give that permission.

2.2.2 Under the Cybercrimes Act s2, it is an offence to unlawfully and intentionally access a computer system by using the identity or access credentials of another person.

2.2.3 Regardless of whether Peter authorized Rynette's use of his credentials (see the Impersonation Dilemma in EA-02), the operation constitutes:

| Scenario | Offence |
|----------|---------|
| **Authorized** | Conspiracy to commit identity fraud; Peter complicit |
| **Unauthorized** | Identity fraud by Rynette; unauthorized access |

### 2.3 Impact on Financial Records

2.3.1 The dual-identity operation corrupted the integrity of the Sage accounting records. Transactions attributed to "Peter Faucitt" were in fact executed by Rynette. This means:

2.3.1.1 Financial records cannot be relied upon for attribution;

2.3.1.2 Director authorizations recorded in Sage are unreliable;

2.3.1.3 Audit trails based on user identity are compromised;

2.3.1.4 The Applicant's claims of personal knowledge and oversight are undermined.

## 3. OFFENCE 2: SHOPIFY AUDIT TRAIL DESTRUCTION

### 3.1 The Conduct

3.1.1 On **22 May 2025**, the Shopify audit trails were deleted. This destroyed sales data, customer records, and revenue flow documentation across 37 jurisdictions and 51 stores.

3.1.2 The deletion was not a technical failure. It required deliberate administrative action within the Shopify platform — specifically, accessing the audit log settings and executing a purge across all connected stores.

### 3.2 The Legal Analysis

3.2.1 Under ECTA s86(2), it is an offence to intentionally interfere with data in a way that causes it to be modified, destroyed, or rendered ineffective.

3.2.2 Under the Cybercrimes Act s5, it is an offence to unlawfully and intentionally interfere with data by deleting, altering, or destroying it.

3.2.3 The destruction:

| Element | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **Intentional** | Required deliberate multi-step administrative action |
| **Unlawful** | No director authorization for destruction of company records |
| **Interference with data** | Complete deletion of audit trails across 37 jurisdictions |
| **Resulting in damage** | R1M+/mo revenue to zero; R50M+ regulatory exposure |

## 4. OFFENCE 3: FABRICATION OF DIGITAL RECORDS

### 4.1 The Conduct

4.1.1 On **17–18 June 2025**, Rynette Farrar instructed De Novo Business Services / Marisca Meyer to create 2019 financial statements.

4.1.2 These 2019 financials were created in June 2025 — five to six years after the period they purport to represent. The instruction came during the period when the interdict application was being formulated.

### 4.2 The Legal Analysis

4.2.1 Under ECTA s87(1), it is an offence to perform or threaten to perform any act that constitutes a computer-related extortion, fraud, or forgery.

4.2.2 The creation of backdated financial records constitutes forgery under common law (making a false document with intent to deceive) and potentially fraud (if submitted to SARS, CIPC, or a court).

4.2.3 The timing is significant: authentic records were being destroyed (Shopify on 22 May; Sage locked out on 23 July) while fabricated records were being created (De Novo instruction on 17–18 June). This is evidence of a coordinated strategy to replace authentic evidence with false evidence.

## 5. OFFENCE 4: UNAUTHORIZED SYSTEM ACCESS RESTRICTIONS

### 5.1 The Conduct

5.1.1 On or about **8 July 2025**, system access was restricted for the Second Respondent and the First Respondent. They were denied access to company systems they were entitled to use as directors and members.

### 5.2 The Legal Analysis

5.2.1 Under the Companies Act s26, any person with a beneficial interest is entitled to access company records. Directors have an enhanced right under s26(2).

5.2.2 The restriction of access by non-directors (Rynette, who held no directorship) to directors (Jacqueline and Daniel) inverts the corporate governance hierarchy. An employee cannot lawfully restrict a director's access to company systems.

5.2.3 Under ECTA s86(3), it is an offence to intentionally and unlawfully deny access to a computer system to an authorized person.

## 8. LEGAL SIGNIFICANCE

8.1 The computer crimes are independently material to the void ab initio argument because they demonstrate:

8.1.1 The evidence the Applicant relies upon (Sage records) is corrupted by identity fraud;

8.1.2 The evidence that would have contradicted the Applicant's claims was deliberately destroyed;

8.1.3 The Respondents were deliberately denied access to the systems that would have enabled them to prepare a defence in time.

8.2 A reasonable court, informed of these cyber crimes, would have questioned the reliability of the financial evidence presented in the founding affidavit and would have required independent forensic verification before granting relief.

### 2.1 Company Law Pathway - Director Collective Action Requirement

**Legal Principle:** Directors must act collectively through board resolutions for significant corporate decisions.

**Statutory Basis:** Companies Act 71/2008 s66

**Case Application:** Peter's unilateral card cancellation June 7, 2025

**Optimal Resolution Pathway:**

**JR Response Integration (PARA 7.2-7.5, PARA 8.4):**
> **Director Collective Action Requirement Breach**
>
> Peter's unilateral cancellation of business cards on June 7, 2025, constitutes a **breach of the director collective action requirement** under the Companies Act 71 of 2008, s66.
>
> **Legal Principle:**
> Directors must act collectively through board resolutions for significant corporate decisions. Individual directors cannot take unilateral action affecting company operations without board authority.
>
> **Application to Facts:**
> - Card cancellation was **significant corporate action** affecting all business operations
> - Peter took action **without board resolution or consultation** with co-directors (Jax, Dan)
> - Action had **immediate operational impact** (service disruptions, documentation inaccessibility)
> - No **emergency circumstances** justified bypassing board process
> - Peter provided **no notice or alternative arrangements**
>
> **Legal Consequence:**
> Peter's unilateral action was **ultra vires** (beyond his authority) and constitutes **breach of fiduciary duty**. The action is **voidable** and Peter may be **personally liable** for consequences.

> **Supporting Lex Principle:** `director-collective-action-requirement`, `board-resolution-necessity`, `unilateral-action-prohibition`
